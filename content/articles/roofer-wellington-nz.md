## Wellington Roofing Context

### Wellington's Wind — The Key Factor
Wellington regularly experiences winds above 100km/h — the city holds the record for the highest sustained wind speeds in any NZ city. This puts Wellington roofs under sustained stress that simply doesn't occur in other NZ centres.

**What wind does to roofs:**
- **Lifts and loosens flashings:** Valley flashings, ridge cappings, apron flashings, and soaker flashings all rely on proper fixing and sealing. Wind works at any weakness. Even a pinhole gap in a flashing seal can allow wind-driven rain to track in.
- **Lifts roofing iron:** Screws backing out or fastener failure allows iron sheets to lift, deform, or even detach in severe gales. Galvanised screws corroding in Wellington's salt-laden air (from Cook Strait) contributes.
- **Dislodges tiles:** Ridge capping tiles and terracotta hip tiles are particularly vulnerable — the mortar bedding deteriorates and wind eventually lifts them.
- **Blows debris into valleys:** Leaf and debris accumulation in roof valleys is accelerated by wind. Blocked valleys cause water backup and leaks.

**Wellington homeowners should:** Have roofs inspected more frequently than the national recommendation (every 3–5 years rather than 5–7 years). After any major storm event (wind gusts above 120km/h), a visual inspection of ridges and valleys is worthwhile.

### Wellington's Steep Sections
Wellington is famously hilly — Kelburn, Brooklyn, Karori, Northland, and many other suburbs have steep sections with challenging access. This affects roofing:

- Scaffold is more complex and costly to erect on steep sections
- Some properties have limited road access for large trucks
- Steep pitches on the roofs themselves add difficulty

Factor in access costs when getting Wellington roofing quotes — a site visit is essential before pricing can be accurate.

### Salt Air
Wellington Harbour, Cook Strait, and the outer bays (Seatoun, Eastbourne, Miramar) expose properties to salt-laden air from the sea. Salt accelerates:
- Corrosion of roofing iron fasteners (use stainless steel screws in salt zones)
- Breakdown of roof paint coatings
- Corrosion of gutters and downpipes

Properties within 500m of the coast or harbour should use stainless steel fasteners and higher-spec coatings — a Wellington roofer familiar with the area will know this.

### Roofing Emergencies
Wellington wind events can cause sudden roof damage requiring urgent attention. After a major storm:
1. **Safety first:** Don't go on the roof yourself in wind conditions
2. **Temporary cover:** A tarpaulin over the damaged area prevents further water ingress — many Wellington roofers offer emergency tarp services
3. **Document everything:** Photos for insurance before any temporary repairs
4. **Insurance claim:** Major wind damage is typically covered under residential insurance — contact your insurer before authorising permanent repairs

## Finding a Good Wellington Roofer

**What to look for:**
- Wellington-based company (not travelling from outside the region)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does roof replacement cost in Wellington?**
Iron roof (3-bed): $18,000–$40,000. Concrete tile roof (3-bed): $22,000–$50,000. Wellington's steep sections and access challenges can push costs above these ranges. Get 3 written quotes from Wellington-based roofers who've visited the site.

**How often should I get my Wellington roof inspected?**
More frequently than most NZ cities — every 3–5 years, plus after any severe storm. Wellington's wind works at roofing joints and seals more aggressively than calmer regions. After a storm with gusts above 100km/h, a visual inspection of ridge capping and valley flashings is worthwhile.

**What causes most roof leaks in Wellington?**
Wind-related flashing failure is the most common cause — specifically: ridge capping mortar failure, valley flashing lifting, and apron flashing seal breakdown. Salt air corrosion of iron roof screws is the second most common cause, leading to holes around fasteners. Both are more prevalent in Wellington than other NZ cities.

**Do I need to worry about salt air for my Wellington roof?**
If you're within 500m of the Wellington Harbour, Cook Strait coastline, or outer bays (Seatoun, Miramar, Eastbourne): yes. Specify stainless steel fasteners (not galvanised) for any roofing work, and use a high-quality Colorbond product rather than budget iron. Roofing paint recoat cycles are shorter in salt zones.
