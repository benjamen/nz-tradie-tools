# Managing Construction Insolvency Risks: How to Stay Secure in 2026

The New Zealand construction sector is currently under intense pressure. With annual company liquidations rising significantly—reaching over 760 annually—it's a challenging time to be in the industry. For small to medium-sized tradie businesses, managing insolvency risk has never been more important.

Insolvencies often cascade through the supply chain. If a main contractor or a key client fails, the impact can be devastating for the subcontractors and suppliers they owe. Here is how you can proactively protect your business.

## 1. Prioritize Cash Flow Over Volume
The most common cause of business failure isn't a lack of profit; it's a lack of cash.
- **Invoice Promptly:** Do not let invoices sit on your desk. Send them as soon as milestones are reached.
- **Tighten Credit Terms:** If you are dealing with new clients, be clear about payment terms and don't be afraid to request deposits for larger jobs.
- **Monitor Your Debtors:** Keep a close eye on overdue payments. Don't let one client hold up a significant portion of your cash flow for too long.

## 2. Vet Your Clients and Contractors
Before signing a contract, look at who you are doing business with.
- **Do Your Due Diligence:** Research the main contractors you are subcontracting for. Have they been around for a long time? Are they known for paying on time?
- **Diversify:** Avoid relying on a single client for the majority of your business. If they fail, your entire business is at risk.

## 3. The Power of Perfect Documentation
If a contractor or client enters insolvency, your only hope of getting paid is having perfect, legally sound documentation.
- **Record Everything:** Use [Fastcrew](https://fastcrew.nz) to create a perfect record of every job—including progress photos, signed off-site instructions, and agreed-upon variations.
- **Contractual Clarity:** Ensure all your work is covered by a clear, written contract that outlines payment terms and the process for variations. Never rely on verbal agreements.

## 4. Keep Your Own House in Order
Professionalism builds trust and makes you a preferred partner for main contractors who are also trying to manage their risk.
- **Financial Reporting:** Use our [GST Calculator](/calculators/gst-calculator) to keep your tax reporting accurate and up to date. You don't want to get hit with unexpected tax bills when cash flow is already tight.
- **Pricing:** Use our [job pricing guide](/calculators/job-pricing-guide) to ensure you are accurately pricing your work to account for your overheads and a healthy margin.

## The Outlook
The current "reset" in the construction market is difficult, but it is also forcing the industry to become more disciplined. By focusing on your cash flow, being selective about your clients, and maintaining perfect records, you position your business as a safe, reliable partner. 

When others are failing, your stability is your greatest competitive advantage.
