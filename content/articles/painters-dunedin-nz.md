## Dunedin Painting Context

### Heritage and Character Homes
Dunedin has one of the largest concentrations of Victorian and Edwardian homes in NZ — North Dunedin, Roslyn, Mornington, Corstorphine, and the CBD fringes are full of villas and bungalows from the 1880s–1920s. These homes require:

- **Careful prep:** Lead paint is present in many pre-1980 Dunedin homes. Any sanding or stripping of old paint should be done with appropriate precautions — ask your painter about lead paint testing and management.
- **Multiple surface types:** Rimu weatherboard, ornate fascia boards, sash windows, and decorative bargeboards all require different techniques and patience.
- **Oil vs acrylic:** Some heritage painters still prefer oil-based undercoats on old timber for better penetration and adhesion. Discuss product choice with your painter for heritage work.
- **Colour matching:** Many Dunedin villas have specific heritage colour schemes. Resene's Period Colours range and Benjamin Moore historical palettes are popular for restoration work.

### Dunedin's Climate

Dunedin has a cool, temperate climate with significant seasonal variation:

**Summers (Dec–Feb):** Warm enough for exterior painting but not hot. UV is high in summer — a consideration for dark colours on north-facing surfaces.

**Winters (Jun–Aug):** Cold and often wet. Temperatures regularly drop below 10°C, below which exterior acrylic paint should not be applied. Dunedin painters largely pause exterior work June–August. Frost on painted surfaces before they've cured is a real risk in Dunedin winters.

**Rain and wind:** Dunedin can be wet from any direction — nor'easters bring rain off the harbour, southerlies bring Antarctic air. Exterior painting windows are shorter here than in northern NZ; planning matters.

**Mould:** Dunedin's cooler, damper climate means mould is a persistent issue on south-facing and shaded exterior walls. Biocide pre-treatment is essential before any exterior repaint. Consider a paint with built-in mould resistance (Resene SpaceCote or Porters Excel Wash & Wear).

### Steep Sections
Dunedin is famously hilly — Baldwin Street, the Roslyn ridge, Maori Hill, and much of the peninsula. Steep sections and high-set Victorian homes mean scaffolding costs can be significant. Some properties require specialist access. Factor this into your budget.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does a painter cost in Dunedin?**
Labour rate: $50–$75/hr. Interior 3-bedroom: $3,000–$7,000. Exterior 3-bedroom weatherboard: $4,000–$10,000. Heritage villas with complex prep: $5,500–$14,000+. Dunedin rates are among the most affordable of NZ's main centres.

**When is the best time to paint a house exterior in Dunedin?**
October to April — avoiding Dunedin's cold, wet winters. The best windows are November–March when temperatures are reliably above 10°C and rain is less persistent. Book early: good Dunedin painters fill up for summer exterior work.

**Do Dunedin homes have lead paint?**
Many homes built before 1980 — which includes most of Dunedin's Victorian and bungalow stock — may have lead paint in older layers. Don't sand old Dunedin exterior paint without testing first. A reputable painter will manage this; ask specifically before work starts.

**What paint should I use for a Dunedin exterior?**
Top-tier acrylic with good cold-temperature application properties: Resene Lumbersider, Dulux Weathershield, or Wattyl Solagard. For heritage timber, an oil-based primer under the acrylic top coat improves adhesion. Include a mould inhibitor or mould-resistant product on south-facing surfaces.
