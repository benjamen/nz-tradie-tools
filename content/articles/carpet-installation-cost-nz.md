New carpet transforms a room instantly. Here's what carpet installation costs in NZ in 2026, how carpet type and underlay affect the total price, and how to find carpet layers near you.

## Carpet Installation Cost NZ 2026

| Scope | Typical cost |
|---|---|
| Budget carpet + underlay + install | $50–$80/m² |
| Mid-range carpet + underlay + install | $80–$130/m² |
| Premium carpet + underlay + install | $130–$200/m²+ |
| Underlay only (supply + lay, no carpet) | $15–$30/m² |
| Installation labour only (you supply carpet) | $15–$25/m² |
| Removal of old carpet (per m²) | $5–$12/m² |
| Stair carpet (per step, supply + install) | $30–$80/step |

**Typical total costs for a 3-bedroom house (approx. 70m² of carpet):**
- Budget: $3,500–$5,600
- Mid-range: $5,600–$9,100
- Premium: $9,100–$14,000+

*All prices GST inclusive. Auckland 10–15% above these figures.*

## What's Included in a Carpet Quote

A complete carpet installation includes:

1. **Carpet supply** — the carpet itself, priced per m²
2. **Underlay supply** — the foam or rubber pad beneath the carpet
3. **Gripper rods** — toothed strips nailed around the perimeter that hold the carpet in tension
4. **Installation (stretching and trimming)** — the labour to stretch, cut, and fit the carpet
5. **Removal and disposal of old carpet** — sometimes included, sometimes quoted separately

Always confirm whether your quote includes underlay, removal of old carpet, and GST. Carpet retailers often advertise carpet-only prices that don't include underlay or installation.

## Carpet Types and What They Cost

### Loop Pile (Berber, Loop)
Fibres loop back into the backing without being cut. Hard-wearing, hides footprints, good for high-traffic areas (hallways, stairs, family rooms). Can snag on pet claws.

Cost: $25–$60/m² supply only.

### Cut Pile (Twist, Plush, Saxony)
Fibres are cut at the top, giving a softer feel underfoot. More luxurious look. Shows footprints and vacuum marks more than loop pile. Best for bedrooms and low-traffic areas.

Cost: $30–$80/m² supply only. Premium wool twist: $60–$150/m².

### Cut-Loop (Patterned)
Combines cut and loop fibres for texture and pattern. Good durability. Popular in living areas.

Cost: $35–$75/m² supply only.

### Wool vs Synthetic

**Wool carpet:** Natural fibre, luxurious, warm underfoot, naturally flame-retardant, long-lasting. NZ has some of the best wool carpet in the world (Bremworth, Cavalier Bremworth). Significantly more expensive than synthetic.

**Nylon carpet:** The most durable synthetic fibre. Good stain resistance (especially with solution-dyed or stain-treated nylon). Holds colour well. Most popular residential choice for mid-range.

**Polyester / PET:** Soft, good colour options, but less durable than nylon under heavy traffic. Lower cost. Often made from recycled plastic bottles.

**Polypropylene:** Budget option. Stain resistant (solution-dyed), low cost, but harder and less resilient than nylon. Fine for low-traffic areas or rental properties.

## Underlay: Why It Matters

Underlay affects the feel, warmth, acoustic performance, and longevity of your carpet. A cheap carpet on good underlay often outperforms a better carpet on poor underlay.

**Types:**
- **PU foam (polyurethane):** The standard. Comes in various densities (8mm, 10mm, 12mm). 10–12mm premium PU is the sweet spot for residential.
- **Rubber (waffle):** Denser, more durable, firmer underfoot. Better for high-traffic areas and stairs.
- **Felt:** Traditional, firm. Less common now but still used in heritage or period homes.

**Don't skimp on underlay.** Upgrading from standard to premium underlay adds $5–$10/m² but significantly improves comfort and extends carpet life.

## Removing Old Carpet

Most carpet layers will remove and dispose of old carpet as part of the job. Typical cost: $5–$12/m². DIY removal is possible — pull up the gripper rods and roll up the carpet for council skip or disposal. This can save $300–$800 on a full house re-carpet.

**Note:** Old carpet contains dust and allergens. If removing yourself, wear a dust mask and keep children and pets out of the area.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does carpet installation cost in NZ?**
Mid-range carpet (nylon twist), premium underlay, and installation: $80–$130/m² all in. A typical 3-bedroom house at 70m²: $5,600–$9,100. Budget options start around $50/m²; premium wool carpet can reach $200/m²+.

**How long does carpet installation take?**
A 3-bedroom house: typically 1–2 days including moving furniture and removal of old carpet. Most carpet layers work efficiently — the stretching and trimming is the time-consuming part, not the laying.

**Can I lay carpet myself in NZ?**
Technically yes, but carpet installation requires a power stretcher (rental ~$100/day) and experience to avoid ripples and poor seams. DIY is feasible for a single room; for a whole house it's generally better value to use a professional, especially on stairs.

**What is the best carpet for NZ bedrooms?**
A mid-pile twist nylon or wool carpet in 10–12mm underlay is the classic NZ bedroom choice — soft underfoot, warm, and durable. Wool is the premium option; good-quality nylon performs comparably at lower cost.

**How often does carpet need replacing in NZ?**
With good-quality carpet and underlay, and reasonable care: 10–20 years in a typical residential setting. High-traffic areas (hallways, stairs) wear faster. Rental properties may see 7–12 years due to heavier use. Signs it's time to replace: persistent staining, permanent matting, worn-through pile, or rippling that can't be re-stretched.
