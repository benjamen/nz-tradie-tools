A bathroom renovation is one of the most complex trades jobs in a NZ home — it touches plumbing, tiling, waterproofing, electrical, joinery, and plastering. Here's what it actually costs in NZ in 2026 and how to budget realistically.

## Bathroom Renovation Cost NZ 2026

| Scope | Typical cost |
|---|---|
| Cosmetic refresh (paint, accessories, vanity swap) | $2,000–$6,000 |
| Mid-range renovation (new tiles, fixtures, vanity, shower) | $15,000–$30,000 |
| Premium renovation (full gut, designer fixtures, custom joinery) | $30,000–$60,000+ |
| Ensuite (smaller, existing layout) | $12,000–$25,000 |
| Toilet-only replacement | $800–$2,500 |
| Shower recess only (retile + new screen) | $4,000–$9,000 |

*All prices GST inclusive. Auckland 15–25% above these figures. These include labour — materials are typically 40–50% of total cost.*

## What Drives Bathroom Renovation Cost

### Layout Changes
The cheapest renovation keeps plumbing in the same position. Moving a toilet, shower, or bath to a new location means relocating drain pipes and water lines — adds $2,000–$8,000 and requires a licensed plumber and drainlayer.

**Keep the layout, save the money** — unless the layout is genuinely unusable, working with existing drain positions is far more cost-effective.

### Waterproofing
Non-negotiable under NZ Building Code (E3). The shower and wet areas must be waterproofed to a membrane system that meets NZS 4858. Skimping here leads to water damage behind tiles — one of the most expensive remediation jobs in NZ homes.

Waterproofing is licensed work in the sense that building inspectors check it on consented bathrooms. Cost: $800–$2,500 for a typical bathroom waterproofing job.

Signs of failed waterproofing in older bathrooms: soft flooring outside the shower, mould at the base of walls, musty smell, peeling paint on the exterior wall behind the shower.

### Tiling
Tiling is typically the largest single labour cost in a bathroom renovation.

| Tile type | Cost per m² (supply + lay) |
|---|---|
| Standard ceramic / porcelain | $80–$150/m² |
| Large format (600×600+) | $100–$200/m² |
| Natural stone (marble, slate) | $150–$350/m² |
| Mosaic | $150–$300/m² |

A typical NZ bathroom (walls + floor, ~25m² of tiling): $2,000–$5,000 in tile labour plus $500–$3,000 in tile supply depending on product choice.

### Trades Involved in a Full Renovation

A complete bathroom renovation involves multiple trades, typically coordinated by a builder:

- **Plumber** — remove/install fixtures, relocate pipes if needed
- **Tiler** — floor and wall tiles
- **Waterproofer** or builder with waterproofing qualification
- **Electrician** — extractor fan, heated towel rail, lighting
- **Plasterer** — stopping walls before tiling or painting
- **Builder / project manager** — structural, joinery, coordination
- **Drainlayer** — if drain pipes are moved

Expect 3–4 weeks for a medium bathroom renovation (1–2 weeks demolition and building work, 1 week tiling, 1 week plumbing fixtures and finishing).

## Does a Bathroom Renovation Need a Building Consent?

**Minor work (like-for-like replacements):** Swapping a toilet, vanity, or shower screen in the same position generally doesn't require building consent.

**Full renovation:** If you're moving walls, altering plumbing drain positions, or altering the room structure — yes, building consent is required. Many bathroom builders get consent anyway for complex bathrooms because inspections at the waterproofing stage give you confirmation the work is done correctly.

Required regardless: A licensed plumber must sign off plumbing work, and a licensed drainlayer if drain pipes are moved.

## How to Budget Realistically

**The 40% rule:** Materials (tiles, fixtures, waterproofing, joinery) typically run 40–50% of total cost; labour is 50–60%. Use this to sense-check quotes.

**Budget for surprises:** Old bathrooms often reveal rotten framing, mould in walls, or non-compliant plumbing once opened up. Experienced renovators budget a 15–20% contingency.

**Stages to get quotes for:**
1. Builder (project management, structural, removal)
2. Plumber (rough-in and fit-off)
3. Tiler
4. Waterproofing (some builders include this)
5. Electrician
6. Fixtures (you supply or builder supplies — clarify this upfront)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does a bathroom renovation cost in NZ?**
A mid-range full bathroom renovation (new tiles, vanity, shower, toilet, tiling): $15,000–$30,000. Budget cosmetic refresh: $2,000–$6,000. Premium full renovation: $30,000–$60,000+. Auckland is 15–25% above these figures.

**How long does a bathroom renovation take in NZ?**
A full bathroom renovation: 3–5 weeks on site (depending on drying times for waterproofing and adhesive). Larger or more complex bathrooms may take 6–8 weeks. Lead time to start (booking a builder): often 4–12 weeks, so plan ahead.

**Do I need building consent for a bathroom renovation in NZ?**
For like-for-like replacement of fixtures without moving walls or drain positions: usually not. For moving plumbing, altering the room structure, or significant layout changes: building consent is required. Always confirm with your local council or builder.

**What is the most expensive part of a bathroom renovation?**
Tiling labour and fixtures together are typically the largest cost items. Moving plumbing (drain or supply pipes) adds significant cost if required. Waterproofing is non-negotiable and must be done correctly — it's not where to save money.

**Is it worth renovating a bathroom before selling in NZ?**
A dated bathroom can lose you $15,000–$40,000 on sale price in a competitive NZ market. A mid-range renovation ($15,000–$25,000) often adds $20,000–$40,000 to sale price in most NZ cities. The ROI depends on the property value and local market — talk to a real estate agent before committing.
