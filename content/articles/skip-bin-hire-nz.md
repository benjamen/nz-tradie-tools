Skip bin hire is the most cost-effective way to manage large volumes of waste from a renovation, cleanout, or landscaping project. Here's what skip bins cost to hire in NZ in 2026, which size you need, and what you can and can't put in them.

## Skip Sizes Explained

**2m³ (mini skip):** Fits in a standard residential driveway. Equivalent to about 10 standard 240L wheelie bins. Good for: small cleanouts, garage clear-outs, garden waste, minor renovation waste.

**3–4m³:** Medium capacity — suits small to medium bathroom or kitchen rip-out, one room of flooring.

**5–6m³:** A full kitchen or bathroom renovation, significant landscaping, or partial house cleanout.

**7–9m³:** Full house cleanout, major renovation, large landscaping job, roof replacement waste.

**10–12m³ (hook lift):** Demolition waste, large construction sites, commercial clearances.

**Rule of thumb:** It's almost always cheaper to hire one size up than to order a second skip. If you're unsure, go larger.

## What Can Go in a Skip in NZ?

### Generally Accepted (General Waste)
- Household furniture and appliances
- Timber (untreated or treated — check with provider)
- Plasterboard / GIB board (some providers accept, some don't — ask)
- Garden waste (soil, branches, lawn clippings)
- Concrete, bricks, tiles, pavers (heavy material surcharge usually applies)
- Roofing iron and metal
- Carpet and underlay
- General building waste

### Not Accepted in Skip Bins
- **Asbestos:** Must be removed by licensed asbestos removalist and disposed of at a licensed facility
- **Hazardous chemicals:** Paints, solvents, pesticides, oils
- **Tyres:** Special tyre recycling required
- **Batteries and electronics:** E-waste — council recycling facilities or Greentec/Planet Ark
- **Gas cylinders:** Return to supplier
- **Medical waste**

**Always declare what you're putting in** — if a skip company discovers hazardous material after collection, they can charge significant surcharges or return the bin to you.

## Heavy Material Surcharges

Concrete, bricks, tiles, and soil are dense and heavy — they significantly increase the bin weight beyond what's included in standard hire.

**How it works:** Standard skip hire includes a weight allowance (often 1–3 tonnes). Heavy materials exceed this quickly:
- 1m³ of concrete weighs approximately 2.4 tonnes
- 1m³ of soil weighs approximately 1.5 tonnes
- 1m³ of timber weighs approximately 0.5–0.8 tonnes

**Surcharge:** Typically $60–$120 per tonne over the included weight.

**Tip:** For heavy materials only (concrete rubble, soil), hire a **heavy skip** or **concrete skip** — specifically designed for dense material, rated to carry the weight, and priced accordingly. Using a standard waste skip for a concrete driveway demolition will incur large weight surcharges.

## Hire Period and Extensions

Standard hire period: **3–7 days** depending on the company. Extensions are usually available at $15–$40/day.

**Plan your job** around the skip — the most cost-effective approach is to have everything ready before the skip arrives, fill it in 1–2 days, and have it collected immediately.

**Council permits for road placement:** If the skip must go on a public road (not on private property), most NZ councils require a temporary traffic management permit. Skip bin companies often arrange this ($50–$150 extra). Always ask if your placement requires a permit.

## Mixed vs Separated Waste

**Mixed general waste** goes to landfill. **Separated or clean loads** often qualify for lower disposal fees because they can be recycled:
- Clean concrete and masonry: may be accepted at reduced rates at concrete recycling facilities
- Clean soil: may be accepted at fill sites for free or low cost
- Green waste (clean garden waste): council green waste facilities or composting sites
- Metal: often accepted for free or for a buy-back at metal recyclers

If you're doing a major renovation, separating concrete, metal, and timber into different loads (or separate skips) can reduce total disposal cost.

## Alternatives to Skip Bins

**Council inorganic collection:** Most NZ councils offer a free annual inorganic collection — great for furniture and large items. Schedule and limits vary.

**Rubbish removal service:** A truck and team come to you, load the waste, and take it away — you don't need to load anything. More expensive than a skip ($200–$600 for a small load) but convenient. Good for seniors or where loading is impractical.

**TradeMe / Facebook Marketplace:** Useful timber, furniture, and materials can often be given away free — saves landfill cost and someone else benefits. Post in advance of your cleanout.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does it cost to hire a skip bin in NZ?**
A 2m³ mini skip: $180–$320. A 4–5m³ medium skip: $300–$500. A 7–9m³ large skip: $450–$750. Prices include delivery, standard hire period (3–7 days), and disposal. Heavy materials like concrete attract weight surcharges on top.

**What size skip bin do I need for a bathroom renovation?**
A typical bathroom renovation (removing a toilet, vanity, bath, tiles, flooring): 3–4m³ is usually sufficient. If you're also doing structural work or removing plasterboard walls, allow 5–6m³.

**Can I put concrete in a skip bin in NZ?**
Yes, but most skip companies charge a heavy material surcharge (typically $60–$120 per tonne over the included allowance). For large amounts of concrete (driveway demolition), a dedicated heavy skip or concrete-only bin is more cost effective. Ask specifically about the weight allowance and surcharge when quoting.

**Do I need a permit to put a skip on the road in NZ?**
Yes — placing a skip on a public road requires a temporary traffic management permit from your council. Your skip company can usually arrange this for $50–$150. If the skip goes on your private driveway or property, no permit is needed.

**Can I put asbestos in a skip bin in NZ?**
No. Asbestos is a notifiable hazardous substance and cannot go into standard waste skips. It must be removed by a licensed asbestos removalist and taken to a licensed asbestos disposal facility. See our [asbestos removal cost guide](/articles/asbestos-removal-cost-nz/) for more information.
