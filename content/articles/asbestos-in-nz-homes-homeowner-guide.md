## Introduction
Asbestos in NZ homes is a common issue, with approximately one in three homes built before 1990 containing asbestos-containing materials (ACM). The presence of asbestos can be a significant concern for homeowners, as it poses serious health risks if disturbed or damaged. However, it's essential to note that asbestos in good condition that is not disturbed is generally safe. This comprehensive guide will cover everything you need to know about asbestos in NZ homes, from where it's commonly found to how to identify and safely remove it.

As a homeowner, it's crucial to be aware of the potential risks associated with asbestos and take necessary precautions to ensure your safety and the safety of your family. The Health and Safety at Work (Asbestos) Regulations 2016 provide a framework for managing asbestos in NZ, and it's essential to understand your responsibilities as a homeowner. In this guide, we'll provide you with the information you need to navigate the complex issue of asbestos in NZ homes.

## Where Asbestos is Found in NZ Homes
Asbestos was widely used in NZ homes built before 1990, and it can be found in various forms and locations. Some common areas where asbestos is found include:

* Exterior cladding: Fibrous cement sheet (Hardiflex, Monlex, Super Six), corrugated roof sheets
* Interior linings: Textured ceilings ("popcorn" ceilings), stipple texture paint
* Flooring: Vinyl floor tiles and the adhesive underneath (common in homes 1950s-1980s)
* Insulation: Pipe lagging, some ceiling batts in older homes
* Roof: Super six corrugated asbestos cement roofing sheets
* Bathrooms: Some wall tiles and tile adhesive
* Window surrounds, gutters, soffits (older fibrous cement products)

It's essential to note that asbestos can be found in many other areas, and a thorough inspection is necessary to identify all potential sources. As a homeowner, it's crucial to be aware of the potential risks associated with asbestos and take necessary precautions to ensure your safety.

## How to Identify Asbestos
Visual inspection is not a reliable method for identifying asbestos, as it can be difficult to distinguish from other materials. The only definitive way to confirm the presence of asbestos is through laboratory testing. To arrange an asbestos test in NZ, you can take a small sample yourself (wearing gloves and a mask) and send it to an accredited NZ laboratory for analysis. The cost of asbestos testing can range from $50 to $150 per sample, depending on the laboratory and the type of test required.

Alternatively, you can hire an asbestos inspector to conduct a thorough inspection of your home and provide a detailed report. The cost of an asbestos inspection can range from $200 to $600, depending on the size of your home and the complexity of the inspection. ## What You Can Legally Do Yourself
Under NZ law, homeowners can remove up to 10m² of non-friable (bonded) asbestos from their own home without a licensed removalist. This includes small areas of fibrous cement sheet if undamaged. However, it's essential to note that you cannot remove:

* Friable asbestos (loose or powdery — insulation, sprayed coatings)
* Any asbestos from the roof if the area is large
* More than 10m² of any ACM

If you're unsure about the type or quantity of asbestos, it's always best to consult a licensed removalist. As a homeowner, it's crucial to understand your responsibilities and take necessary precautions to ensure your safety.

## When to Use a Licensed Removalist
Friable asbestos always requires the services of a licensed removalist. Additionally, if you need to remove more than 10m² of any ACM or work with asbestos from the roof, you'll need to hire a licensed removalist. WorkSafe NZ has notification requirements for larger jobs, and it's essential to ensure that your removalist is compliant with these regulations.

To find a Class A (friable) or Class B (non-friable) licensed removalist, you can search online or contact WorkSafe NZ for a list of approved removalists. Some popular tools for managing your trades business, such as Xero, Fergus, or Tradify, can also help you find and manage licensed removalists.

## Costs of Asbestos Removal in NZ
The cost of asbestos removal in NZ can vary widely, depending on the type and quantity of asbestos, as well as the location and complexity of the job. Some estimated costs include:

* Small removal of fibrous cement (under 10m²): DIY if careful, or $500-$2,000
* Fibrous cement cladding replacement (whole house): $15,000-$50,000
* Roof sheet removal and replacement: $5,000-$20,000
* Friable asbestos (pipe lagging, ceiling insulation): $3,000-$20,000+

It's essential to note that these costs are estimates, and the actual cost of asbestos removal may vary depending on your specific circumstances.

## Safe DIY Removal (if Legal)
If you're allowed to remove asbestos yourself, it's crucial to follow safe procedures to minimize the risk of exposure. This includes:

* Using the wet method to reduce dust
* Wearing a full face P2 respirator (not just a dust mask)
* Wearing disposable coveralls
* Double-bagging in 0.2mm polythene bags labelled "ASBESTOS"
* Disposing of asbestos at a council-approved facility (most NZ councils have an asbestos disposal facility)

## Renovation Rule: Always Test Before Demo
The most important rule when renovating a pre-1990 home is to always test for asbestos before starting demolition. This will help you identify potential sources of asbestos and take necessary precautions to ensure your safety.

## FAQ
Here are some frequently asked questions about asbestos in NZ homes:

* Q: Is fibrous cement cladding dangerous?
A: Fibrous cement cladding is generally safe if it's in good condition and not disturbed. However, if it's damaged or deteriorated, it can release asbestos fibres, which can be hazardous to your health.
* Q: Do I have to disclose asbestos when selling my NZ home?
A: Yes, as a seller, you're required to disclose any known asbestos in your home to potential buyers. This is a legal requirement under the Health and Safety at Work (Asbestos) Regulations 2016.
* Q: Can I paint over asbestos fibrous cement?
A: Yes, you can paint over asbestos fibrous cement, but it's essential to use a specialized paint and follow safe procedures to minimize the risk of exposure.
* Q: How much does asbestos testing cost?
A: The cost of asbestos testing can range from $50 to $150 per sample, depending on the laboratory and the type of test required.
* Q: What is a friable vs non-friable asbestos?
A: Friable asbestos is loose or powdery, while non-friable asbestos is bonded or solid. Friable asbestos is more hazardous than non-friable asbestos, as it can release fibres more easily.
