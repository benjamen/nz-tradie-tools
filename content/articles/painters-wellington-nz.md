## Wellington's Unique Painting Challenges

### Wind
Wellington is the windiest city in NZ — frequently classified as one of the windiest capitals in the world. This has significant implications for exterior painting:

**Lost painting days:** Painters cannot spray-apply paint in high winds (overspray and uneven application). Brush and roller application is safer but slower. Wellington painters typically lose 20–30% of planned exterior working days to wind compared with 5–10% in Auckland or Christchurch.

**This affects pricing:** Exterior paint jobs in Wellington often take longer than quoted due to weather delays. Good Wellington painters build a weather contingency into their schedule and pricing — be wary of unrealistically short timeframes.

**Scaffold stability:** Scaffolding on exposed Wellington properties (particularly hill suburbs — Karori, Kelburn, Brooklyn, Northland, Hataitai) must be engineered for Wellington wind loads. Scaffold hire is often higher than other cities.

### Salt Air
Wellington Harbour exposure, southern exposure, and frequent strong onshore winds mean many Wellington homes — particularly in Seatoun, Miramar, Eastbourne, and the Bays — are in high-salt-air environments. Salt air accelerates paint breakdown:

- **Surfaces corrode faster:** Metal flashings, gutters, and fixings rust more quickly
- **Paint chalks and fades sooner:** UV + salt = accelerated breakdown
- **Biocide is essential:** Mould and algae establish faster on surfaces that don't dry quickly

**Solution:** Use a top-tier exterior acrylic specifically formulated for salt-air environments (Resene X-200, Dulux Weathershield Maximum, or similar). Two coats minimum. Re-coat cycle of 8–10 years rather than 12–15 years.

### Wellington Hill Sections
Many Wellington homes sit on steep hill sections — Kelburn, Northland, Karori, Wadestown, Mt Victoria, Aro Valley. Access for painters is challenging:

- Scaffold on a steep site requires engineering
- Rope access is common on extreme sites
- Multi-level homes on a slope may have 3+ effective stories on the downhill face

**Expect:** 20–40% premium on exterior painting for steep section properties compared with a flat-section home of the same size.

## Wellington Interior Painting

Wellington's cooler, wetter winters mean interior painting is popular year-round (unlike exterior, which has weather windows). Interior rates are broadly in line with the national average.

**Wellington-specific consideration:** Many of Wellington's older character homes (villas, bungalows, and art deco in Mt Victoria, Thorndon, Hataitai) have intricate timber detailing — sashes, cornices, casement windows. These take significantly longer to cut in around than modern open-plan interiors.

**Plastered interior walls:** Older Wellington homes often have hard plaster walls rather than GIB board. Hard plaster is harder to fill and patch (requires different products) and has more surface texture variation.

## Finding a Reliable Wellington Painter

**What to look for:**
- Experience with Wellington conditions specifically — ask how they handle weather delays
- Knowledge of the specific products suited to salt-air or high-wind environments
- References in your suburb (steep site experience matters)
- Master Painters NZ membership
- Written quote with clear scope of work

**Suburbs with high demand / limited painters:** Eastbourne (ferry access), Karori, Wadestown, and Khandallah can have longer wait times due to fewer painters willing to work in those areas regularly.

**Questions to ask:**
1. How do you handle weather delays — what's your scheduling approach?
2. What paint product are you specifying for the exterior, and why?
3. Have you worked on similar sites in Wellington (hill section / salt air)?
4. What's your lead time to start?
5. Is scaffold included in your quote or separate?

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does a painter cost in Wellington?**
Labour rate: $60–$90/hr. Interior 3-bedroom: $4,000–$9,000. Exterior 3-bedroom weatherboard: $6,500–$15,000 — comparable to Auckland due to wind and access challenges. Steep hillside properties add 20–40%.

**Why is exterior painting expensive in Wellington?**
Wind delays reduce painting days, steep sections increase scaffold cost and access time, salt air requires premium products and more frequent recoating, and scaffold must be engineered for Wellington wind loads. All of these add cost compared with painting in less exposed cities.

**How long does an exterior repaint take in Wellington?**
A 3-bedroom weatherboard exterior: 2–4 weeks of elapsed time (including weather delays). In a Wellington winter this can extend to 4–6 weeks. Summers (December–March) have the best painting windows and painters are typically booked out months in advance.

**What paint should I use for a Wellington exterior?**
Top-tier acrylic exterior paint rated for coastal/high-wind conditions: Resene X-200, Resene Lumbersider (for timber), or Dulux Weathershield Maximum. Two coats minimum. For salt-air suburbs (Seatoun, Eastbourne, Miramar Peninsula), specify a product with a salt-air warranty or recommendation from the manufacturer.

**How do Wellington painters handle wind delays?**
Professional Wellington painters build weather contingency into their projects and will communicate delays clearly. A good painter won't leave you with half-finished exterior walls — they'll prime exposed timber before any break and have a plan to return. Avoid painters with extremely tight timelines who haven't factored Wellington's weather reality.
