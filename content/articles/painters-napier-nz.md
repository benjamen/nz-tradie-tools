## Napier's Art Deco Heritage

Napier is world-famous for its Art Deco architecture — rebuilt after the 1931 earthquake in the Art Deco style that was fashionable at the time. The city has one of the largest concentrations of Art Deco buildings in the world, and many Napier homes and commercial buildings reflect this heritage.

### Painting Art Deco Homes

Art Deco buildings in Napier are typically stucco (cement plaster) render over timber or masonry. Painting these requires specific knowledge:

**Surface preparation:** Stucco must be clean, sound, and free of friable material. Any cracks must be filled with a flexible filler (not rigid cement) and allowed to cure. Stucco that's been previously painted with oil-based paint needs compatible primer.

**Colour authenticity:** Napier's Art Deco buildings traditionally feature cream, white, and soft pastel shades — often with contrasting trim in terracotta, sage green, or gold. The Art Deco Trust maintains colour guidelines for heritage precinct buildings. A good Napier painter will know which colours are appropriate.

**Acrylic over stucco:** Modern acrylic masonry paints (Resene Lumbersider, Dulux Weathershield) adhere well to stucco and are breathable — important for cement render that needs to manage moisture. Avoid solvent-based coatings that can trap moisture and cause blistering.

**Repaint cycle:** Hawke's Bay's sunny, dry climate is actually excellent for exterior paint life — expect 12–18 years between repaints on a well-prepared stucco surface, compared to 8–12 years in wetter regions.

## Hawke's Bay's Climate Advantage

Napier and Hastings receive around 2,200 hours of sunshine per year — one of the sunniest regions in NZ. For exterior painting, this is largely positive:

- **Long painting season:** The dry Hawke's Bay climate allows exterior painting for most of the year, with only mid-winter (June–July) being less than ideal
- **Low humidity:** Lower humidity than Auckland or Wellington means paint dries and cures more reliably
- **UV consideration:** High UV in Hawke's Bay summers means dark colours on north-facing surfaces will fade faster — lighter colours or UV-resistant topcoats recommended for sun-exposed walls

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does a painter cost in Napier?**
Labour rate: $50–$78/hr. Interior 3-bedroom: $3,000–$7,000. Exterior 3-bedroom weatherboard: $4,000–$9,500. Art Deco stucco exterior: $4,500–$11,000. Hawke's Bay rates are among the most affordable of NZ's main centres.

**What paint is best for Napier's Art Deco stucco homes?**
Breathable acrylic masonry paint: Resene Lumbersider, Dulux Weathershield Masonry, or Haymes Protect All. These adhere well to cement render, allow moisture to escape, and resist Hawke's Bay's high UV. Apply over a quality masonry primer on bare or previously unpainted surfaces.

**How often should I repaint the exterior of a Napier home?**
Hawke's Bay's dry, sunny climate is kind to exterior paint. A well-prepared exterior on a stucco Art Deco home: every 12–18 years. Weatherboard: 10–15 years. North-facing surfaces in full Hawke's Bay sun may show UV fade earlier — lighter colours extend the recoat cycle.

**Are Napier painters affected by earthquake risk?**
Hawke's Bay sits in a moderate seismic zone — but painting itself is unaffected by seismic risk. For homes being repaired after earthquake damage (cracked plaster, movement cracks), ensure cracks are properly filled with flexible sealant before repainting.
