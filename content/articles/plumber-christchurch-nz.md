## Christchurch Plumbing Context

### Post-Earthquake Plumbing Issues
The 2010–2011 Canterbury earthquakes caused widespread damage to Christchurch's underground infrastructure and household plumbing:

**Drain and sewer damage:** The earthquake liquefaction caused significant lateral spreading and settlement, breaking clay and PVC drain lines throughout the city. Christchurch City Council (CCC) repaired the main sewer network, but private drain connections (from house to the street boundary) were the homeowner's responsibility. Many properties still have earthquake-damaged or poorly repaired private drain connections.

**If you're buying a Christchurch property:** A CCTV drain inspection of the private drain connection is strongly recommended. Root intrusion, joint displacement, and cracked pipes from earthquake movement are common findings — especially in TC2 and TC3 land zones.

**Pipe movement:** Earthquake settlement moved some internal plumbing — particularly in TC2/TC3 areas. Signs: persistent slow drains, gurgling pipes, minor leaks at pipe joints, or drains that worked fine pre-earthquake but now have issues.

### Canterbury's Water Supply
Christchurch's water supply comes from an extensive underground aquifer system — some of the highest-quality tap water in NZ, with no chlorine treatment required (unlike most NZ cities). This means:

- **No chlorine taste or odour:** Christchurch water is notably clean-tasting
- **Hard water in some areas:** Some Canterbury water sources are moderately hard — scale buildup in hot water cylinders and on tapware is more common than in soft-water regions like Auckland
- **Hot water cylinder maintenance:** Scale reduces hot water cylinder efficiency and life. A plumber can fit a sacrificial anode or recommend a descale treatment

### Christchurch's TC Land Zones
Properties in TC2 and TC3 ground zones had more significant earthquake movement. For plumbing work on TC2/TC3 properties:
- Foundation-related pipe movement may need to be assessed
- Any renovation involving subfloor drain access should include an inspection of existing drain condition
- Building consent for new bathrooms or plumbing in TC2/TC3 may have additional engineering requirements

## PGDB Verification — Non-Negotiable

All NZ plumbing work must be done by a PGDB-registered plumber. Verify at [pgdb.co.nz](https://www.pgdb.co.nz). All work requires a Certificate of Compliance (COC).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does a plumber cost in Christchurch?**
Hourly rate: $85–$145/hr. Call-out fee: $90–$190. Hot water cylinder replacement: $1,000–$2,200. Christchurch rates are 10–20% below Auckland — good value for a main-centre city.

**Should I get a drain inspection on a Christchurch property?**
Yes, strongly recommended for any pre-2012 property. Earthquake-related drain damage is common, particularly in TC2 and TC3 zones. A CCTV inspection ($250–$600) can identify cracked pipes, joint displacement, and root intrusion before they become expensive emergencies.

**Is Christchurch tap water safe?**
Yes — Christchurch's artesian aquifer water is among the best in NZ. No chlorine is added. It is moderately hard in some areas, which can cause scale in hot water cylinders and on tapware over time.

**How long does a plumber take to replace a hot water cylinder in Christchurch?**
Typically 2–4 hours for a like-for-like replacement in an accessible location. A heat pump hot water conversion takes 3–6 hours. Same-day service is often available from established Christchurch plumbers for cylinder replacements.
