## Wellington Tiling Considerations

### Wellington's Older Housing Stock
Wellington is rich in early 20th-century homes — villas, bungalows, and workers' cottages in Newtown, Mt Cook, Thorndon, Aro Valley, and Brooklyn. Tiling in these homes requires specific considerations:

**Subfloor movement:** Older Wellington homes often have timber framed floors that flex more than concrete subfloors. Tile adhesive and grout on a flexible substrate will crack and de-bond over time unless the substrate is properly stiffened. Options: overlay with a rigid tile backer board (Hardiebacker, Durock) before tiling, or use a flexible adhesive system. A good Wellington tiler will assess the floor before quoting.

**Unlevel floors:** Heritage floors are rarely level. Significant tiles require more adhesive bed and levelling compound. Large format tiles (600×600+) are particularly unforgiving on unlevel floors — lippage (tiles not aligning flush) is immediately visible.

**Waterproofing before tiling:** NZ Building Code (E3) requires waterproof membranes in wet areas. In older Wellington homes being renovated, existing waterproofing (if any) must be assessed or replaced before retiling. Ensure your tiler includes proper waterproofing — not just tiling over old tiles.

### Wellington's Wind and Humidity
Wellington's famous wind creates a drier environment than Auckland — but internal humidity in bathrooms is the relevant factor for tiling, and it's similar nationwide. More relevant: Wellington's cold winters mean heating and temperature swings, which stress tile adhesive bonds. Use a flexible adhesive (C2 class) for any Wellington tile installation.

### Seismic Considerations
Wellington's seismic activity is a real-world factor for tiled surfaces. Tiles are rigid and grout lines are brittle — seismic movement can crack tile installations. Some Wellington homeowners are moving toward larger, more flexible surface options (engineered stone, vinyl planking) in areas where tile cracking is a concern. If you're tiling, use flexible adhesive and grout with polymer additives.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does tiling cost in Wellington?**
Labour only floor tiling: $60–$120/m². Supply and lay (mid-range tile): $120–$250/m². Full bathroom floor and walls (4m² room, mid-range tiles): $2,500–$6,000. Wellington rates are similar to Auckland — expect to pay more than provincial NZ rates.

**Can you tile over old tiles in Wellington?**
Technically yes, if the existing tiles are soundly bonded, the substrate can handle the extra weight and height, and you're not in a wet area. In bathrooms: retiling over old tiles without renewing the waterproofing membrane is not compliant with NZ Building Code E3. Most Wellington tilers recommend full removal and reinstating a proper waterproofing system.

**What tiles are suitable for Wellington's earthquake risk?**
Use flexible tile adhesive (C2 class or better, e.g. Mapei Granifix Flex, Ardex X77) and flexible grout (polymer-modified). Smaller tiles with more grout lines are marginally more earthquake-tolerant than large format tiles. Large format tiles on timber floors in Wellington are higher risk — the substrate must be rigid and a fully flexible adhesive system used.

**How do I find a good tiler in Wellington?**
Ask your builder or bathroom renovation company for recommendations — they use tilers regularly. Check Google reviews. Ask to see previous work in Wellington homes. Confirm the tiler understands waterproofing requirements and includes a proper membrane system in wet areas.
