## Introduction to Roof Replacement in NZ 2026
As a homeowner in New Zealand, it's essential to understand when your roof needs replacing, rather than just repairing. ## Signs You Need a New Roof
A roof's lifespan can vary depending on the material and quality of installation. Generally, a corrugated iron roof can last between 30 to 50 years, while a concrete tile roof can last between 40 to 60 years. However, there are several signs that indicate your roof may need replacing:
* Visible rust or corrosion on metal roofs
* Lifted or missing tiles, which can lead to water damage and leaks
* Interior leaks or water stains on ceilings and walls
* Sagging or uneven rooflines, which can be a sign of structural issues
* Gutters filled with granules from asphalt shingles or concrete tiles, indicating wear and tear
* Failed flashings, which can be a sign of a larger issue with the roof itself

It's essential to distinguish between failed flashings and a failed roof, as the latter can be a more significant and costly issue. Failed flashings can often be repaired or replaced, whereas a failed roof may require a full replacement.

## Roofing Materials Comparison
When it comes to choosing a new roof, there are several materials to consider. Here's a comparison of some of the most popular options:
* Long-run steel (Colorsteel Endura, Maxx): $90–$140/m² installed, with a 50-year+ warranted life
* Corrugate/tray profile (traditional NZ iron look): $80–$130/m² installed
* Concrete tile: $120–$180/m² installed, with a heavy weight that can have structural implications
* Clay tile: $180–$280/m², with a heritage look and the longest lifespan

## Full Cost Breakdown for a Typical NZ House
The cost of a new roof can vary depending on the material, size of the house, and pitch of the roof. For a typical 150m² house, the cost can range from $15,000 to $35,000, depending on the material and pitch. Here's a breakdown of the costs:
* Removal and disposal: $2,000–$6,000
* Sarking (building wrap/underlay): $1,500–$3,500
* Flashings (ridgeline, valleys, penetrations): $2,000–$5,000
* Gutters and downpipes: $3,000–$8,000 (if replacing simultaneously)
* Regional variation: Auckland 20% premium, South Island 10–15% cheaper

It's essential to note that these costs are estimates, and the final price may vary depending on your location and the complexity of the job. It's recommended to get at least three quotes from licensed roofing contractors to compare prices and services.

## Does a New Roof Need Building Consent?
In most cases, like-for-like re-roofing is exempt from building consent under Schedule 1 of the Building Act. However, if you're changing the material type or making significant structural changes, you may need to obtain a building consent. It's recommended to call your local council's duty planner to confirm whether a consent is required.

## What to Look for in a Roofing Contractor

* A Licensed Building Practitioner (LBP) for restricted building work
