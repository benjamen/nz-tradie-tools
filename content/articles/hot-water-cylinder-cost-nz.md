## Electric vs Heat Pump Hot Water: Which Should You Choose?

**Electric cylinder (resistive):**
The standard NZ hot water system for decades. Heats water using an electric element — simple, reliable, and inexpensive to buy and install. Running costs are higher than heat pump.

**Heat pump hot water (HPWH):**
Extracts heat from the surrounding air (like a reverse refrigerator) to heat water. Uses 60–75% less electricity than a resistive cylinder for the same hot water output. Higher upfront cost, pays back in lower running costs.

| Factor | Electric cylinder | Heat pump |
|---|---|---|
| Purchase + install | $1,500–$3,200 | $3,500–$6,500 |
| Annual running cost (4-person household) | $700–$1,200 | $200–$400 |
| Payback vs electric (HPWH) | — | 4–7 years |
| Works in cold spaces? | Yes | Reduced efficiency below 5°C |
| Noise | Silent | Fan noise (like a dehumidifier) |
| Space required | Standard | Slightly larger + clearance |

**For most NZ households staying in their home long-term:** A heat pump hot water system is the better financial choice. Running cost savings of $400–$800/year mean payback in 4–7 years, with 15–20 years of operation remaining.

**For rental properties or short-term ownership:** Electric cylinder is simpler, cheaper to install, and easier to maintain.

| 3–4 people | 180–250L |
| 5–6 people | 250–300L |

**Continuous flow gas** (instantaneous) heats water on demand — no tank, no standby heat loss, and unlimited hot water. Requires a gas connection. Good for large families. Not available everywhere in NZ (natural gas network is concentrated in main cities).

## When to Replace vs Repair Your Hot Water Cylinder

**Replace when:**
- The cylinder is over 15 years old (most electric cylinders last 15–25 years)
- You have active leaking from the cylinder body or around the sacrificial anode
- The element has failed twice (electrical elements cost $200–$500 to replace — a second failure on an old cylinder often signals end of life)
- The thermostat is failing and water is too hot or too cold
- You're switching from electric to heat pump (most are new installs, not retrofits)

**Repair when:**
- Element has failed on a cylinder under 10 years old ($200–$500 for an element replacement)
- Thermostat fault on a relatively new cylinder
- Pressure relief valve replacement ($150–$350)

**Emergency:** If your cylinder is actively leaking water, turn off the cold water inlet to the cylinder immediately (the valve on the pipe entering the cylinder, usually in a cupboard). This stops water flooding. You can usually still use cold taps — just no hot water until the cylinder is replaced.

## Hot Water Is Licensed Plumbing Work in NZ

Hot water cylinder installation must be done by a **licensed plumber** under the Plumbers, Gasfitters, and Drainlayers Act 2006. This is not DIY work.

The plumber will:
- Disconnect and remove the old cylinder
- Install the new cylinder and connect supply/hot lines
- Connect the pressure relief valve and temperature/pressure valve (TPV) discharge line (must discharge to a safe location outdoors)
- Commission the system and check for leaks
- Issue a certificate of compliance (for mains pressure systems)

## Emergency Hot Water Replacement

If your cylinder fails on a weekend or overnight:

- **Emergency plumber call-out:** $150–$350 call-out fee plus time on site
- **Can they install same day?** Sometimes — if they carry stock (common sizes: 180L and 250L electric cylinders). Otherwise next business day.
- **Temporary workaround:** A cold shower or kettle while waiting for parts is usually the only option

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does a hot water cylinder replacement cost in NZ?**
A standard 250L electric cylinder, supply and installation: $1,800–$3,200. A 250L heat pump hot water system: $4,000–$6,500. Emergency call-outs add $150–$350 on top of the installation cost.

**How long do hot water cylinders last in NZ?**
Electric cylinders: 15–25 years. Heat pump hot water: 15–20 years. Gas continuous flow: 15–20 years. Sacrificial anodes inside cylinders are designed to corrode instead of the tank — they should be replaced every 5–10 years. Neglecting this shortens cylinder life.

**Is a heat pump hot water system worth it in NZ?**
Yes, for most NZ homeowners planning to stay in the home for 5+ years. Annual running cost savings of $400–$800 vs electric mean payback in 4–7 years. EECA's Warmer Kiwi Homes programme has previously offered subsidies for heat pump hot water — check eeca.govt.nz for current availability.

**Can I replace a hot water cylinder myself in NZ?**
No. Hot water cylinder installation is licensed plumbing work in NZ. An unlicensed installation risks damage to the hot water system, compliance issues, and insurance complications. Licensed plumbers are not much more expensive than the parts alone — use one.

**What is a sacrificial anode in a hot water cylinder?**
A metal rod (usually magnesium) inside the cylinder that corrodes preferentially, protecting the steel tank from rust. They last 5–10 years and cost $150–$350 to replace (labour + part). Most NZ homeowners never replace them — but it's the single cheapest way to extend your cylinder's life by 5–10 years.
