## Introduction to Renovation ROI in NZ
When it comes to renovating a home in New Zealand, not all projects add dollar-for-dollar value. Some renovations can increase the value of a property significantly, while others may add less value than they cost. As a homeowner in NZ, it's essential to have realistic expectations about the return on investment (ROI) for different types of renovations. This guide provides an overview of the current market conditions and helps homeowners understand which renovations are worth doing in 2026.

In the NZ property market, the cost of renovations can vary significantly depending on the type of project, materials, and labour costs. With the current market rates and regulations in mind, homeowners can make informed decisions about which renovations to prioritize. The Building and Construction Industry Training Organisation (BCITO) and the Electrical Workers Registration Board (EWRB) are just a few of the organisations that provide guidance on the construction industry in NZ.

According to the Master Plumbers, the average cost of a kitchen renovation in NZ can range from $30,000 to $70,000. However, the added value to the property can be significantly higher, ranging from $25,000 to $60,000. Similarly, the Registered Master Builders association reports that a bathroom renovation can cost between $20,000 to $45,000, with an added value of $20,000 to $40,000.

## Thinking About Renovation ROI
When considering renovation ROI, it's crucial to think about the different types of value that can be added to a property. While financial value is often the primary concern, lifestyle value and resale value are also important factors to consider. Buyer expectations can vary significantly depending on the suburb, price point, and type of property.

For example, a $40,000 kitchen renovation in a $500,000 house may add less proportional value than the same kitchen in a $1.5 million house. This is because the kitchen renovation is a smaller percentage of the overall property value in the more expensive house. On the other hand, overcapitalising on renovations can be a significant risk, especially if the added value is not reflected in the sale price.

To avoid overcapitalising, homeowners should consider the potential ROI of different renovations and prioritize those that are likely to add the most value. The Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment (MBIE) and the Inland Revenue Department (IRD) provide guidance on the tax implications of renovations, which can also impact the overall ROI.

## High ROI Renovations
Some renovations are more likely to add significant value to a property than others. These high ROI renovations include:

* **Kitchen refresh**: A kitchen refresh can be a cost-effective way to add value to a property. This can include painting the cabinets, installing a new benchtop, and updating the tapware and splashback. The cost of a kitchen refresh can range from $8,000 to $18,000, with a typical added value of $15,000 to $35,000.
* **Bathroom refresh**: A bathroom refresh can also be a high ROI renovation. This can include updating the tapware, resurfacing or retiling the bathroom, and installing a new vanity. The cost of a bathroom refresh can range from $8,000 to $15,000, with a typical added value of $12,000 to $25,000.
* **Kerb appeal**: Improving the exterior of a property can have a significant impact on its value. This can include painting the exterior, landscaping, and installing a new front door. The cost of improving kerb appeal can range from $5,000 to $12,000, with a disproportionate impact on buyer first impression.
* **Heat pump installation**: Installing a heat pump can be a high ROI renovation, especially in NZ's climate. The cost of a heat pump installation can range from $3,500 to $7,000, with a typical added value of $5,000 to $12,000.
* **Insulation**: Insulating a property can be a high ROI renovation, especially with the current focus on energy efficiency. The cost of insulation can range from $3,000 to $8,000, with a typical added value of $5,000 to $10,000. The Energy Efficiency and Conservation Authority (EECA) provides subsidies for insulation, which can help reduce the cost.

## Medium ROI Renovations
Some renovations may not add as much value as others, but can still be worthwhile. These medium ROI renovations include:

* **Bathroom full renovation**: A full bathroom renovation can be a medium ROI renovation. The cost can range from $20,000 to $45,000, with a typical added value of $20,000 to $40,000.
* **Kitchen full renovation**: A full kitchen renovation can also be a medium ROI renovation. The cost can range from $30,000 to $70,000, with a typical added value of $25,000 to $60,000.
* **New deck**: Installing a new deck can be a medium ROI renovation. The cost can range from $15,000 to $30,000, with a typical added value of $10,000 to $25,000.
* **Double glazing**: Installing double glazing can be a medium ROI renovation. The cost can range from $15,000 to $40,000, with a perceived value but slow payback on sale.

## Low ROI or Over-Capitalising Risks
Some renovations may not add as much value as they cost, or may even be considered over-capitalising. These low ROI renovations include:

* **Full house extension**: A full house extension can be a low ROI renovation. The cost can range from $100,000 to $500,000, with a typical added value of $50,000 to $200,000.
* **Luxury fitouts**: Installing luxury fitouts in entry-level suburbs can be a low ROI renovation. The cost can range from $10,000 to $50,000, with a typical added value of $5,000 to $20,000.
* **Swimming pools**: Installing a swimming pool can be a low ROI renovation. The cost can range from $50,000 to $150,000, with a typical added value of $20,000 to $50,000.
* **Bespoke or personal taste renovations**: Renovations that are highly bespoke or reflect personal taste can be a low ROI renovation. The cost can range from $5,000 to $50,000, with a typical added value of $2,000 to $10,000.

## The Healthy Homes Effect
The Healthy Homes Standards have had a significant impact on the NZ property market. Buyers are now more aware of the importance of insulation, ventilation, and heating in a property. Homes that are clearly warm and dry are attracting a premium, especially in the investor market.

According to WorkSafe NZ, the Healthy Homes Standards are designed to ensure that rental properties meet minimum standards for insulation, ventilation, and heating. This has led to an increase in demand for properties that meet these standards, and a corresponding increase in value.
