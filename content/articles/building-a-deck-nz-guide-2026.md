## Do you need building consent for a deck?
Under Schedule 1 of the Building Act 2004, decks that are less than 1.5 metres above ground level generally do not require building consent. However, there are some important exceptions to consider. If your deck is attached to a consented structure, such as a house, in a way that affects its weathertightness, you will need to obtain consent. Additionally, if your deck includes a significant structural element, such as a beam or a post, you may need to obtain consent. It's also important to check your district plan, as some councils have specific setback rules or requirements for pool fencing if your deck is near a pool. Balustrade height is also a critical factor, with a minimum height of 1 metre required where the fall from the deck exceeds 1 metre.

To determine whether you need building consent, it's always best to consult with your local council's duty planner, who can provide free advice and guidance. ## Timber selection
Choosing the right timber for your deck is a critical decision, as it will affect the appearance, durability, and maintenance requirements of your deck. Here are some popular options to consider:

* **Pine (treated)**: H3.2 treated pine is suitable for above-ground decking, while H4 treated pine is required for posts that will be in contact with the ground. Kiln-dried pine is recommended for decking to minimize cupping and warping. The cost of pine decking is around $30–$60 per linear metre.
* **Hardwood (kwila/merbau, vitex)**: Hardwood decking offers a beautiful finish and is more durable than pine. However, it is also more expensive, with prices ranging from $60–$120 per linear metre.
* **Composite decking (Trex, Futurewood, EasyDek)**: Composite decking is a low-maintenance option that is resistant to splintering and rot. It is also more expensive than timber, with prices ranging from $100–$200 per square metre. However, it comes with a 25-year warranty and requires minimal maintenance.

## Design considerations
When designing your deck, there are several key factors to consider. These include:

* **Board spacing**: For timber decking, a spacing of 5mm is recommended to allow for expansion and contraction. For composite decking, follow the manufacturer's specifications.
* **Joist spacing**: A typical joist spacing for 90mm decking is 450mm centres.
* **Post sizing**: A minimum post size of 90×90mm is recommended.
* **Ledger attachment**: The ledger attachment to the house is critical for weathertightness, so ensure that it is properly flashed and secured.
* **Drainage falls**: A minimum fall of 1:100 is recommended to ensure that water drains away from the house.

## Step-by-step construction overview
Here is a step-by-step guide to constructing your deck:

1. **Layout and posts**: Mark out the deck area and dig holes for the posts. Concrete the posts into place.
2. **Bearer installation**: Install the bearers on top of the posts, ensuring that they are level and securely attached.
3. **Joist frame**: Install the joist frame, spacing the joists at 450mm centres.
4. **Decking boards**: Install the decking boards, starting from the outside edge and working your way inwards.
5. **Balustrades**: If required, install the balustrades, ensuring that they meet the minimum height requirement of 1 metre.
6. **Finishing**: Finish the deck with oil or paint, depending on the type of timber used.

## Balustrade requirements
If your deck is more than 1 metre above the adjacent ground level, you will need to install balustrades to ensure safety. The minimum height requirement is 1 metre, and openings must not exceed 100mm to prevent child safety hazards. You can either build your own balustrades or purchase a kit system.

## Deck maintenance
To ensure that your deck lasts for years to come, regular maintenance is essential. Here are some tips:

* **Pine decking**: Re-oil or paint every 2–3 years to protect the timber from rot and decay.
* **Hardwood decking**: Oil annually for the first 2 years, then as needed to maintain the finish.
* **Composite decking**: Wash annually with soap and water to keep the deck clean and free of debris. No oiling is required.
