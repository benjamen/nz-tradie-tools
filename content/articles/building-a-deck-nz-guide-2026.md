## Do you need building consent for a deck?
Under Schedule 1 of the Building Act 2004, decks that are less than 1.5 metres above ground level generally do not require building consent. However, there are some important exceptions to consider. If your deck is attached to a consented structure, such as a house, in a way that affects its weathertightness, you may need to obtain consent. Additionally, if your deck includes a significant structural element, such as a beam or a post, you may need to consult with a building professional to determine whether consent is required.

It's also important to check your district plan for any specific rules or regulations that may apply to your deck. For example, some councils have setback rules that dictate how close to the boundary you can build, while others may have specific requirements for pool fencing or balustrades. If you're unsure about whether you need building consent, it's always best to err on the side of caution and consult with your local council's duty planner for free advice. ## Timber selection
When it comes to choosing the right timber for your deck, there are several options to consider. Here are a few popular choices:

* **Pine (treated)**: H3.2 treated pine is a popular choice for above-ground decking, while H4 treated pine is suitable for posts that will be in contact with the ground. Kiln-dried pine is also a good option for decking, as it is less prone to cupping and warping. The cost of pine decking can range from $30 to $60 per linear metre.
* **Hardwood (kwila/merbau, vitex)**: Hardwood decking offers a beautiful finish and is more durable than pine. However, it is also more expensive, with prices ranging from $60 to $120 per linear metre.
* **Composite decking (Trex, Futurewood, EasyDek)**: Composite decking is a low-maintenance option that is resistant to splintering and rot. It also comes with a 25-year warranty, making it a popular choice for homeowners who want a hassle-free deck. The cost of composite decking can range from $100 to $200 per square metre.

## Design considerations
When designing your deck, there are several key factors to consider. These include:

* **Board spacing**: The spacing between deck boards will depend on the type of timber you choose. For timber decking, a spacing of 5mm is typical, while composite decking may require a different spacing depending on the manufacturer's specifications.
* **Joist spacing**: The spacing between joists will depend on the size of your deck boards. For 90mm decking, a joist spacing of 450mm centres is typical.
* **Post sizing**: The minimum post size for a deck is 90x90mm.
* **Ledger attachment**: The ledger is the horizontal beam that attaches to the house. It's critical to ensure that the ledger is properly attached to the house to maintain weathertightness.
* **Drainage falls**: The deck should be designed to drain water away from the house, with a minimum fall of 1:100.

## Step-by-step construction overview
Here is a step-by-step guide to constructing your deck:

1. **Layout and posts**: Mark out the location of your deck and dig holes for the posts. Pour concrete into the holes and set the posts in place.
2. **Bearer installation**: Install the bearers, which are the horizontal beams that support the joists.
3. **Joist frame**: Install the joist frame, which consists of the joists and the ledger.
4. **Decking boards**: Install the decking boards, starting from the outside edge of the deck and working your way inwards.
5. **Balustrades**: If required, install balustrades to provide a safe barrier around the deck.
6. **Finishing**: Finish the deck with oil or paint to protect the timber and enhance its appearance.

## Balustrade requirements
If your deck is more than 1 metre above the adjacent ground level, you will need to install balustrades to provide a safe barrier. The minimum height for balustrades is 1 metre, and openings should not be more than 100mm to prevent children from falling through. You can either build your own balustrades or purchase a kit system.

## Deck maintenance
To keep your deck looking its best, it's essential to maintain it regularly. Here are some tips:

* **Pine decking**: Re-oil or paint every 2-3 years to protect the timber.
* **Hardwood decking**: Oil annually for the first 2 years, then as needed.
* **Composite decking**: Wash annually with soap and water, and avoid oiling or painting.
