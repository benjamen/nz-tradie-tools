## Introduction
As a homeowner in New Zealand, having a reliable hot water cylinder (HWC) is essential for daily life. However, like all appliances, HWCs have a limited lifespan and will eventually need replacing. Signs that your HWC needs replacing include its age (typically 10-15 years), rust, leaks, or cold showers. Waiting for your HWC to fail can lead to inconvenience, damage to your property, and higher replacement costs. It's crucial to plan ahead and consider your options before your current HWC fails. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of replacing your hot water cylinder in NZ, including the costs, options, and what to expect.

## Types of Hot Water Cylinders
When it comes to replacing your HWC, you have several options to consider. The most common type is the electric storage cylinder, which accounts for the majority of installations in NZ. However, other options like heat pump hot water, gas continuous flow, and solar thermal are gaining popularity due to their energy efficiency and environmental benefits.

* **Electric Storage Cylinder**: This is the most common type of HWC in NZ, with a capacity range of 180-300 litres. The supply cost for an electric storage cylinder ranges from $1,200 to $2,500, depending on the brand and quality.
* **Heat Pump Hot Water**: Heat pump hot water systems are 2-3 times more efficient than traditional electric storage cylinders. Brands like Sanden, Rheem, and Bosch offer heat pump hot water systems, with a supply cost ranging from $2,500 to $5,000. Installation costs can add an additional $500 to $1,000.
* **Gas Continuous Flow**: Gas continuous flow systems, like those offered by Rinnai and Rheem, provide hot water on demand without the need for a storage tank. The supply cost for a gas continuous flow system ranges from $1,000 to $2,500.
* **Solar Thermal**: Solar thermal systems use solar energy to heat water, with an electric boost to ensure a consistent supply of hot water. The installed cost of a solar thermal system can range from $5,000 to $10,000.

## Sizing Guide
When selecting a new HWC, it's essential to choose the right size to meet your household's hot water needs. A general rule of thumb is to allow 45-60 litres of storage per person. For example, a family of four would require a cylinder with a capacity of at least 180-240 litres. You should also consider the peak draw-off, which is the maximum amount of hot water required at any given time. Additionally, if you're on an off-peak tariff, you'll need to consider the difference between off-peak and anytime tariff cylinders.

## Cost of Replacement
The cost of replacing a hot water cylinder in NZ can vary depending on the type and size of the cylinder, as well as the labour costs involved. The supply cost of a standard electric storage cylinder can range from $1,200 to $2,500. Labour costs can add an additional $350 to $650, depending on the complexity of the installation. Other costs to consider include:

* Draincock: $50-$100
* Pressure limiting valve: $50-$100
* Thermostat expansion valve: $50-$100
* Disposal of old unit: $100-$200

The total typical cost of replacing a standard electric storage cylinder can range from $2,000 to $4,000.

## Heat Pump Hot Water — Is It Worth It?
Heat pump hot water systems are an energy-efficient alternative to traditional electric storage cylinders. With the help of the EECA Warmer Kiwi Homes subsidy, eligible households can receive up to $2,500 towards the cost of a heat pump hot water system. Additionally, heat pump hot water systems can provide typical electricity savings of $600 to $1,200 per year. While the upfront cost of a heat pump hot water system is higher, the payback period can be as short as 5-7 years, making it a worthwhile investment for many homeowners.

## Off-Peak Tariff
Off-peak tariffs, also known as Economy 7 or controlled load, offer a discounted electricity rate for hot water heating during off-peak hours. This can be a cost-effective option for households with a high hot water demand. However, it's essential to understand how off-peak pricing works in NZ and which retailers offer it. You'll also need to consider how off-peak pricing affects cylinder sizing, as you may require a larger cylinder to take advantage of the off-peak rate.

## Do You Need a Licensed Plumber?
Yes, in NZ, hot water cylinder replacement is considered restricted plumbing work and requires a licensed plumber. The plumber must be registered with the Plumbers, Gasfitters and Drainlayers Board (PGDB) and provide a Certificate of Compliance upon completion of the work.
