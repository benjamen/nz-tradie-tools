## Introduction
When it comes to home improvement, a well-designed and constructed driveway is a significant investment for any New Zealand homeowner. Not only does it provide a safe and durable entrance to your property, but it also adds to the overall aesthetic appeal of your home. In New Zealand's climate, with its high levels of rainfall, intense sunlight, and potential for heavy vehicle traffic, a driveway must be built to withstand these conditions. A concrete driveway is a popular choice among homeowners due to its durability, low maintenance requirements, and versatility in design. In this guide, we will explore the various options available for concrete driveways in New Zealand, including costs, sizing, drainage requirements, and what to expect from the construction process.

## Driveway Surface Options
When it comes to choosing a surface for your concrete driveway, there are several options to consider, each with its own unique characteristics, benefits, and costs. Here are some of the most common options:

* **Plain Concrete**: This is the most common type of concrete driveway and is known for its low maintenance requirements and affordability. The cost of plain concrete driveways ranges from $80 to $130 per square metre.
* **Exposed Aggregate**: This type of concrete driveway involves washing the fresh concrete to expose the pebbles, providing a textured surface with more grip. Exposed aggregate driveways are more expensive than plain concrete, with costs ranging from $110 to $160 per square metre.
* **Stamped/Coloured Concrete**: For a more decorative look, stamped or coloured concrete driveways are a popular choice. These driveways are more expensive, with costs ranging from $130 to $200 per square metre.
* **Asphalt**: Asphalt driveways are another option, although they require resealing every 5 years. The cost of asphalt driveways ranges from $50 to $90 per square metre.
* **Pavers (Concrete Block)**: Concrete pavers are a versatile option, allowing for individual blocks to be lifted and re-laid if services need access. The cost of pavers ranges from $120 to $220 per square metre.
* **Chip Seal (Tar Seal)**: Chip seal driveways are the least expensive option, but they require resurfacing regularly. The cost of chip seal driveways ranges from $30 to $60 per square metre.

## Concrete Driveway Sizing and Thickness
The size and thickness of your concrete driveway will depend on the intended use and the weight of the vehicles that will be using it. For typical residential use, a concrete driveway with a thickness of 80-120mm is sufficient. However, for heavier vehicles such as utes, vans, or trucks, a thicker driveway with a minimum thickness of 125-150mm is recommended. Reinforcing mesh, typically F72, is standard for residential concrete driveways to provide added strength and durability. Sub-base preparation is also critical to preventing cracking, with a compacted layer of AP40 or GAP40 material, 150-200mm thick, providing a stable foundation for the concrete.

## Drainage Requirements
In New Zealand, councils require that driveway drainage does not direct water onto neighbouring properties or public footpaths. A channel drain at the entrance (vehicle crossover) is mandatory in many areas to prevent water from flowing onto the road. Permeable options, such as permeable pavers or permeable asphalt, are increasingly encouraged as they allow water to drain through the surface, reducing the risk of flooding and erosion.

## Council Crossover/Vehicle Crossing
The area between the street and your property boundary is known as the council crossover or vehicle crossing. This area is subject to council regulations, and you may need a council permit to construct or modify it. The cost of a council crossover can range from $500 to $3,000, depending on the complexity of the job and the council's requirements. It's essential to check with your local council before starting any work on your driveway to ensure compliance with their regulations.

## DIY vs Contractor
While it may be tempting to attempt to build your concrete driveway yourself, it's not a project for the faint of heart. Concrete work is physically demanding and time-sensitive, with a limited window of 30-60 minutes to place and finish the concrete once the truck arrives. Additionally, formwork, screeding, and floating require skill and experience to avoid cracking and surface defects. It's highly recommended to hire a professional contractor to ensure a high-quality finish and avoid costly mistakes.

## FAQ
Here are some frequently asked questions about concrete driveways in New Zealand:

1. **How long does a concrete driveway last in NZ?**
A concrete driveway can last for 20-30 years or more, depending on the quality of the construction, maintenance, and environmental conditions.
2. **How soon can I drive on a new concrete driveway?**
It's recommended to wait at least 7-10 days before driving on a new concrete driveway to allow the concrete to cure and set.
3. **Do I need council permission for a driveway in NZ?**
Yes, you may need a council permit to construct or modify a driveway, especially if it involves the council crossover or vehicle crossing.
4. **What causes concrete driveways to crack?**
Concrete driveways can crack due to a variety of factors, including poor sub-base preparation, inadequate reinforcing, and extreme temperature fluctuations.
5. **Can I seal my own driveway?**
While it's possible to seal your own driveway, it's recommended to hire a professional to ensure a high-quality finish and avoid costly mistakes.
