## Introduction
A well-designed and constructed driveway is a significant home improvement that can enhance the curb appeal and value of a property in New Zealand. With the country's unique climate, characterised by heavy rainfall, intense sunlight, and varying temperatures, a durable and low-maintenance driveway is essential. The weight of vehicles, including cars, utes, and trucks, also plays a crucial role in determining the type of driveway surface and construction method. In this guide, we will explore the various options for concrete driveways in NZ, including costs, sizing, and drainage requirements. Whether you're a homeowner or a tradesperson, this comprehensive guide will provide you with the necessary information to make informed decisions about your next driveway project.

## Driveway Surface Options
When it comes to choosing a driveway surface, there are several options to consider, each with its own set of benefits and drawbacks. The most common types of driveway surfaces in NZ are:

* Plain concrete: This is the most common type of driveway surface, known for its low maintenance and durability. The cost of plain concrete driveways ranges from $80 to $130 per square metre.
* Exposed aggregate: This type of driveway surface involves washing the fresh concrete to expose the pebbles, providing a textured and slip-resistant finish. Exposed aggregate driveways cost between $110 and $160 per square metre.
* Stamped/coloured concrete: For a more decorative look, stamped or coloured concrete can be used to create a unique and visually appealing driveway. The cost of stamped or coloured concrete driveways ranges from $130 to $200 per square metre.
* Asphalt: Asphalt driveways are a cost-effective option, with prices ranging from $50 to $90 per square metre. However, they require resealing every 5 years to maintain their durability.
* Pavers (concrete block): Pavers are a popular choice for driveways, offering a durable and low-maintenance option. The cost of pavers ranges from $120 to $220 per square metre.
* Chip seal (tar seal): Chip seal driveways are the least expensive option, with prices ranging from $30 to $60 per square metre. However, they require resurfacing every 5-7 years.

## Concrete Driveway Sizing and Thickness
The sizing and thickness of a concrete driveway are critical factors in determining its durability and lifespan. Typical residential driveways in NZ are constructed with a thickness of 80-120mm. However, for heavier vehicles such as utes, vans, and trucks, a thicker driveway with a minimum thickness of 125-150mm is recommended. Reinforcing mesh, typically F72, is a standard requirement for residential driveways to provide additional strength and durability. Sub-base preparation is also critical, with a compacted layer of AP40 or GAP40 material, 150-200mm thick, to prevent cracking and ensure a stable foundation.

## Drainage Requirements
Drainage is a critical aspect of driveway construction in NZ, with councils requiring that driveways do not direct water onto neighbouring properties or public footpaths. A channel drain at the entrance of the driveway, also known as a vehicle crossover, is mandatory in many areas. Permeable options, such as permeable pavers or permeable asphalt, are increasingly encouraged to reduce stormwater runoff and alleviate pressure on the drainage system.

## Council Crossover/Vehicle Crossing
The council crossover, also known as the vehicle crossing, is the section of the driveway that connects the property to the street. In many areas, a council permit is required, and the work must meet council standards. The cost of a council crossover can range from $500 to $3,000, depending on the complexity of the work and the council's requirements. It's essential to check with your local council before starting any driveway project to determine the specific requirements and costs involved.

## DIY vs Contractor
While some homeowners may consider taking on a driveway project as a DIY task, it's not recommended. Concrete work is physically demanding and time-sensitive, requiring specialized skills and equipment to achieve a high-quality finish. Once the concrete truck arrives, there is only a 30-60 minute window to place and finish the concrete, making it a challenging task for inexperienced homeowners. Formwork, screeding, and floating require skill and attention to detail to avoid cracking and surface defects.

## FAQ
Here are some frequently asked questions about concrete driveways in NZ:

1. **How long does a concrete driveway last in NZ?**
A well-constructed concrete driveway can last for 20-30 years or more, depending on the quality of the materials and workmanship.
2. **How soon can I drive on a new concrete driveway?**
It's recommended to wait at least 7-10 days before driving on a new concrete driveway to allow the concrete to cure and reach its full strength.
3. **Do I need council permission for a driveway in NZ?**
Yes, in many areas, a council permit is required for driveway construction, especially if the work involves a council crossover or vehicle crossing.
4. **What causes concrete driveways to crack?**
Concrete driveways can crack due to a variety of factors, including poor sub-base preparation, inadequate reinforcing, and extreme temperature fluctuations.
5. **Can I seal my own driveway?**
While it's possible to seal your own driveway, it's recommended to hire a professional to ensure a high-quality finish and to avoid any potential damage to the concrete.
