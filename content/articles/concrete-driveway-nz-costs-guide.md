## Introduction
When it comes to home improvements, a well-designed and constructed driveway is often overlooked, yet it's one of the first things visitors notice when arriving at your property. In New Zealand, where the climate can be quite harsh with significant rainfall, UV exposure, and varying temperatures, a durable and long-lasting driveway is essential. The weight of vehicles, especially those with heavy loads, also puts a lot of stress on the driveway surface. As a homeowner, investing in a high-quality driveway not only enhances the aesthetic appeal of your property but also increases its value. This comprehensive guide will walk you through the various options, costs, and considerations for a concrete driveway in NZ, helping you make an informed decision for your next home improvement project.

## Driveway Surface Options
Choosing the right surface for your driveway can be a daunting task, given the numerous options available. Here's a breakdown of the most common types of driveways, including their costs, lifespans, maintenance requirements, and aesthetic appeal:

* **Plain Concrete**: This is the most common type of driveway in NZ, known for its low maintenance and affordability. The cost of plain concrete driveways ranges from $80 to $130 per square metre. While it may not offer the most decorative finish, it's a practical choice for many homeowners.
* **Exposed Aggregate**: This type of driveway involves washing the fresh concrete to expose the pebbles, providing a textured surface with better grip. Exposed aggregate driveways are priced between $110 and $160 per square metre. They offer a more decorative finish than plain concrete and are a popular choice for many NZ homeowners.
* **Stamped/Coloured Concrete**: For a more decorative finish, stamped or coloured concrete driveways are a great option. The cost of these driveways ranges from $130 to $200 per square metre. They offer a unique and personalized look, making them a favourite among homeowners who want to add some character to their property.
* **Asphalt**: Asphalt driveways are another option, priced between $50 and $90 per square metre. However, they require resealing every 5 years, which can add to their overall cost. Asphalt driveways are a good choice for those on a budget, but they may not be as durable as concrete driveways.
* **Pavers (Concrete Block)**: Pavers are a popular choice for driveways, offering a decorative finish and the ability to lift and re-lay them if services need access. The cost of pavers ranges from $120 to $220 per square metre. They're a great option for homeowners who want a low-maintenance driveway with a unique look.
* **Chip Seal (Tar Seal)**: Chip seal driveways are the least expensive option, priced between $30 and $60 per square metre. However, they require resurfacing every few years, which can add to their overall cost. Chip seal driveways are a good choice for those on a tight budget, but they may not be as durable as other options.

## Concrete Driveway Sizing and Thickness
When it comes to concrete driveways, sizing and thickness are crucial factors to consider. The typical residential concrete driveway is 80-120mm thick, while those that need to support heavy vehicles, such as utes, vans, or trucks, require a thickness of 125-150mm. Reinforcing mesh, typically F72, is standard for residential driveways to provide added strength and durability. Sub-base preparation is also critical, with 150-200mm of compacted AP40 or GAP40 material required to prevent cracking.

## Drainage Requirements
Drainage is an essential aspect of driveway construction, and NZ councils have specific requirements to ensure that water is not directed onto neighbouring properties or public footpaths. A channel drain at the entrance (vehicle crossover) is mandatory in many areas, and permeable options, such as permeable pavers or permeable asphalt, are increasingly encouraged. Homeowners must ensure that their driveway design meets these requirements to avoid any potential issues or fines.

## Council Crossover/Vehicle Crossing
The council crossover, also known as the vehicle crossing, is the section of the driveway that connects the property to the street. This area is subject to council regulations, and homeowners may need to obtain a permit before construction can begin. The cost of the council crossover can range from $500 to $3,000, depending on the council's requirements and the need for a kerb cut. It's essential to check with your local council before starting any driveway construction project.

## DIY vs Contractor
While some homeowners may consider taking on a driveway construction project themselves, it's not recommended. Concrete work is physically demanding and time-sensitive, with a limited window of 30-60 minutes to place and finish the concrete once the truck arrives. Formwork, screeding, and floating require skill and experience to avoid cracking and surface defects. It's best to hire a professional contractor to ensure a high-quality finish and avoid any potential issues.

## FAQ
Here are some frequently asked questions about concrete driveways in NZ:

1. **How long does a concrete driveway last in NZ?**
A concrete driveway can last for 20-30 years or more, depending on the quality of construction, maintenance, and environmental factors.
2. **How soon can I drive on a new concrete driveway?**
It's recommended to wait at least 7-10 days before driving on a new concrete driveway to allow the concrete to cure and harden.
3. **Do I need council permission for a driveway in NZ?**
Yes, you may need to obtain a permit from your local council, especially if you're constructing a new driveway or making significant changes to an existing one.
4. **What causes concrete driveways to crack?**
Concrete driveways can crack due to various factors, including poor construction, inadequate sub-base preparation, and environmental factors such as temperature fluctuations and soil movement.
5. **Can I seal my own driveway?**
While it's possible to seal your own driveway, it's recommended to hire a professional to ensure a high-quality finish and avoid any potential issues.
