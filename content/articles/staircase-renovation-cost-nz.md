## Common NZ Staircase Renovations

### Removing Carpet and Installing Timber Treads
The most popular NZ staircase renovation — removing worn carpet and exposing or adding timber treads gives a modern, low-maintenance look.

**If existing treads are good timber (rimu, pine):** Remove carpet, sand, and polish the existing timber. Cost per stair: $80–$180. Result depends on condition of existing timber under the carpet — common in older NZ homes to find good rimu underneath.

**Timber tread overlay:** A new engineered or solid timber tread glued and screwed over the existing tread. Faster and less structural disruption than full replacement. Common timber species in NZ: oak, rimu, bamboo, pine (stained). Cost per stair: $150–$350.

**Full tread and riser replacement:** Remove existing treads/risers and install new timber. More invasive but the cleanest result — allows full customisation of profile and species. Cost per stair: $200–$500.

### Handrails and Balustrades
Replacing an old timber balustrade (1980s–2000s common style: chunky pine spindles) is one of the highest-impact renovations per dollar spent.

**NZ Building Code requirements (F4):**
- Handrail height: 900–1,000mm above stair nosing for residential
- Balustrade must not be climbable (no horizontal rails acting as a ladder)
- Balustrade must withstand a minimum horizontal load of 0.75 kN
- Balusters: gaps must not allow a 100mm sphere to pass through

Any replacement balustrade must comply with these requirements — a builder or joiner doing this work should be familiar with them.

**Balustrade options:**
- **Timber spindles (painted or stained):** Traditional look, most affordable. $200–$500/lm installed.
- **Stainless steel or powder-coated steel:** Modern look, low maintenance. Wire rope balusters are popular in contemporary NZ homes. $300–$700/lm.
- **Glass panels (frameless or semi-frameless):** Premium look, opens up the space. Requires toughened or laminated safety glass to NZ Standard. $600–$1,400/lm.
- **Feature timber and steel mix:** Timber handrail over steel balustrade — popular contemporary style. $400–$900/lm.

### Full Staircase Replacement
When the existing staircase is structurally compromised, poorly configured, or completely dated, a full replacement makes more sense than piecemeal repairs.

**Straight staircase (standard):** $8,000–$25,000 supply and install, depending on materials, balustrade type, and whether a carpenter or specialist stair company does the work.

**Custom or feature staircases:** Floating treads (cantilevered from wall), curved staircases, open-riser designs, or spiral stairs are significantly more complex and expensive. Allow $20,000–$80,000+ for premium custom work.

## Does Staircase Work Need Building Consent?

- **Cosmetic work** (staining, carpet removal, painting): No consent required.
- **Like-for-like handrail/balustrade replacement:** Generally no consent, but the new balustrade must still meet F4 requirements.
- **Structural changes** (moving staircase position, changing configuration, altering floor opening): Building consent required.
- **Full staircase replacement in-situ:** Usually no consent if it's the same position and configuration; check with your builder.

## Who Does Staircase Work in NZ?

- **Stair specialists:** Companies that design and build staircases — best for custom or full replacement work
- **Joiners / cabinet makers:** Good for handrails, balustrades, and tread work
- **Builders / carpenters (LBP):** Suitable for most staircase renovation work
- **Floor sanders:** Handle sanding and polishing of timber treads

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does it cost to renovate a staircase in NZ?**
Carpet removal + sand and polish existing timber (12 stairs): $1,000–$2,500. Timber tread overlay (12 stairs): $2,000–$4,500. New balustrade (5lm, glass): $3,000–$7,000. Full staircase replacement (straight, standard): $8,000–$25,000. A mid-range staircase update (new treads + new balustrade) typically costs $6,000–$15,000.

**Is it worth renovating a staircase in NZ?**
Yes for most two-storey homes — the staircase is a focal point and a prominent feature when entering the home. A tired 1990s pine balustrade replaced with steel or glass, combined with polished timber treads, has a dramatic visual impact and adds real value at resale. Budget for $5,000–$15,000 for a mid-range renovation with meaningful visual impact.

**What timber should I use for NZ stair treads?**
Engineered oak: most popular choice in NZ renovation staircases — stable, hard-wearing, beautiful grain, available in multiple widths. Solid rimu: classic NZ look, excellent if you can match existing floors. Solid pine (stained): affordable but softer — will dent under heavy use. Bamboo: stable and hard, but limited colour options. Match to existing floor species and finish if possible.

**Do I need a building consent to replace my staircase balustrade in NZ?**
Usually no, for like-for-like replacement in the same position. However, the new balustrade must comply with NZ Building Code clause F4 (handrail height, load rating, gap sizing). Your builder or joiner should be aware of these requirements. If you're changing the staircase configuration or position, building consent is required.

**How long does a staircase renovation take?**
Carpet removal + sand and polish: 2–3 days. Tread overlay (12 stairs): 2–4 days. Balustrade replacement: 1–3 days. Full staircase replacement: 1–3 weeks depending on complexity. Custom staircases with fabrication time: 4–10 weeks from order to installation.
