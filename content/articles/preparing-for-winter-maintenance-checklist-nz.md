# Preparing for Winter: A Maintenance Checklist for NZ Tradies

The colder months are approaching fast in New Zealand, and for many trades, this means a shift in the type of work you’ll be doing. Instead of just new builds, you’ll likely see an increase in call-outs for weatherproofing, roof repairs, and insulation upgrades.

This guide provides a professional checklist you can use to consult with your clients on preparing their properties for the upcoming NZ winter.

## 1. Roof and Gutter Inspection
Heavy rainfall and wind are standard features of an NZ winter.
- **Check for blockages:** Clear out gutters and downpipes to prevent overflow, which can lead to timber rot or structural issues.
- **Inspect roofing:** Look for loose tiles, damaged flashings, or signs of rust on metal roofs. Early intervention prevents costly leaks.

## 2. Insulation and Moisture Control
Warmer homes are healthier homes.
- **Ventilation:** Check sub-floor ventilation and bathroom/kitchen extraction to prevent condensation buildup, which leads to mould—a major issue in many NZ homes.
- **Insulation:** Suggest top-up insulation if properties are older. Better insulation not only keeps heat in but also helps manage moisture balance.

## 3. Outdoor Areas
- **Drainage:** Ensure external drains are clear to prevent localized flooding during heavy storm events.
- **Decking:** Check for slippery surfaces. Regular cleaning helps remove moss and algae that become hazardous in wet conditions.
