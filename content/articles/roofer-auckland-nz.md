## Auckland Roofing Context

### Auckland's Roof Challenges
Auckland's climate is demanding for roofs:

**High rainfall:** Auckland receives 1,200mm+ of rain annually (more in wet years), spread throughout the year. Roofs have no long dry season to recover.

**Salt air:** Large parts of Auckland — the North Shore, eastern bays (Howick to Beachlands), Waiheke Island, west coast (Piha, Bethells, Muriwai) — are in salt-air environments. Salt accelerates corrosion of roofing iron and fasteners, breaks down sealants, and causes premature failure of unpainted roofs.

**Volcanic rock:** Parts of Auckland (particularly Waitemata Harbour areas) have shallow volcanic rock close to the surface, which can affect drainage around buildings and accelerate moisture problems.

**Diverse housing stock:** Auckland has a wide range of roof types — Colorsteel long-run iron (most common in new builds), concrete tile (common 1970s–2000s), clay tile (some older suburbs), stone chip metal tile (1980s–2000s), and natural slate (rare, heritage homes).

### When Auckland Roofs Fail
Signs your Auckland roof needs attention:
- Water stains on ceilings or walls
- Missing, cracked, or slipping tiles
- Rust visible on iron roof (surface rust vs holes — key distinction)
- Lifted or missing flashing at valleys, ridges, or penetrations
- Visible daylight from inside the roof space
- After-storm debris near downpipes (granules from stone-chip tiles)
- Dark streaks or moss on the roof surface

## Finding a Reliable Auckland Roofer

### What to Look For
**Roofing NZ membership:** The industry association for NZ roofers. Members agree to a code of practice.

**Experience with your roof type:** Iron roof specialists, tile roofers, and slate specialists all exist. Match the roofer to your roof type.

**Written inspection report before quoting repairs:** A reputable Auckland roofer will do a proper inspection and provide a written report before quoting repairs. Be very cautious of door-knockers who spot a problem from the street and want to start work immediately.

**Warranty:** On both materials and workmanship. Colorsteel comes with a 25+ year manufacturer warranty; workmanship warranties of 5–10 years are reasonable.

**Height safety compliance:** Auckland roofers must comply with WorkSafe NZ's Working at Height regulations. Ask whether they use scaffolding, safety harnesses, and fall arrest systems. A roofer working without safety systems is breaking the law and putting themselves and you at risk.

### Auckland Roofing Scams to Watch For
Auckland has had ongoing issues with roofing scammers — travelling teams who approach homeowners claiming they spotted damage from the street:

- They request cash payment and want to start same day
- They "find" far more damage than actually exists once on the roof
- They use low-quality materials or do substandard work
- They cannot be found after payment

**Rule:** Never agree to roof work from a cold-caller at your door. Get a second opinion from an established local roofer. Legitimate roofers don't need to cold-call.

**Questions to ask:**
1. How long have you been operating in Auckland?
2. Can you provide 2–3 local references for similar work?
3. Do you carry public liability insurance?
4. Will you provide a written inspection report and scope of work before starting?
5. What safety systems do you use when working at height?
6. What warranty do you provide on workmanship?

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does a roof replacement cost in Auckland?**
Iron roof (3-bed home): $18,000–$40,000. Concrete tile roof (3-bed): $22,000–$50,000. Auckland prices are 15–25% above the national average due to labour costs and material delivery costs. Get 3 written quotes from established Auckland roofers.

**How do I find a trustworthy roofer in Auckland?**
Ask neighbours who have had recent roofing work done. Check Roofing NZ membership. Get 3 written quotes (not verbal). Avoid anyone who cold-calls at your door claiming to have spotted damage. Check Google reviews for Auckland-based roofing companies with a track record.

**How long does a roof last in Auckland?**
Colorsteel long-run iron: 40–70 years with painting and maintenance. Concrete tile: 30–50 years (tiles last longer, mortar bedding needs attention at 20–30 years). Clay tile: 50–100+ years. Stone-chip metal tile: 30–50 years. Salt air areas on Auckland's coast will be at the shorter end of these ranges.

**Do I need consent for roof replacement in Auckland?**
Like-for-like replacement (same material, same design): generally no consent required. Changing roof material or pitch: building consent required. Any structural changes: consent required. Your roofer will advise — Auckland Council consent for straightforward re-roofing is not complex.

**Why does my Auckland roof have moss and lichen?**
Auckland's humid, mild climate is ideal for moss and lichen growth — particularly on north-facing and shaded areas with less UV. Moss retains moisture against the roof surface, accelerating deterioration underneath. Treatment: biocide spray (kills the growth at root level), followed by washing once dead. Regular roof washing every 2–3 years prevents heavy buildup.
