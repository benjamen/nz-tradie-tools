## Christchurch Roofing Context

### Post-Earthquake Roof Issues

The 2010–2011 Canterbury earthquakes caused significant roof damage across Christchurch:

**Chimney damage and removal:** Earthquake movement toppled or damaged many brick chimneys — and many Christchurch homes had chimneys removed as part of EQC repairs. Where chimneys were removed, the roof penetration needed careful waterproofing (a new flashing and lead apron). Poorly done chimney removals are a known leak source on post-EQ Christchurch homes.

**Ridge and hip displacement:** Seismic movement shifted ridge cappings and hip tiles on many concrete tile roofs — gaps allow water ingress. If you're buying a Christchurch property with a tile roof, a roof inspection specifically checking ridge integrity is worthwhile.

**Gutter and fascia movement:** Settlement cracked or displaced gutters on many properties, especially in TC2 and TC3 zones.

**EQC repair quality:** Some EQC-funded roofing repairs were done under time pressure and may not have been to a high standard. If your property was repaired post-earthquake, a professional roof inspection gives you confidence about the repair quality.

### Canterbury Climate Considerations

**Hail:** Canterbury experiences periodic significant hailstorms. Longrun or corrugate steel roofs are generally unaffected, but lightweight or older profile steel can dent. Concrete tiles can crack from heavy hail. After a major Canterbury hailstorm, a professional inspection is worthwhile.

**UV:** Canterbury's high sunshine hours mean significant UV on roof surfaces — particularly relevant for painted iron roofs. Repainting aged corrugated iron is a cost-effective way to extend life (vs full re-roofing) when the substrate is still sound.

**Frost:** Canterbury frosts can cause rubber flashings and sealants to deteriorate faster than in warmer NZ regions.

## When to Re-Roof vs Repair in Christchurch

**Re-roof when:**
- Corrugated iron is rusting through (not just surface rust)
- Multiple leaks in different locations — re-roofing is cheaper than patching every 2 years
- Roof is 35+ years old on iron, 50+ on tile
- You're renovating and want to avoid future disruption

**Repair when:**
- Single or isolated leak with identifiable cause (flashing, broken tile, joint failure)
- Roof is under 25 years old and in overall good condition
- EQC assessment identified localised damage

A good roofer will give you an honest assessment — if a repair will last 5+ years, a reputable roofer will say so rather than push for a full re-roof.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does it cost to re-roof a house in Christchurch?**
Re-roofing 100m² with longrun steel (COLORSTEEL): $12,000–$25,000. Corrugated iron: $8,000–$18,000. Concrete tile: $14,000–$28,000. Christchurch rates are 10–20% below Auckland. Size, pitch, removal of existing roof, and product grade all affect final cost.

**Should I get a roof inspection on a Christchurch property?**
Yes, particularly for any property that was earthquake-affected. Key things to check: ridge/hip tile integrity, chimney removal flashings, gutter alignment post-settlement, and quality of any EQC-funded roof repairs. A professional inspection costs $150–$400 and can identify issues before they become expensive leaks.

**How long does a roof last in Canterbury?**
Painted corrugated iron: 25–40 years (may need repainting at 15–20 years). Longrun COLORSTEEL®: 40–50+ years. Concrete tile: 40–60 years. Canterbury's temperature extremes and UV accelerate some degradation relative to milder NZ regions.

**Is longrun or corrugate iron better for a Canterbury home?**
Longrun steel is the modern preference — concealed fixings mean fewer potential leak points, and it performs well in Canterbury's hail events. Corrugate iron is suitable for heritage character homes or budget re-roofs and is well-proven in Canterbury. Both are appropriate; the choice often comes down to aesthetic preference and budget.
