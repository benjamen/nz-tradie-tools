## Steam Cleaning vs Dry Cleaning

### Hot Water Extraction (Steam Cleaning)
The most common professional carpet cleaning method in NZ. Hot water and cleaning solution are injected into the carpet under pressure, then extracted with a powerful vacuum. Removes deep-set dirt, allergens, and bacteria effectively.

**Drying time:** 4–8 hours for carpets to dry fully. Avoid heavy foot traffic until dry.

**Best for:** Regular maintenance cleaning, allergen removal, heavily soiled carpets, most residential situations.

**Limitation:** Not suitable for natural fibre rugs (wool, sisal) that can shrink when wet — check with the cleaner first.

### Dry Cleaning / Low-Moisture
A dry compound or foam is worked into the carpet, then extracted. Dries much faster (1–2 hours) but typically doesn't penetrate as deeply as hot water extraction.

**Best for:** Commercial settings needing rapid turnaround, delicate fibres, or spot-treatment between full cleans.

### Encapsulation
A newer method where cleaning crystals encapsulate soil particles as they dry, then are vacuumed up. Very fast drying. Good for commercial maintenance. Increasingly offered by NZ cleaning companies.

## End of Tenancy Carpet Cleaning in NZ

Under the [Residential Tenancies Act 1986](https://www.legislation.govt.nz/act/public/1986/0120/latest/whole.html) and 2019 amendments, tenants must leave carpets in the same condition as at the start of tenancy, allowing for fair wear and tear.

**What this means in practice:**
- Carpets that were professionally cleaned at the start of tenancy must be professionally cleaned at the end
- Fair wear and tear (lightening from use, normal flattening) is NOT the tenant's responsibility
- Stains beyond normal wear, pet damage, or odours may be the tenant's responsibility

Most landlords and property managers require a **receipt from a professional carpet cleaning company** as proof. DIY cleaning is generally not accepted for end-of-tenancy purposes.

**Typical end-of-tenancy carpet cleaning cost (3-bed house):** $250–$400.

## How Often Should Carpets Be Professionally Cleaned?

| Situation | Recommended frequency |
|---|---|
| Standard household (no pets) | Every 12–18 months |
| Household with pets | Every 6–12 months |
| High-traffic areas (hallways) | Every 6–12 months |
| End of tenancy | Before vacating |
| Asthma / allergy households | Every 6–12 months |
| After flood or water damage | Immediately |

Regular vacuuming (2–3 times per week) extends the time between professional cleans by preventing dirt from grinding deep into carpet fibres.

## What Makes Carpet Cleaning More Expensive?

**Stain severity:** Heavy staining (pet urine, red wine, paint) requires pre-treatment and sometimes multiple passes — adds $30–$80 per stain area.

**Pet urine:** Urine odour often requires treatment beyond standard cleaning — an enzyme treatment or deodoriser application ($50–$150 extra) is typically needed. Severe or long-standing pet urine damage may require carpet replacement, not just cleaning.

**Furniture moving:** Most cleaners work around furniture you've moved to the edges. If you want them to move furniture, expect an additional fee or check if it's included.

**Carpet condition:** Very dirty carpets take longer and use more product. Some companies charge more for heavily soiled carpets.

**Access:** Ground floor, easy access — standard rate. Long carry from van to carpet (apartment, no lift) — may add time cost.

## DIY Carpet Cleaning

Carpet cleaning machines are available from Mitre 10, Bunnings, and specialist hire companies ($80–$150/day hire). DIY cleaning can work for light maintenance but has limitations:

- **Less suction power:** Hire machines extract less water than professional truck-mount units — carpets stay damp longer (12–24 hours) with risk of mould
- **Less heat:** Less effective on bacteria and allergens
- **Learning curve:** Over-wetting or wrong product can damage carpets
- **Pet odour:** Domestic machines rarely achieve the deep penetration needed for pet urine treatment

For end-of-tenancy purposes, DIY cleaning is usually not sufficient — you need a receipt from a professional.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does carpet cleaning cost in NZ?**
A full 3-bedroom house: $250–$500 for professional steam cleaning. A single large room: $80–$150. End-of-tenancy clean (3 bed): $250–$400. Most companies have a minimum call-out fee of $120–$180 regardless of area.

**How long does carpet cleaning take to dry in NZ?**
Hot water extraction (steam cleaning): 4–8 hours with good ventilation. Open windows and run fans to speed drying. Avoid walking on carpets until dry — walking on damp carpet presses dirt back in and can cause re-soiling.

**Does professional carpet cleaning remove pet urine smell?**
Regular steam cleaning reduces odour but may not eliminate it. Pet urine requires enzyme treatment to break down uric acid crystals — these aren't removed by steam alone. Confirm with your cleaner that they offer enzyme treatment if pet urine odour is present.

**Can tenants use DIY cleaning instead of professional at end of tenancy?**
In most cases, no — if the property inspection report at the start of tenancy noted professionally cleaned carpets, landlords can require the same standard at the end. A receipt from a professional cleaning company is the standard of proof. Check the condition report from when you moved in.

**How often should I professionally clean my carpets in NZ?**
Every 12–18 months for a standard household. Every 6–12 months with pets or allergy sufferers. More frequent vacuuming (2–3×/week) is the most effective way to extend the time between professional cleans.
