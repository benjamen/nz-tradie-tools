## What You'll Need
Before you start painting, it's essential to have the right tools and materials. Here's a list of what you'll need:
* A roller and extension pole (approximately $20-$50)
* A paint tray (around $10-$20)
* Angled sash brushes (2.5" or 6.4cm) for cutting in (around $10-$20 each)
* Drop cloths to protect your floors (approximately $20-$50)
* Masking tape for skirting boards and architraves (around $5-$10)
* Filler (such as Polyfilla) for holes and cracks (approximately $10-$20)
* Sandpaper for smoothing out surfaces (around $5-$10)
* Sugar soap for cleaning walls (approximately $10-$20)
* Paint, of course! (prices vary depending on the brand and type, but expect to pay between $35-$80 per litre)

When it comes to paint, you'll need to decide between water-based and oil-based options. Water-based paints are generally easier to clean up and have less odour, while oil-based paints provide a harder, more durable finish. In NZ, popular paint brands include Resene, Dulux, and Wattyl. Finish types also vary, with options including flat, low-sheen, semi-gloss, and gloss.

To calculate how much paint you'll need, you'll need to consider the surface area of the walls, ceiling, and trim. A general rule of thumb is to allow for 1 litre of paint to cover approximately 12-16 square metres of surface area, depending on the surface and the number of coats. For example, if you're painting a standard bedroom with a surface area of around 40 square metres, you'll need around 2.5-3.5 litres of paint.

## Prep: The Key to a Good Result
Preparation is the most critical step in achieving a professional-looking paint job. Here's what you need to do:
* Wash the walls with sugar soap to remove grease, grime, and old smoke residue. This will ensure a strong bond between the paint and the surface.
* Fill any holes or cracks with filler, such as Polyfilla. Allow the filler to dry completely before sanding it back to a smooth finish.
* Sand back any rough patches or areas where the filler has been applied. This will help create a smooth, even surface for painting.
* Mask up skirting boards, architraves, and power points using masking tape. This will protect these areas from paint and make cleanup easier.
* Protect your floors with drop cloths. This will prevent paint spills and stains from damaging your flooring.
* Prime new GIB or repaired patches if necessary. If the new surface is a different colour to the surrounding area, it's essential to prime it first to prevent the colour from showing through.

Priming is a crucial step that many DIYers skip, but it's essential for achieving a professional-looking finish. If you're painting over a dark colour or a bold pattern, you may need to apply a primer first to ensure the new paint colour covers evenly.

## Order of Painting
When it comes to painting, it's essential to follow the correct order to avoid lap marks and uneven coverage. Here's the order you should follow:
* Paint the ceiling first. This will prevent paint from dripping onto the walls and trim.
* Paint the walls next. Start at the top and work your way down to avoid drips and runs.
* Paint the trim last. This includes skirting boards, architraves, and door frames.

By following this order, you'll be able to achieve a smooth, even finish and avoid the common mistake of lap marks.

## Cutting In
Cutting in is the process of painting the edges of the walls, ceiling, and trim using a brush. It's a critical step in achieving a professional-looking finish, and it requires some skill and patience. Here's how to do it:
* Use an angled sash brush (2.5" or 6.4cm) to cut in around the edges of the walls, ceiling, and trim.
* Work in small sections, using a technique called the 3-wet-edge method. This involves painting a small section, then moving on to the next section before the paint dries.
* Use a steady, smooth motion to paint the edges, working from the top down.
* Avoid rushing the cutting-in process, as this can lead to uneven coverage and lap marks.

Cutting in is one of the most challenging parts of painting, but with practice, you'll get the hang of it. Remember to take your time and work slowly, using a steady, smooth motion to achieve a professional-looking finish.

## Rolling
Rolling is the process of applying paint to the main surfaces of the walls and ceiling using a roller. Here's how to do it:
* Use a W-pattern to roll the paint onto the surface, working in small sections.
* Keep a wet edge by rolling in the same direction as the previous section.
* Avoid applying too much pressure, as this can create roller marks.
* Work in small sections, using a technique called the 3-wet-edge method.

Rolling is a bit easier than cutting in, but it still requires some skill and patience. Remember to work slowly and steadily, using a W-pattern to achieve a smooth, even finish.

## Trim and Skirting
When it comes to painting trim and skirting, it's essential to use the right brush and technique. Here's what you need to do:
* Use a high-quality brush with synthetic bristles to paint the trim and skirting.
* Work in small sections, using a steady, smooth motion to paint the edges.
* Use a oil-based paint for trim and skirting, as this will provide a harder, more durable finish.

Painting trim and skirting requires some skill and patience, but with practice, you'll get the hang of it. Remember to work slowly and steadily, using a high-quality brush and the right technique to achieve a professional-looking finish.

## Drying Times and Second Coats
When it comes to drying times and second coats, it's essential to follow the manufacturer's instructions. Here's what you need to know:
* Resene and Dulux recommend waiting at least 2 hours before applying a second coat.
* Wattyl recommends waiting at least 1 hour before applying a second coat.
* Humidity can affect drying times, so be sure to check the manufacturer's instructions for specific guidance.

It's essential to wait the recommended amount of time before applying a second coat, as this will ensure the paint is fully dry and ready for the next coat.

## Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them
Here are some common mistakes to avoid when painting a room:
* Skipping primer: This can lead to uneven coverage and a poor finish.
* Painting over dusty or dirty surfaces: This can lead to a poor bond between the paint and the surface.
* Using the wrong roller nap: This can lead to roller marks and an uneven finish.
* Painting in direct sunlight: This can lead to uneven drying and a poor finish.

By avoiding these common mistakes, you'll be able to achieve a professional-looking finish and avoid costly rework.
