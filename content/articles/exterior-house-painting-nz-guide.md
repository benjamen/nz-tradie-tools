## Introduction
Exterior house painting is one of the most important maintenance jobs on a New Zealand home. The country's harsh climate, with high levels of UV exposure, rain, and wind, can take a significant toll on a house's exterior surfaces. If left unchecked, this can lead to costly repairs and even compromise the structural integrity of the building. As a homeowner, it's essential to know when to repaint and how to do it correctly to ensure your home remains safe, secure, and looking its best. In this guide, we'll cover the importance of exterior painting, how often to do it, and what to use. We'll also delve into the costs involved, provide tips on how to prepare your home, and offer advice on finding the right painter for the job.

## How Often Does Exterior Paint Last in NZ?
The lifespan of exterior paint in New Zealand varies significantly depending on the region. Homes in coastal areas, such as Northland and Auckland, typically require repainting every 7-10 years due to the high salt content in the air and intense sunlight. In central New Zealand, the paint can last for 10-15 years, while in Canterbury and Otago, it can last for 12-18 years due to the lower UV levels and frost. However, these are general guidelines, and the actual lifespan of the paint will depend on various factors, including the quality of the paint, the surface it's applied to, and the level of maintenance.

Signs that it's time to repaint your home's exterior include:
* Peeling or chalking paint
* Cracking or bare timber showing
* Mould that won't wash off
* Fading or discolouration of the paint

If you notice any of these signs, it's essential to act quickly to prevent further damage to your home's surfaces.

## Surface Types and Considerations
Different surface types require different preparation and painting techniques. Here are some common surface types found in New Zealand homes:

* **Weatherboard (timber)**: This is the most prep-intensive surface type, requiring sanding and priming of bare patches. A flexible paint, such as Resene Lumbersider or Dulux Weathershield, is recommended to accommodate the natural movement of the timber.
* **Fibrous cement (Hardiflex)**: If your home has fibrous cement cladding, it's essential to test for asbestos before sanding, especially if it was installed before 1990. Resene Lumbersider or Dulux 10yr exterior paint is suitable for this surface type.
* **Plaster/render (solid plaster, roughcast, monolithic)**: Breathable paint is required for these surfaces to prevent moisture from becoming trapped. Resene Roof & Spouting or specialist plaster paints are recommended. If you have a monolithic plaster home, it's crucial to check for leaky home symptoms before painting.
* **Colosteel/metal surfaces**: A rust-inhibiting primer and direct-to-metal top coat are necessary for these surfaces to prevent corrosion.

## Prep — The Most Important Step
Preparation is the key to a successful exterior painting job. Here are the steps involved:
* **Water blasting**: Hire a water blaster for $80-$150 per day or hire a contractor for $300-$800 to blast your entire house. This will remove dirt, grime, and loose paint.
* **Drying time**: Allow 2-3 fine days for the surfaces to dry before painting.
* **Scrape and sand**: Scrape off peeling paint and sand the surfaces to create a smooth finish.
* **Fill cracks**: Use an exterior-grade filler, such as Resene Filler or Selleys No More Gaps exterior, to fill any cracks or holes.
* **Spot prime**: Apply a primer to bare timber and repairs to ensure a strong bond between the paint and the surface.
* **Mask up**: Mask glass and trim areas where you're changing the colour to prevent paint from getting on these surfaces.

## Paint Selection
When it comes to exterior paint, there are two main types: water-based and oil-based. In New Zealand, most painters now use water-based exterior paint due to its faster drying time, better flexibility, and lower VOC (volatile organic compound) levels. The sheen level of the paint is also important, with low-sheen paint recommended for walls and semi-gloss paint for trim. The three main NZ brands with full exterior systems are Resene, Dulux, and Wattyl.

## Number of Coats
One coat of paint is rarely enough for exterior surfaces. Two coats are standard, but three coats may be necessary on previously bare or heavily sanded surfaces.

## DIY vs Hire a Painter
A full exterior repaint of a 3-bedroom house is a significant job that requires 2-3 people for a week. It involves working at heights and accessing difficult areas, which can be risky. While some homeowners may be able to paint ground-level and garage areas, two-storey homes require scaffolding, which can be hired for $400-$1,200 per week. Professional painters have the right equipment and expertise to do the job safely and efficiently. ## FAQ
Here are some frequently asked questions about exterior house painting in New Zealand:
1. **How often should you paint the exterior of a house in NZ?**
The frequency of exterior painting depends on the region, surface type, and climate. As a general rule, homes in coastal areas require repainting every 7-10 years, while those in central New Zealand can last for 10-15 years.
2. **How do I get rid of mould before painting?**
To remove mould, use a solution of water and bleach, and scrub the area with a brush. Rinse the area thoroughly and allow it to dry before painting.
3. **Can I paint fibrous cement cladding myself?**
While it's possible to paint fibrous cement cladding yourself, it's essential to test for asbestos first, especially if it was installed before 1990. If you're not comfortable with the process, consider hiring a professional painter.
4. **What's the best exterior paint for NZ conditions?**
The best exterior paint for New Zealand conditions is a water-based paint with a high level of UV resistance and flexibility. Resene, Dulux, and Wattyl are reputable brands that offer a range of exterior paints suitable for NZ conditions.
5. **Do painters supply the paint or do I?**
It's common for painters to supply the paint, but it's essential to clarify this with your painter before hiring them. Make sure to specify the paint brand and system you want to use to ensure you get the best results.
