## Introduction
Exterior house painting is one of the most important maintenance jobs on a New Zealand home. The country's unique climate, with high levels of UV exposure, rain, and wind, can take a significant toll on a house's exterior surfaces. If left unchecked, this can lead to costly repairs and even compromise the structural integrity of the building. As a homeowner, it's essential to stay on top of exterior painting to protect your investment and maintain your property's value. But when is the right time to repaint, and what are the signs that it's too late? This guide will cover everything you need to know about exterior house painting in NZ, from preparation and surface types to costs and finding the right painter.

## How often does exterior paint last in NZ?
The lifespan of exterior paint in NZ varies significantly depending on the region. In coastal areas like Northland and Auckland, the high salt content in the air and intense UV radiation can reduce the paint's lifespan to 7-10 years. In central NZ, the paint can last 10-15 years, while in Canterbury and Otago, the combination of UV radiation and frost can result in a lifespan of 12-18 years. Signs that it's time to repaint include peeling, chalking, cracking, bare timber showing, and mould that won't wash off. If you notice any of these signs, it's essential to act quickly to prevent further damage.

## Surface types and considerations
Different surface types require unique considerations when it comes to exterior painting. 

* **Weatherboard (timber)**: This is the most prep-intensive surface type, requiring sanding and priming of bare patches. A flexible paint like Resene Lumbersider or Dulux Weathershield is recommended to accommodate the natural movement of the timber.
* **Fibrous cement (Hardiflex)**: If your home has fibrous cement cladding installed before 1990, it may contain asbestos. It's crucial to test for asbestos before sanding to avoid releasing harmful particles into the air. Resene Lumbersider or Dulux 10yr exterior paint is suitable for fibrous cement surfaces.
* **Plaster/render (solid plaster, roughcast, monolithic)**: Breathable paint is essential for plaster surfaces to prevent moisture buildup. Resene Roof & Spouting or specialist plaster paints are recommended. If you have a monolithic plaster home, it's essential to check for leaky home symptoms before painting.
* **Colosteel/metal surfaces**: A rust-inhibiting primer and direct-to-metal top coat are necessary to protect metal surfaces from corrosion.

## Prep — the most important step
Preparation is the most critical step in exterior painting. It's what sets the stage for a successful and long-lasting paint job. Here are the key steps to follow:

* **Water blasting**: Hire a water blaster for $80-$150 per day or hire a contractor for $300-$800 to blast your entire house. This will help remove dirt, grime, and loose paint.
* **Drying time**: Allow 2-3 fine days for the surfaces to dry completely before painting.
* **Scrape and sand**: Scrape off peeling paint and sand the surfaces to create a smooth finish.
* **Fill cracks**: Use exterior-grade filler like Resene Filler or Selleys No More Gaps exterior to fill cracks and holes.
* **Spot prime**: Apply a primer to bare timber and repairs to ensure a strong bond between the paint and the surface.
* **Mask up**: Mask glass and trim areas where you're changing the colour to prevent paint from getting on these surfaces.

## Paint selection
When it comes to exterior paint, water-based is the preferred choice in NZ. It offers faster drying times, better flexibility, and lower VOCs (volatile organic compounds) compared to oil-based paint. For sheen level, low-sheen is recommended for walls, while semi-gloss is suitable for trim. The three main NZ paint brands, Resene, Dulux, and Wattyl, offer full exterior systems that cater to different surface types and conditions.

## Number of coats
One coat of paint is rarely enough for exterior surfaces. Two coats are standard, but three coats may be necessary for previously bare or heavily sanded surfaces. This ensures a strong, even finish that will withstand the elements.

## DIY vs hire a painter
A full exterior repaint of a 3-bedroom house is a significant undertaking that requires 2-3 people and a week's worth of work. It involves heights and access, which can be hazardous if not done properly. While some homeowners may be able to tackle ground-level and garage areas, two-storey houses require scaffolding, which can be hired for $400-$1,200 per week. The risk of falls and other accidents is high, making it essential to consider hiring a professional painter. ## Exterior painting costs NZ
The cost of exterior painting in NZ varies depending on the surface type, size of the house, and location. Here are some estimated costs:

* **Professional painter — weatherboard 3-bed house**: $6,000-$15,000 (includes prep, materials, and 2 coats of paint for walls and trim)
* **Professional painter — plaster house (less prep)**: $5,000-$12,000
* **Per m² rate**: $20-$45 per m² of wall area (surfaces vary significantly)
* **Scaffolding (if required)**: $800-$2,500 extra
* **Materials only (DIY)**: $1,500-$4,000 for a 3-bed house

## FAQ
Here are some frequently asked questions about exterior house painting in NZ:

1. **How often should you paint the exterior of a house in NZ?**: The frequency of exterior painting depends on the region, surface type, and environmental conditions. As a general rule, it's recommended to repaint every 7-18 years.
2. **How do I get rid of mould before painting?**: To get rid of mould, use a solution of water and bleach to scrub the affected area. Rinse thoroughly and allow the surface to dry before painting.
3. **Can I paint fibrous cement cladding myself?**: While it's possible to paint fibrous cement cladding yourself, it's essential to take necessary safety precautions, especially if the cladding contains asbestos. Consider hiring a professional painter who has experience with fibrous cement surfaces.
4. **What's the best exterior paint for NZ conditions?**: The best exterior paint for NZ conditions is a water-based paint that offers flexibility, durability, and breathability. Resene, Dulux, and Wattyl are reputable brands that offer high-quality exterior paint systems.
5. **Do painters supply the paint or do I?**: It depends on the agreement you have with the painter. Some painters may supply the paint as part of their service, while others may require you to purchase the paint separately.
