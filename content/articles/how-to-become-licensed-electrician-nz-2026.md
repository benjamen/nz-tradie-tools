## EWRB Licensing Explained
The EWRB offers several types of licenses for electricians, each with its own set of responsibilities and requirements. The main types of licenses are:

* **Electrical Worker**: This is the entry-level license, which allows you to work under the supervision of a licensed electrician.
* **Craftsman Electrician**: This license requires a minimum of 2 years of experience as an electrical worker and allows you to work independently on electrical installations.
* **Certifying Electrician**: This is the highest level of license, which requires a minimum of 5 years of experience as an electrical worker and allows you to certify electrical installations.
* **Electrical Inspector**: This license requires a minimum of 5 years of experience as a certifying electrician and allows you to inspect electrical installations.

Each type of license has its own set of requirements and responsibilities, and electricians must meet the necessary standards to progress to the next level. The EWRB is responsible for ensuring that all licensed electricians meet the required standards, and it provides a range of resources and support to help electricians achieve their goals.

## Step-by-Step Path to Becoming a Licensed Electrician
To become a licensed electrician in NZ, you must follow a step-by-step process:

### Step 1: Find an Apprenticeship
The first step is to find an apprenticeship with a registered training provider, such as the Building and Construction Industry Training Organisation (BCITO) or a company-sponsored apprenticeship. BCITO apprenticeships are available through a range of providers, including polytechnics and private training establishments. Company-sponsored apprenticeships, on the other hand, are offered by employers who are willing to take on an apprentice and provide on-the-job training.

### Step 2: Complete the 4-Year Apprenticeship
The apprenticeship typically lasts for 4 years, during which time you will complete a range of unit standards and practical hours. The apprenticeship is designed to provide you with the necessary skills and knowledge to become a competent electrician. You will work under the supervision of a licensed electrician and complete a range of tasks, including electrical installations, maintenance, and repairs.

### Step 3: Sit and Pass the Registration Exam
After completing your apprenticeship, you will be eligible to sit the registration exam, which is administered by the EWRB. The exam tests your knowledge and skills in areas such as electrical theory, safety, and installation practices. The pass rate for the exam is around 65%, and you will need to achieve a minimum of 70% to pass.

### Step 4: Apply for Electrical Worker Registration
Once you have passed the registration exam, you can apply for electrical worker registration, which costs between $250-$400. This registration allows you to work as an electrical worker under the supervision of a licensed electrician.

### Step 5: Work Toward Certifying Electrician Licence
To become a certifying electrician, you will need to complete an additional 3 years of experience as an electrical worker and pass a range of exams. This will require you to demonstrate your competence in areas such as electrical design, installation, and testing.
