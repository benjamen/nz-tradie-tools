## EWRB Licensing Explained
The EWRB offers several types of licences for electrical workers, each with its own set of requirements and responsibilities. The four main types of licences are:

* Electrical Worker: This licence allows the holder to perform electrical work under the supervision of a certifying electrician.
* Craftsman Electrician: This licence is no longer issued, but existing holders can continue to work as electricians.
* Certifying Electrician: This licence allows the holder to perform electrical work without supervision and to certify their own work.
* Electrical Inspector: This licence allows the holder to inspect electrical installations and issue certificates of compliance.

Each type of licence has its own set of requirements, including the completion of an apprenticeship, passing a registration exam, and paying the required fees. The EWRB also requires licence holders to complete ongoing professional development to maintain their licence.

For example, a certifying electrician can work independently and certify their own work, while an electrical worker must work under the supervision of a certifying electrician. The EWRB also provides a list of approved training providers, such as the BCITO, which can help individuals obtain the necessary training and qualifications.

## Step-by-Step Path to Becoming a Licensed Electrician
To become a licensed electrician in NZ, you will need to follow these steps:

1. **Find an apprenticeship**: You can find an apprenticeship through the BCITO or by approaching a company directly. The BCITO is a government-funded organisation that provides apprenticeships in the building and construction industry.
2. **Complete the 4-year apprenticeship**: The apprenticeship will include a combination of on-the-job training and block courses. You will need to complete a minimum of 8,000 hours of practical work and achieve the required unit standards.
3. **Sit and pass the Registration exam**: The registration exam is administered by the EWRB and consists of a written and practical component. The pass rate for the exam is around 65%.
4. **Apply for Electrical Worker registration**: Once you have passed the registration exam, you can apply for an Electrical Worker registration. The registration fee is between $250 and $400.
5. **Work toward Certifying Electrician licence**: To become a certifying electrician, you will need to complete an additional 3 years of work experience and pass a series of exams.

For example, an individual can start by finding an apprenticeship through the BCITO, which can provide them with the necessary training and qualifications. After completing the apprenticeship, they can sit and pass the registration exam, and then apply for an Electrical Worker registration.
