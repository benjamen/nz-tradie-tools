## Common Heat Pump Faults in NZ Homes

### Not Heating or Cooling Adequately
**Possible causes:**
- Dirty filters (DIY fix — clean monthly)
- Low refrigerant (gas leak — requires licensed technician)
- Dirty outdoor coil (service clean required)
- Fault with reversing valve (refrigerant circuit)
- Undersized unit for the space

**DIY check first:** Clean or replace the filters — a blocked filter is the most common cause of reduced performance. Many NZ homeowners never clean their filters; a clogged filter can reduce output by 25–40%.

### Error Codes on the Display
Most modern heat pumps display fault codes when a problem is detected. Common codes relate to:
- Communication errors between indoor and outdoor units
- Temperature sensor failures
- Fan motor faults
- Refrigerant pressure issues

Don't ignore error codes — they often indicate an early-stage fault that's cheaper to fix than a failure. Note the code and share it with your heat pump technician when calling.

### Leaking Water Inside
**Indoor unit dripping water:** Usually a blocked condensate drain. Condensation from the indoor unit drips into a drain pan and out through a drain line — if this blocks (algae, debris), water overflows inside.

**Fix:** A technician can clear the drain line — typically part of a service visit. Cost: included in service, or $80–$150 if a separate call-out.

### Noisy Operation
- **Rattling:** Loose panel or foreign object in outdoor unit (leaf, debris)
- **Grinding/scraping:** Fan blade damage or bearing wear
- **Gurgling/bubbling:** Normal refrigerant sounds on startup — usually not a problem
- **Loud compressor:** May indicate refrigerant issues or compressor wear

### Heat Pump Not Turning On
- Check power at the wall (isolator switch, circuit breaker)
- Check remote battery
- Wait 3–5 minutes after a power cut — many units have a restart delay
- Check for error code display

If none of these resolve it: a technician fault diagnosis is needed.

## Refrigerant (Gas) — What You Need to Know

NZ heat pumps use refrigerant gases (R32 is the modern standard; older units use R410A or R22). Refrigerant work is **EPA-regulated** — only a person with an EPA refrigerant handling licence can legally purchase or handle refrigerant in NZ.

**Signs of a refrigerant leak:**
- Heat pump blowing lukewarm air despite running (not heating/cooling effectively)
- Ice forming on the outdoor unit
- Hissing sound from the outdoor unit
- Higher electricity bills with same usage

A refrigerant top-up without finding and fixing the leak is a short-term fix — the refrigerant will leak again. A good technician will find the leak, repair it, and then recharge the system.

**R22 refrigerant:** Older heat pumps (pre-2010) may use R22 refrigerant, which is being phased out globally. R22 is now expensive and increasingly hard to source in NZ — if your R22 system has a refrigerant leak, replacement is often more cost-effective than repair.

## When to Repair vs Replace

| Scenario | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Unit under 8 years old, minor fault | Repair — good value |
| Unit 8–12 years old, major fault (compressor) | Compare repair cost vs new unit price carefully |
| Unit 12+ years old, any significant repair | Replace — unit is past mid-life |
| R22 refrigerant system with leak | Replace — R22 is being phased out |
| Multiple faults in 2 years | Replace — reliability declining |
| Repair cost > 50% of new unit price | Replace |

A new 2.5kW heat pump (small room) costs $1,200–$2,000 installed. A new 5kW (lounge) costs $2,000–$3,500 installed. If a repair quote is approaching 50% of a replacement cost, replacement often makes more sense — particularly on older units.

## Annual Servicing — Worth Doing

An annual service extends heat pump life, maintains efficiency, and catches early faults before they become expensive:
- Filter clean (or replacement)
- Indoor and outdoor coil clean
- Refrigerant pressure check
- Electrical connection check
- Drain line clear

**Cost:** $120–$250 per unit. For a home with 2–3 heat pumps, service contracts are often available at a discount.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does it cost to service a heat pump in NZ?**
Annual service (clean, check, refrigerant pressure check): $120–$250 per unit. Service contracts for 2–3 units: $300–$500/year. Regular servicing extends life and maintains efficiency — worth doing annually.

**Why is my heat pump not heating properly in winter?**
The most common causes: dirty filters (clean first), low refrigerant, or a dirty outdoor coil. Check and clean your filters — this is a DIY task. If filters are clean and the unit is still underperforming, call a heat pump technician for a diagnosis. Low refrigerant (a gas leak) is the next most common cause.

**How long do heat pumps last in NZ?**
Typical life: 12–18 years. Major brands (Mitsubishi, Daikin, Fujitsu) tend to have the longest service lives and best parts availability. Regular annual servicing extends life. Budget for replacement after 15 years.

**Does my heat pump need a gas recharge in NZ?**
Heat pumps are sealed systems — they should NOT need regular gas top-ups. If refrigerant is low, there is a leak that needs to be found and repaired. Be wary of technicians who offer a "top-up" without finding the leak — the refrigerant will simply leak out again.
