## Introduction
Home renovation budgets in New Zealand often blow out, leaving homeowners stressed and financially strained. According to the Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment (MBIE), the three main causes of budget blowouts are inadequate planning, unforeseen site costs, and scope creep. This comprehensive guide aims to help homeowners plan their renovation costs realistically, avoiding the common pitfalls that lead to budget blowouts. We will cover the true cost of a renovation, indicative costs per room type, and provide tips on how to get an accurate budget, control costs, and manage contingencies.

## The True Cost of a Renovation
The cost of a renovation can be broken down into several components, including construction costs, council and consent fees, designer and architect fees, engineer fees, and contingency funds. Construction costs, which include labour and materials, typically account for 70-80% of the total budget. Council and consent fees can range from 2-8% of the total budget, while designer and architect fees can range from 5-12% of the construction cost. Engineer fees are typically around 1-3% of the construction cost. It's also essential to consider temporary accommodation costs if you need to move out during the renovation. Finally, don't forget to factor in the 15% Goods and Services Tax (GST) on all costs.

| Component | Percentage of Total Budget |
| --- | --- |
| Construction Costs | 70-80% |
| Council and Consent Fees | 2-8% |
| Designer and Architect Fees | 5-12% |
| Engineer Fees | 1-3% |
| Contingency Funds | 15-20% |
| Temporary Accommodation | variable |
| GST | 15% |

## What Drives Cost Variation in NZ
The cost of a renovation can vary significantly depending on several factors, including:

* Region: Auckland tends to have a 15-25% premium compared to other regions
* Site access: Difficult site access can increase costs
* Existing condition: The condition of the existing building can impact costs
* Finish level: The level of finish can significantly impact costs
* Plumbing and electrical moves: Moving plumbing and electrical services can add to the cost

It's essential to consider these factors when planning your renovation budget.

## How to Get an Accurate Budget
To get an accurate budget, it's crucial to start with a concept design and then get quotes from at least three builders on the same scope. Don't go to builders with vague ideas, as they will likely provide vague prices. Consider hiring a Quantity Surveyor (QS) for larger projects, as their report can provide a detailed breakdown of costs. The cost of a QS report can range from $2,000 to $8,000, but it's money well spent.

Using job management and invoicing tools like Xero, Fergus, or Tradify can also help you stay on top of your budget and track expenses.

## The Contingency — Non-Negotiable
A contingency fund is essential for any renovation project in New Zealand. With a minimum of 15% of the total budget, it's crucial to factor in unexpected costs, such as:

* Hidden damage: Asbestos, leaky plumbing, rotten framing, and undersized wiring are common issues in older homes
* Scope creep: Changes to the original scope can add to the cost

A real example of a Christchurch bathroom renovation that found black mould and rotten wall framing behind the tiles, resulting in an extra $12,000 not budgeted for, highlights the importance of a contingency fund.

## Finance Options
There are several finance options available for home renovations, including:

* Top-up mortgage: The cheapest option, but you'll need equity in your home
* Home renovation loan: No equity needed, but interest rates may be higher
* Credit cards: Expensive, but can provide fast access to funds
* Construction loan: Suitable for larger projects, but requires a detailed project plan and budget

It's essential to consider your finance options carefully and choose the one that best suits your needs.

## How to Control Costs
To control costs, it's crucial to:

* Choose a fixed-price contract or cost-plus contract, depending on your needs
* Manage variations carefully, as they can add to the cost
* Design before you demo, to avoid in-build changes
* Avoid making changes during the construction phase

Using tools like Fergus or Tradify can help you manage your project and track expenses, ensuring you stay within budget.

## FAQ
Here are some frequently asked questions about home renovation budgets:

* Q: How much contingency should I allow for a renovation?
A: A minimum of 15% of the total budget.
* Q: Should I get a fixed price or cost-plus contract?
A: It depends on your needs and the complexity of the project. Fixed-price contracts provide more certainty, while cost-plus contracts can be more flexible.
* Q: Can I save money by being my own project manager?
A: While it's possible to save money, being your own project manager can be time-consuming and may not always result in cost savings.
* Q: How long does a full house reno take?
A: The duration of a full house renovation can vary significantly, depending on the scope and complexity of the project. It's essential to plan carefully and allow for contingencies.
