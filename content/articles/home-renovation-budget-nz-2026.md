## Introduction
Home renovation budgets in New Zealand often blow out, leaving homeowners stressed and financially strained. According to the Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment (MBIE), the three main causes of budget blowouts are inadequate planning, unforeseen site costs, and scope creep. As a homeowner, it's essential to understand the true costs involved in a renovation and plan your budget realistically. This guide will walk you through the key components of a renovation budget, provide indicative costs for different room types, and offer tips on how to control costs and manage your project effectively. By the end of this article, you'll be well-equipped to plan your home renovation budget and avoid costly surprises.

## The True Cost of a Renovation
A typical home renovation budget in New Zealand consists of several components, including construction costs, council and consent fees, designer and architect fees, engineer fees, contingency funds, and temporary accommodation costs if necessary. The construction cost, which includes labour and materials, typically accounts for 70-80% of the total budget. Council and consent fees can range from 2-8%, while designer and architect fees can be between 5-12% of the construction cost. Engineer fees usually fall within the 1-3% range. It's also essential to include a contingency fund of at least 15-20% to cover unexpected expenses, such as hidden damage or scope creep. Additionally, you'll need to factor in the cost of temporary accommodation if you need to move out during the renovation. Don't forget to include GST (15%) on all costs.

Here's a rough breakdown of the typical costs involved in a home renovation:

* Construction costs (labour + materials): 70-80%
* Council/consent fees: 2-8%
* Designer/architect fees: 5-12% of construction cost
* Engineer fees: 1-3%
* Contingency: 15-20%
* Temporary accommodation (if necessary): variable
* GST: 15% on all costs

The Building and Construction Industry Training Organisation (BCITO) recommends that homeowners work with a registered builder and obtain multiple quotes to ensure they're getting a fair price. It's also crucial to check if your builder is registered with the Electrical Workers Registration Board (EWRB) and Master Plumbers, if applicable.

## What Drives Cost Variation in NZ
The cost of a home renovation in New Zealand can vary significantly depending on several factors, including:

* Region: Auckland tends to have a 15-25% premium compared to other regions
* Site access: Difficult site access can increase costs
* Existing condition: The condition of the existing building can impact the cost of the renovation
* Finish level: The level of finish, including materials and fixtures, can significantly impact the cost
* Plumbing and electrical work: Moving plumbing and electrical components can add to the cost

It's essential to consider these factors when planning your renovation budget to ensure you're getting an accurate estimate.

## How to Get an Accurate Budget
To get an accurate budget for your home renovation, it's recommended that you:

* Develop a concept design first
* Get three builder quotes on the same scope
* Avoid going to builders with vague ideas, as they'll likely provide vague prices
* Consider hiring a Quantity Surveyor (QS) for larger projects, as their report can provide a detailed breakdown of costs ($2,000-$8,000 for a QS report is money well spent)

## The Contingency — Non-Negotiable
A contingency fund is essential for any home renovation project in New Zealand. With a minimum of 15% recommended, this fund will help cover unexpected expenses, such as hidden damage or scope creep. Old homes, in particular, can have asbestos, leaky plumbing, rotten framing, and undersized wiring, which can lead to costly surprises.

A real example of a Christchurch bathroom renovation found black mould and rotten wall framing behind the tiles, resulting in an extra $12,000 not accounted for in the budget. This highlights the importance of including a contingency fund in your renovation budget.

## Finance Options
There are several finance options available for home renovations in New Zealand, including:

* Top-up mortgage: The cheapest option, but you'll need equity in your home
* Home renovation loan: No equity needed, but interest rates may be higher
* Credit cards: Expensive, but can provide fast access to funds
* Construction loan: Suitable for larger projects, but often requires a higher deposit

It's essential to discuss your finance options with a financial advisor or lender to determine the best approach for your situation.

## How to Control Costs
To control costs during your home renovation, consider the following:

* Fixed-price contract vs cost-plus: Each has its risks, so it's essential to understand the implications
* Variation management: Establish a clear process for managing changes to the scope
* Design before you demo: Avoid making changes during the construction phase
* Avoid in-build changes: Try to finalize your design before construction begins

## FAQ
Here are some frequently asked questions about home renovation budgets in New Zealand:

* Q: How much contingency should I allow for a renovation?
A: A minimum of 15% is recommended, but this can vary depending on the project scope and complexity.
* Q: Should I get a fixed price or cost-plus contract?
A: It depends on the project scope and your risk tolerance. Fixed-price contracts can provide certainty, but cost-plus contracts can be more flexible.
* Q: Can I save money by being my own project manager?
A: While it's possible to save money, being your own project manager can be time-consuming and may not always result in cost savings.
* Q: How long does a full house renovation take?
A: The duration of a full house renovation can vary significantly, but it's typically several months to a year or more.
