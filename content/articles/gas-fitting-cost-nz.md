## Why All Gas Work Must Be Licensed

Under the Gas Act 1992 and Gas (Safety and Measurement) Regulations 2010, **all gasfitting work** must be carried out by a licensed gasfitter registered with the Plumbers, Gasfitters and Drainlayers Board (PGDB).

This includes:
- Installing, servicing, or modifying any gas appliance connected to fixed pipework
- Running or modifying gas supply pipework
- Connecting or disconnecting gas appliances
- Commissioning gas systems

**Check PGDB registration** before hiring: [pgdb.co.nz](https://www.pgdb.co.nz) — enter the tradesperson's name. Takes 30 seconds.

**Certificate of Compliance (COC):** Every piece of gasfitting work must be certified with a COC. Keep all COCs — they prove the work is safe and are required if you sell the property or make an insurance claim involving gas.

**Gas leaks are emergencies.** If you smell gas: don't operate electrical switches, don't use a phone near the leak, ventilate the space, shut off the gas at the meter or bottle, and call a licensed gasfitter or your gas supplier.

| | Natural gas (reticulated) | LPG (bottled) |
|---|---|---|
| Availability | Main centres (Auckland, Wellington, Christchurch) — street-by-street | Nationwide, anywhere |
| Supply | Continuous, piped from street | Delivered in 45kg bottles |
| Connection cost | $1,500–$5,000 (new connection from street) | Lower — just a regulator and bottle |

**Check natural gas availability:** Contact Vector (Auckland) or First Gas (other NZ centres) to confirm if natural gas is available at your street address before assuming it is.

## Common Gas Jobs Explained

### Gas Hob Installation
Installing a gas hob where a gas point already exists (e.g. replacing an old gas hob): relatively simple — the gasfitter connects the appliance, checks for leaks, commissions, and issues a COC. Allow $180–$400.

Installing a new gas hob where no gas connection exists (converting from electric): requires running a new gas line from the meter, which involves more pipework and cost. Allow $600–$1,500+ depending on distance from meter.

### Gas Hot Water
Continuous flow (instant) gas hot water heaters are popular in NZ — no storage cylinder, hot water on demand. Leading brands: Rinnai, Rheem, Bosch.

**Replacement (existing gas HW system):** $1,500–$3,000 — gasfitter disconnects old unit, installs new, connects pipework and flue, commissions and COC.

**New installation (converting from electric):** $2,500–$6,000 — requires new gas pipework run to HW location, flue penetration through exterior wall, and commissioning.

**Gas vs heat pump hot water (2026):** Gas instant HW costs roughly 15–25c/kWh equivalent at current gas prices. A heat pump HW system costs 8–12c/kWh equivalent using electricity. For new installs with no existing gas, heat pump HW is now often the better economic choice.

### Gas Fireplaces and Log Fires
Gas log fires and fireplaces require:
- A gas supply point in the right location
- Flue penetration through wall or ceiling (for balanced-flue models) or existing chimney connection
- Commissioning and safety test

Balanced-flue gas fires (most common in NZ) don't require an existing chimney — the flue goes directly through an exterior wall. Allow $1,500–$4,000 fully installed.

### Gas BBQ Points
An outdoor gas point for a BBQ or outdoor kitchen is a popular addition in NZ. A gasfitter runs a line from the interior gas supply to an outdoor point with a capped bayonet fitting. Allow $400–$900 depending on pipe run length and difficulty.

## Finding a Licensed Gasfitter

- Verify PGDB registration at pgdb.co.nz
- Many plumbers also hold gasfitter licences — one trade, both COCs
- For new installations, get 2–3 quotes
- Ask whether they supply the appliance or if you should source separately (usually cheaper to source yourself through a retailer)

**Questions to ask:**
1. Are you PGDB licensed as a gasfitter? What is your registration number?
2. Will I receive a COC on completion?
3. Is the appliance supply included in your quote or separate?
4. Do I need natural gas availability confirmed before proceeding?

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does a gasfitter cost in NZ?**
Hourly rate: $95–$160/hr. Call-out fee: $100–$220. Gas hob installation (existing connection): $180–$400. Gas continuous flow hot water installation: $1,500–$3,500. After-hours callouts are 1.5–2× standard rates.

**Can I install a gas appliance myself in NZ?**
No. All gasfitting work must be done by a PGDB-licensed gasfitter. This includes connecting any gas appliance to fixed pipework. Even plugging in an appliance with a flexible hose to a bayonet fitting is regulated. Unlicensed gas work is illegal and can void your home insurance.

**How do I know if natural gas is available at my address?**
Contact Vector (Auckland) or First Gas (other centres). Natural gas availability is street-by-street — your neighbour may have it but your street may not. Check before committing to gas appliances.

**What is a COC for gas work?**
A Certificate of Compliance (COC) is issued by your licensed gasfitter after completing gasfitting work. It certifies the work is safe and compliant. Keep all COCs — you'll need them when selling your property and they're essential for insurance claims involving gas incidents.

**Is gas or electric cheaper in NZ in 2026?**
For cooking: gas is generally preferred for performance (instant heat control) but electricity (induction) is becoming comparable in cost and significantly surpasses gas in safety and air quality. For hot water: heat pump hot water is now typically cheaper to run than gas instant at 2026 energy prices. For space heating: heat pumps are consistently cheaper per kWh than gas heating.
