## Wellington Plumbing Context

### Wellington's Housing Stock
Wellington has a large number of older homes — villas, bungalows, and early 20th-century workers' cottages in Newtown, Berhampore, Thorndon, and Island Bay. These homes often have:

- **Galvanised steel pipes:** Pre-1960s Wellington homes commonly have galvanised water supply pipes that corrode from inside over time, reducing flow and eventually failing. Symptoms: low pressure, brown water, frequent leaks.
- **Old copper systems:** 1960s–1980s copper can develop pinhole leaks, especially where soil is corrosive. Wellington's clay soils can be aggressive to buried copper.
- **Clay drain lines:** Older clay earthenware drain pipes are prone to root intrusion (Wellington sections often have large trees) and cracking.

A CCTV drain inspection is worthwhile for any Wellington home built before 1980 when buying or investigating persistent drain issues.

### Wellington's Water Pressure
Wellington's hilly topography means water pressure varies dramatically by location. Elevated suburbs (Karori, Wadestown, Kelburn, Ngaio) may have lower pressure or pressure that fluctuates. Low-lying suburbs (Petone, Lower Hutt flat) can have very high pressure that stresses pipes and appliances.

**High water pressure:** If your Wellington property has high incoming pressure (above 500kPa), a pressure-reducing valve (PRV) is recommended — it protects washing machines, hot water cylinders, and reduces pipe stress. A plumber can test and install.

### Earthquake Resilience
Wellington sits astride the Wellington Fault — seismic resilience matters for plumbing. Flexible connections on hot water cylinders, earthquake straps on cylinders, and understanding of your toby (water shutoff valve) location are important for Wellington homeowners.

**Know where your toby is:** The outdoor toby valve shuts off your water supply from the street. In older Wellington properties it can be overgrown or seized — find it before you need it in an emergency, and have a plumber free it up if it won't turn.

### Wind and Salt Air
Wellington's exposed coastal suburbs (Seatoun, Eastbourne, Breaker Bay, Owhiro Bay) experience salt air that accelerates corrosion on outdoor fixtures, hot water cylinders, and exposed pipework. Stainless steel or plastic outdoor fittings outperform standard chrome in these locations.

## Finding a Reliable Wellington Plumber

**Verify PGDB registration:** All NZ plumbers must be registered with the Plumbers, Gasfitters and Drainlayers Board (PGDB). Check at [pgdb.co.nz](https://www.pgdb.co.nz) — takes 30 seconds. Any work on drain or water supply pipes must be done by a registered plumber.

**Certificate of Compliance (COC):** A COC must be issued for all prescribed plumbing work. Keep these — they're needed if you sell the property and for insurance claims.

**Questions to ask:**
1. Are you registered with the PGDB? What is your registration number?
2. Will I receive a Certificate of Compliance?
3. What are your call-out and hourly rates?
4. Do you charge a minimum call-out?
5. Are you available for weekend emergencies and what is the after-hours rate?

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does a plumber cost in Wellington?**
Hourly rate: $95–$160/hr. Call-out fee: $100–$220. Emergency after-hours: $200–$400 minimum. Hot water cylinder replacement: $1,200–$2,500. Wellington rates are similar to Auckland — among the highest in NZ.

**How do I find a trustworthy plumber in Wellington?**
Check PGDB registration at pgdb.co.nz. Ask friends and neighbours for recommendations. Get 3 quotes for larger jobs. Good Wellington plumbing companies include those who are Master Plumbers members. Always ask for a COC on completion of work.

**Does Wellington have old pipes that need replacing?**
Many Wellington homes built before 1970 have galvanised water pipes that are at or past end of life. Signs: low water pressure throughout the house, discoloured water on first use, frequent small leaks. A whole-house repipe with copper or cross-linked polyethylene (PEX) costs $5,000–$15,000 but eliminates ongoing repair costs and insurance risk.

**What should I do about low water pressure in Wellington?**
Check: is it one fixture (blocked aerator — easy fix) or whole-house? Whole-house low pressure in elevated Wellington suburbs may be a supply issue — contact Wellington Water. It may also be galvanised pipe restriction (see above) or a faulty PRV. A plumber can diagnose with a pressure test.
