# NZ Tradie Tools — Content Strategy

**Generated**: 2026-05-29
**Site**: https://tradietools.nz

---

## Situation

The site is exceptionally well-covered on the tools side: 65 calculators, 47 trades × 20 cities, 28 templates, 118+ articles. The articles are strong on regulatory/compliance topics (ACC, building consent, consumer law) and software comparisons. Key gaps:

- No content on *becoming* a tradie — a massive, consistent search cluster
- No content on tradie income/salary — another large cluster with direct audience overlap
- No consumer-facing "how to hire a tradie" content — different ICP but high volume and earns trusted-source positioning
- No "starting a trade business NZ" pillar — connects directly to the templates and calculators
- No tradie insurance guide despite it being a near-universal decision for NZ tradies
- Tax content exists per-topic but no overarching "NZ tradie tax guide" hub

---

## Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Becoming a Tradie in NZ
**Why**: "how to become a [trade] NZ" and apprenticeship queries are consistently searched by young people and career changers. The audience overlaps directly with future users of the tools and calculators. Near-zero NZ-specific competition.

### Pillar 2: Tradie Income & Pricing
**Why**: "how much does a [trade] earn NZ" and "how much should I charge NZ" are the most searched financial questions in the trade space. The hourly rate calculator makes this a natural content→tool funnel.

### Pillar 3: Starting & Running a Trade Business
**Why**: The templates and job management content are isolated. A pillar article ("starting a trade business NZ") creates a hub that connects invoice templates, the quote builder, tax calculators, and job management software comparisons into one narrative.

### Pillar 4: Hiring a Tradie (Consumer Side)
**Why**: "how to hire a [trade] NZ" is high volume. These readers become the site's audience once they're tradespeople themselves — and building consumer trust creates a network effect (tradies get recommended by happy clients who've used the site).

### Pillar 5: Tradie Tax & Finance
**Why**: The site has 15+ individual tax/finance articles but no hub. An overarching "NZ tradie tax guide" brings them together, earns internal link authority, and positions the site as a trusted reference for EOFY queries.

## Content Format Standards

- All articles link to 2–3 relevant calculators (internal tool CTAs)
- All articles link to relevant trade pages (47 trade slugs = internal anchor opportunities)
- FAQPage schema on every article
- Quick-answer box in first 100 words
- Real 2026 NZ figures — IRD thresholds, NZ apprenticeship rates, MBIE wage data
- June articles timed to EOFY (NZ financial year ends 31 March — wrap-up content peaks May–June)
- CTA: relevant calculator or template download on every article
