What to Look for When Hiring a Plumber in NZ

  • Current registration with the PGD Board
  • Licensed for the specific work type (plumbing, gasfitting, or drainlaying)
  • Public liability insurance
  • Responds promptly to urgent callouts
  • Upfront pricing or clear hourly rate

Red Flags to Watch Out For

  • Can't provide registration number
  • Quotes suspiciously low for gas work
  • No insurance
  • Doesn't provide a written invoice

Typical Costs Across NZ

$90–$130/hr (higher for after-hours callouts)

Costs vary significantly by city — Auckland and Queenstown are typically the most expensive, while Invercargill and Dunedin are among the most affordable.

Licensing Requirements

Must be registered and licensed under the Plumbers, Gasfitters and Drainlayers Act 2006. Check the PGD Board register. Check the register →
