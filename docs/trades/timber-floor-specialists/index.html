What to Look for When Hiring a Timber Floor Specialist in NZ

  • Timber species and grade specification
  • Moisture testing of subfloor before installation
  • Acclimatisation period for solid timber
  • Coating system (oil, polyurethane, wax) specified
  • NZFA membership

Red Flags to Watch Out For

  • No moisture check before installation
  • Won't specify coating system
  • No acclimatisation period for solid timber
  • Unknown timber source or species

Typical Costs Across NZ

Solid hardwood (supply + install): $150–$280/m². Sanding and polishing: $25–$55/m²

Costs vary significantly by city — Auckland and Queenstown are typically the most expensive, while Invercargill and Dunedin are among the most affordable.

Licensing Requirements

No mandatory licensing. NZFA (NZ Flooring Association) membership is a quality indicator. Check the register →
