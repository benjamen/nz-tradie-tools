What to Look for When Hiring a Fencer in NZ

  • Post spacing and depth specification
  • H treatment grade for in-ground posts (H4 or H5)
  • Pool fence compliance certificate if applicable
  • Concrete footing detail
  • Proper capping and finishing

Red Flags to Watch Out For

  • H3 posts for in-ground use (must be H4/H5)
  • No concrete footings mentioned
  • Won't specify post depth
  • No pool fence compliance for pool enclosures

Typical Costs Across NZ

Timber paling fence: $150–$280/m installed. Colorsteel: $200–$350/m. Pool fence (glass): $300–$600/m

Costs vary significantly by city — Auckland and Queenstown are typically the most expensive, while Invercargill and Dunedin are among the most affordable.

Licensing Requirements

No mandatory licensing. Pool fencing must comply with the Fencing of Swimming Pools Act 1987. Check the register →
