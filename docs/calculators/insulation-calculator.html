NZ Insulation Requirements (H1 2023)

The NZ Building Code clause H1 (Energy Efficiency) was significantly updated in 2023, raising minimum R-values across all climate zones. All new builds and major renovations must comply.

Frequently Asked Questions

Measure your ceiling area in m². Add 10% for waste and overlaps. Standard NZ pink batts come in 580 mm or 430 mm widths to fit between joists at 600 mm or 450 mm centres respectively.

H1 5th edition requires R6.6 in ceiling for Auckland/Bay of Plenty and up to R9.6 for alpine zones. A 240 mm glasswool batt gives roughly R6.0, while polyester or higher-density glasswool can achieve R6.6+ in 220–240 mm.

In most NZ climates, a polythene vapour barrier on the ground (0.25 mm minimum) is recommended under the building to reduce ground moisture entering the subfloor space, particularly in zones with high rainfall.

Glasswool and polyester ceiling batts can be installed by homeowners in NZ without a licensed contractor. Underfloor insulation installation (particularly polyester wrap systems) is also DIY-friendly. Spray foam insulation must be installed by a licensed applicator.
