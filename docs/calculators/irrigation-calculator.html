Irrigation in New Zealand

A well-designed irrigation system saves water, time, and keeps your garden thriving through NZ's dry summers — especially in the East Coast, Canterbury, and Central Otago regions.

Water Use Considerations

  • NZ average summer lawn water need: 25–30mm/week
  • At 25mm/week, a 200m² lawn needs 5,000 litres per week
  • Smart controllers with soil moisture sensors can cut water use by 30–50%

Council Water Restrictions

Most NZ councils have seasonal water restriction bylaws — check before designing an irrigation system that relies on town supply. Water meters are now mandatory on all new NZ connections.

Frequently Asked Questions

Lawns need approximately 25 mm of water per week in warm weather. A 100 m² lawn needs 2,500 L/week. Multiply by your sprinkler flow rate to find run time. Vegetable gardens need 30–40 mm/week.

Typical residential irrigation uses 25 mm poly mainline for runs under 50 m, then 13–19 mm laterals to heads. For larger commercial or lifestyle blocks, 32–40 mm mains may be needed. Always size for pressure losses at design flow.

Large-scale irrigation (farming or commercial horticultural) may require a water permit under the RMA from your regional council. Residential garden irrigation generally does not require consent.

Early morning (4–7 am) is ideal — wind is low, evaporation is minimal, and foliage dries before evening reducing disease risk. Avoid midday irrigation as 30–40% of water can be lost to evaporation.
