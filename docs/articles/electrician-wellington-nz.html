Wellington's electricians are busy year-round — between the capital's large housing stock, growing EV charger demand, and an active commercial sector. Here's what electricians charge in Wellington in 2026.

Wellington Electrical Context

Older Housing Stock and Wiring

Wellington has a substantial stock of older homes — particularly pre-1970 villas and bungalows in Thorndon, Mt Victoria, Karori, Newtown, and Aro Valley. These properties often have:

Outdated wiring: Older rubber-insulated wiring (pre-1970s) degrades over time — the insulation becomes brittle and can crack, creating fire and electrocution risk. If your Wellington home hasn't had an electrical inspection recently, an EWRB electrician can check the wiring condition.

Fuse boards (not circuit breakers): Many older Wellington properties still have ceramic fuse holders rather than modern MCB switchboards. Upgrading to a modern switchboard with RCDs (residual current devices) improves safety significantly and is typically required if you're adding significant electrical load (EV charger, heat pump).

Aluminium wiring: Some 1960s–70s Wellington properties have aluminium wiring — which requires special consideration for any alterations. An electrician familiar with Wellington's older housing will know this.

EV Charger Installation — Wellington Growth Area

Wellington has one of NZ's highest EV uptake rates — driven by the government sector, high income demographics, and good public charging infrastructure reinforcing confidence in the technology.

Home EV charger demand in Wellington is high. Common installation scenarios: - Standard 7kW AC wallbox (most home charging): $1,200–$2,500 installed. Works on a standard 32A circuit — most Wellington homes can accommodate this without a switchboard upgrade. - 3-phase or 11kW+ installations: Less common for homes but increasing. May require a switchboard assessment and upgrade. - Apartment/unit charging: More complex — requires landlord/body corporate coordination, dedicated circuit from the switchboard, and often a smart charger with load management.

Grant: Waka Kotahi administered EV charger support programmes — check for current incentives when getting a quote.

Wind and Aerial/Aerial Connectivity

Wellington's notorious wind affects rooftop aerial and antenna installations — and periodically brings down lines or damages mast-mounted equipment. If you need aerial wiring work, ensure your electrician or aerial installer is familiar with Wellington's wind conditions and specifies appropriate fixings.

EWRB Registration — Non-Negotiable

All electrical work in NZ must be done by an EWRB-registered electrician. Verify at ewrb.govt.nz. All work must be accompanied by a Certificate of Compliance (COC) issued by the certifying electrician.

Never use an unregistered person for electrical work. Unregistered electrical work is illegal, and insurance will not cover damage or injury resulting from uncertified electrical installations.

Finding a Good Wellington Electrician

Wellington's electrician market is competitive — good companies are booked out 1–3 weeks. For non-urgent work, book ahead.

What to look for: - EWRB-registered (check the register online) - Written quote or job scope for larger works - Willing to issue COC on completion - Experience with Wellington's older housing stock if relevant - Local references

Questions to ask: 1. Are you EWRB-registered and will you issue a COC? 2. Do you have experience with older Wellington villas (pre-1970 wiring)? 3. Can you assess whether my switchboard needs upgrading before I add a heat pump / EV charger? 4. What's your call-out fee and hourly rate? 5. How far in advance are you booked?

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does an electrician cost in Wellington? Hourly rate: $100–$160/hr. Call-out fee: $100–$200. EV charger install: $1,200–$2,500. Switchboard upgrade: $1,800–$4,500. Wellington rates are in line with Auckland — among NZ's higher-priced electrician markets.

How much does it cost to install an EV charger in Wellington? A standard 7kW AC wallbox: $1,200–$2,500 installed. This includes the charger unit, a dedicated circuit, and labour. If your switchboard needs upgrading first: add $1,800–$3,500. Most Wellington homes can add a 7kW charger without a switchboard upgrade — your electrician will confirm during a site assessment.

Do I need an electrician to install a light fitting in Wellington? In NZ, any electrical work (including changing or adding fixed light fittings) must be done by a registered electrician unless it's classified as owner-occupier work (changing a bulb, connecting a plug). Installing a new light fitting or changing a fitting's wiring is restricted electrical work — always use a registered electrician.

What should I do if I have an electrical emergency in Wellington? For immediate risk (burning smell, sparks, visible damage): call your lines company (Wellington Electricity: 0800 800 632) to disconnect power to your property, then call an electrician. For after-hours faults with no immediate safety risk, most Wellington electrical companies have after-hours call-out service — expect a minimum charge of $200–$400.
