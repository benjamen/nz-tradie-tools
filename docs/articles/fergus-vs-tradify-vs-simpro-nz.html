Fergus vs Tradify vs Simpro: Which is Best for NZ Tradies?

Published 6 April 2026 · NZ Tradie Tools
FergusTradifySimprosoftwarecomparisonNZ

If you've been researching job management software for your NZ tradie business, you've probably come across Fergus, Tradify, and Simpro. They all do broadly the same thing — manage jobs, quotes, invoices, and timesheets — but they're built for very different business sizes.

This comparison cuts through the marketing to help you choose.

Quick Summary

Fergus Tradify Simpro

Fergus — The NZ Standard for Growing Trades

Fergus was built by a NZ plumber for NZ tradies. It shows. The workflow matches how Kiwi businesses actually operate, and the Xero integration is the best in the market.

Pricing (per user/month, + GST)

  • 1 user: $49
  • 2 users: $89
  • 3 users: $119
  • 4 users: $149
  • 5+ users: call for pricing

Strengths

Job management: Drag-and-drop job board, status tracking, client notes, photos — all excellent.

Quoting: Build professional quotes with your rate cards. Clients can accept online. Convert to invoice with one tap.

Timesheets: Field staff clock in/out on the app. Office staff see live job status. Payroll export to Xero is seamless.

Purchase orders: Send POs to suppliers directly from Fergus. Costs flow through to job profitability.

Customer portal: Clients can see job status, invoices, and documents. Reduces phone calls significantly.

Reporting: Job profitability reports show actual vs. quoted. Identify where you're making and losing money.

Weaknesses

  • Gets expensive with larger teams (per-user pricing adds up)
  • Some advanced features (like detailed inventory) are basic
  • Not ideal for complex multi-stage project management

Verdict

Best all-round option for NZ trade businesses with 2–15 staff. Worth paying the premium over Tradify if you have employees.

Tradify — Fast, Simple, Affordable

Tradify is the easiest option to get running. A sole trader can be operational in an afternoon. The focus is on simplicity — quotes, jobs, invoices, done.

Pricing (+ GST)

  • 1 user: $29/mo
  • 3 users: $59/mo
  • 5 users: $89/mo
  • 10 users: $149/mo
  • Unlimited: $199/mo

Strengths

Speed: The mobile app is genuinely fast. Quote on site, send while you're still with the client.

Learning curve: Minimal. Most tradies are up and running within a day.

Affordability: The cheapest serious option for sole traders and micro-businesses.

Customer scheduling: Basic scheduling board, good for day-to-day job management.

Xero integration: Solid — invoices sync automatically.

Weaknesses

  • Less powerful than Fergus for complex businesses
  • Purchase orders and inventory are basic
  • Reporting could be deeper
  • Not ideal for businesses with many field staff

Verdict

Perfect for sole traders or businesses with 2–4 staff who want to get organised without a steep learning curve.

Simpro — Enterprise Power for Larger Operations

Simpro is the heavy-duty option. If you've outgrown Fergus and need multi-site project management, complex job costing, and detailed WIP reporting, Simpro is worth the investment.

Pricing

Simpro doesn't publish public pricing. Expect: - Small businesses: $200–$400/month - Medium businesses: $400–$800/month - Enterprise: custom

Strengths

Project management: Multi-stage jobs, progress billing, retentions — handles complex construction projects.

WIP tracking: Real-time work-in-progress reporting across all jobs. Critical for larger businesses managing cash flow.

Job costing: Granular tracking of actual costs vs. estimates. Identify profitable and unprofitable work patterns.

Inventory: Built-in stock management for businesses that warehouse materials.

Service + project: Handles both high-volume service work and large project work.

Weaknesses

  • Complex to set up (expect 2–4 weeks implementation)
  • Expensive (justified for larger businesses, overkill for small ones)
  • Steeper learning curve for field staff

Verdict

Right for businesses with 10+ field staff doing complex work. Overkill for anyone smaller.

My Recommendation

Start here: - Sole trader → Tradify (sign up today, running tomorrow) - Growing business 2–10 staff → Fergus (the NZ standard) - Large operation 10+ → Simpro (plan a proper implementation)

All three offer free trials. Start with the one that fits your size, try it for 2 weeks on real jobs, and see how it feels.
