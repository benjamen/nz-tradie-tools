Waterblasting Cost NZ 2026 — House, Driveway and Deck Prices

Published 23 June 2026 · NZ Tradie Tools

Regular waterblasting removes mould, lichen, algae, and grime that build up on NZ homes in our wet, humid climate. Here's what waterblasting costs in NZ in 2026, what's worth paying a professional for, and what you can tackle yourself.

Waterblasting Cost NZ 2026

Service Typical cost
House exterior (single storey, weatherboard) $300–$600
House exterior (double storey) $500–$1,000
Driveway (concrete or asphalt, per 50m²) $150–$350
Deck (timber or composite, per 30m²) $150–$300
Roof cleaning (iron or tile, 3-bed house) $400–$900

Paths and paved areas (per 50m²) $100–$250
Full property clean (house + driveway + paths) $600–$1,500

All prices ex-GST. Auckland 15–20% above. Single-storey and easy-access properties at the lower end.

Why Waterblasting Matters in NZ

NZ's temperate, humid climate is ideal for biological growth — mould, mildew, algae, and lichen establish on almost every exterior surface within a few years. This affects:

Appearance: Black mould staining on weatherboards, green algae on driveways, lichen on roofs.

Longevity: Mould and lichen trap moisture against surfaces — accelerating timber decay, causing concrete to spall, and degrading paint bonds. A house that's waterblasted every 2–3 years before repainting will hold paint significantly longer.

Health: Mould on exterior walls can grow into wall cavities. In poorly ventilated homes, exterior mould growth near ventilation gaps can contribute to indoor air quality issues.

Before painting: Exterior painting always requires waterblasting first. Painting over mould, algae, or chalky old paint causes premature failure. A $400 waterblast before a $10,000 paint job is mandatory, not optional.

What's Included in a Professional Waterblast

A professional waterblasting service typically includes:

  • Pre-treatment (soft wash): Application of a biocide/mould killer (sodium hypochlorite solution) to kill mould, lichen, and algae before blasting. This is important — blasting without killing the growth first just spreads spores and doesn't prevent regrowth.
  • High-pressure washing: 1,500–3,500 PSI depending on surface (lower pressure for timber, higher for concrete and stone).
  • Rinse: Clean rinse to remove cleaning agents and loosened material.

DIY waterblasting (renting a pressure washer) skips the pre-treatment chemical step — which means mould comes back faster. Professional soft-wash systems use lower pressure and chemical action for longer-lasting results, especially on roofs.

Roof Cleaning

Roof waterblasting is increasingly popular in NZ — moss and lichen growth on iron and tile roofs:

  • Trap moisture, causing premature oxidation on iron
  • Work under tile edges, lifting tiles over time
  • Add weight to the roof structure
  • Look unsightly

Method: Professional roof cleaners use soft-wash techniques (low pressure + biocide) rather than high-pressure blasting, which can damage roof coatings. After treatment, most installers apply a moss inhibitor that prevents regrowth for 2–5 years.

Cost: $400–$900 for a standard 3-bedroom house. Worth doing every 3–5 years, and always before roof painting.

DIY Waterblasting vs Professional

Factor DIY rental Professional

Equipment quality Consumer/semi-pro Commercial (higher flow rate, purpose-built lances)
Chemical pre-treatment Usually skipped Included — biocide kills mould
Time 4–8 hours (inexperienced) 2–4 hours
Mould regrowth prevention Poor (no biocide) Better (biocide treatment)
Safe for soft surfaces Risk of damage (timber, paint) Lower risk with experience
2-storey / difficult access Ladder risk Proper ladders + experience

DIY makes sense for: Flat driveways and paths, small decks, easy single-storey areas you can reach from the ground.

Professional makes more sense for: House exterior with mould (the chemical pre-treatment is what does the work, not the pressure), roofs, 2-storey homes, or before painting.

How Often Should You Waterblast?

Surface Recommended frequency
House exterior (mould-prone) Every 2–3 years
House exterior (sheltered, less growth) Every 3–5 years
Driveway Every 1–3 years
Deck (before oiling) Every 1–2 years
Roof Every 3–5 years

Before painting (exterior or roof): always waterblast first regardless of when it was last done.

Questions to Ask Before Booking

  1. Do you include a chemical pre-treatment (biocide/soft wash) or just high pressure?
  2. What PSI do you use on timber vs concrete (lower for timber to avoid damage)?
  3. Is a moss inhibitor applied after roof cleaning?
  4. Do you work at heights safely (proper ladders, harness for steep roofs)?
  5. Is GST included in the quote?

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does waterblasting cost in NZ? House exterior (single storey): $300–$600. Driveway (50m²): $150–$350. Full property (house, driveway, paths): $600–$1,500. Roof cleaning (3-bed house): $400–$900. All ex-GST.

How often should I waterblast my house in NZ? Every 2–3 years for most NZ homes — our humid climate promotes mould and algae growth on most exterior surfaces. Before any exterior painting, always waterblast regardless of when it was last done.

Can I waterblast my own roof in NZ? Not recommended. Roofs are slippery when wet, and falls from roofs are a leading cause of serious injury and death in NZ. Professional roof cleaners have the right safety equipment and use low-pressure soft-wash techniques that won't damage roof coatings. Leave roofs to professionals.

Will waterblasting damage my weatherboards? At the wrong pressure, yes — high-pressure water can strip paint and dent soft timber. Professional operators use 1,000–1,800 PSI on painted timber, not the 3,000+ PSI used on concrete. Always confirm the operator's experience with your specific cladding type.

How long does waterblasting take? A single-storey house exterior: 2–4 hours for a professional team. A driveway: 1–2 hours. Full property clean (house + driveway + paths): 4–6 hours. Roof cleaning takes 2–3 hours. Most jobs are done in a single half-day visit.
