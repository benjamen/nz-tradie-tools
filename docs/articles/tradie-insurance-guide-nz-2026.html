Tradie Insurance Guide NZ 2026 — What You Need and What It Costs

Published 18 June 2026 · NZ Tradie Tools
tradie insurancepublic liabilityincome protectionNZ2026

Most NZ tradies are underinsured — not because they don't understand the risk, but because they've never sat down and worked out which policies they actually need. This guide cuts through the options.

The Non-Negotiables

Public Liability Insurance

Covers you if you damage a client's property or injure a third party while working. This is the one policy every tradie needs, no exceptions.

Scenario: You're drilling through a wall and hit a water pipe. The resulting flood damages $15,000 of client flooring and the downstairs neighbour's ceiling. Without public liability, that comes out of your pocket.

What it costs:

Trade Cover Annual premium
Electrician / plumber (sole trader) $2M $500–$900
Builder / carpenter (sole trader) $2M $700–$1,300
Roofer / scaffolder $2M $900–$1,500
Small company (3–5 staff) $5M $2,000–$4,000

Commercial contracts (body corporates, councils, large developers) often require $5M–$10M cover — confirm before you tender.

Statutory Liability

Covers fines and legal costs if WorkSafe NZ or another regulator takes action against you. Often bundled with public liability for $100–$300 more per year. Worth including — WorkSafe prosecutions can result in fines of $50,000–$300,000.

If You Have Tools Worth Protecting

Tool and Equipment Insurance

Covers theft, loss, or accidental damage to your tools and gear. The standard home contents policy won't cover trade tools at a work site.

Tool value Annual premium Typical excess
Up to $10,000 $300–$500 $250–$500
$10,000–$30,000 $500–$900 $350–$600
$30,000–$60,000 $800–$1,400 $500–$750

Check policy wording carefully: some exclude tools left in an unattended vehicle overnight, or require a certain level of security (deadlocks, alarm). Ute break-ins are common and that exclusion matters.

Income Protection — ACC Is Not Enough

ACC covers 80% of your pre-injury income if you're injured at work. That sounds reasonable until you understand the gaps:

  • ACC doesn't cover illness. A bad back from a disc problem — not injury-related — means zero income from ACC.
  • ACC doesn't cover the 20% gap. If you earn $100,000/yr and can't work, ACC pays $80,000. The other $20,000 comes from reserves or private insurance.
  • ACC doesn't cover the waiting period. Claims take time to process.

Private income protection covers you for both illness and injury, and can fill the gap between ACC payments and your full income. Cost varies significantly by age, trade, and waiting period:

Profile Monthly benefit Monthly premium
30yo electrician, $80k income $4,000 $80–$150
40yo builder, $100k income $5,000 $150–$280
50yo plumber, $90k income $4,500 $250–$450

Longer waiting periods (90 days instead of 14) reduce premiums significantly. If you have 3 months of reserves, a 90-day stand-down is fine.

If You Do Design, Advice, or Project Management

Professional Indemnity

Covers you if a client claims your professional advice or design caused them financial loss. Relevant for:

  • Design-and-build builders
  • LBPs providing site supervision services
  • Engineers or surveyors providing certified assessments

Cost: $500–$2,500/year depending on scope and turnover. Often required by your professional association (RMBF, Master Electricians, etc.).

Contract Works Insurance

Covers work in progress — materials on site, partially completed structures — against fire, storm, theft, or accidental damage. Required on most large commercial projects. Residential builders often carry it for new builds.

Cost varies by project value — typically 0.1–0.3% of contract value per project, or an annual blanket policy for $1,500–$5,000.

NZ Insurers Worth Knowing

Insurer Specialty
Trade Risk Specialist tradie insurer — online quotes, tradie-focused wording
NZI Large commercial, good for companies with staff
Vero Broad construction cover, contract works
AIG Professional indemnity and management liability
BrokerWeb Broker network, good for comparing multiple quotes

Get at least two quotes. Trade Risk is a good starting point for sole traders — their online quote tool is fast and their policies are written for tradies, not generic small business.

What Clients Can Ask For

Increasingly, homeowners booking premium work and principal contractors are asking to see: - Your public liability policy number and expiry - Your LBP number (for restricted building work) - Proof of current tool insurance for high-value equipment

Having these ready speeds up job approval and signals professionalism.
