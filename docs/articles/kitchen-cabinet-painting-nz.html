Kitchen Cabinet Painting Cost NZ 2026 — Spray Paint vs Brush, Costs and Results

Published 23 June 2026 · NZ Tradie Tools

Painting or spray-painting kitchen cabinets is a popular way to update a kitchen at a fraction of the cost of a full renovation. Here's what kitchen cabinet painting costs in NZ in 2026, what method produces the best result, and whether it's worth it for your kitchen.

Spray Painting vs Brush Painting — Which Is Better?

Spray Painting (Professional HVLP Spray)

Best result by far. A professional using a High-Volume Low-Pressure (HVLP) spray system applies thin, even coats with no brush marks, sag-free coverage, and a factory-like finish. This is the method used by professional cabinet painters and kitchen manufacturers.

Process: 1. Doors removed and taken to a spray booth (or in-situ masking of the entire kitchen) 2. Thorough clean and degrease (critical — any grease causes adhesion failure) 3. Light sand to key the surface 4. Primer coat (2K or waterborne primer) 5. Topcoat (1–2 coats, 2K polyurethane or premium waterborne) 6. Rehang and adjust

Result: Smooth, hard, durable finish virtually indistinguishable from factory-painted cabinets. Lasts 5–10+ years with normal use.

Cost: Higher than brush painting, but the result justifies it on a quality kitchen.

Brush and Roller Painting

A brush-and-roller repaint is substantially cheaper, but results vary:

  • On flat, profile-free cabinet doors: a skilled painter with the right products can achieve a good result — visible brush texture under raking light, but acceptable from normal viewing distance.
  • On routed or decorative door profiles: brush marks accumulate in grooves and look poor.
  • Durability is lower than spray-applied 2K coatings.

Best for: Budget update of a simple, flat-door kitchen where perfect finish isn't the priority.

What Paint to Use for Kitchen Cabinets

Kitchen cabinets are high-use surfaces — they must withstand: - Repeated contact (greasy hands, cleaning chemicals) - Humidity from cooking - UV from windows

Best options for NZ:

2K (two-pack) polyurethane: Extremely hard, solvent-resistant finish. Sprayed, not brushable. Requires proper respirator. Used by professional cabinet sprayers. Best durability — can last 10–15 years.

Waterborne alkyd (Resene Enamacryl, Dulux Aquanamel, Beckers Cabinet): Water-based with alkyd hardness. Can be brushed or sprayed. Good durability for a DIY-compatible product. Used by many professional painters who don't want to work with 2K solvents.

Chalk paint / fusion mineral paint: DIY-friendly, low prep, no primer needed — popular for furniture painting. Durability is lower than purpose-made cabinet paints; suitable for a low-budget refresh but not a premium result.

Avoid: Cheap wall paint on cabinets — it will scuff, mark, and peel within months in kitchen conditions.

Painting makes sense when: - The cabinet boxes and drawers are structurally sound - You want to change colour without a full reno cost - You're staging a house for sale - You're updating a rental property

Replacing makes more sense when: - Cabinets are structurally damaged, swollen, or poorly configured - You want to change the layout - You're doing a full kitchen renovation anyway

Finding a Cabinet Spray Painter in NZ

Not all painters do cabinet spray work. You want someone who: - Has a spray booth or the right masking setup for in-situ work - Uses appropriate primers and topcoats for cabinetry (not just wall paint) - Can show examples of previous cabinet spray work - Provides a written quote specifying the paint product and number of coats

Questions to ask: 1. What primer and topcoat product are you using? 2. Will you remove the doors for off-site spraying, or spray in-situ? 3. How many coats are included? 4. What preparation (cleaning, sanding) is included? 5. Can I see photos of previous kitchen cabinet spray projects?

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to spray paint kitchen cabinets in NZ? Professional spray paint: $1,500–$3,500 for a small kitchen (10–15 doors); $2,500–$5,500 for a medium kitchen (16–24 doors). These prices usually cover doors and drawer fronts only — painting frames and boxes adds 30–50%.

How long does painted kitchen cabinet finish last? Professional 2K polyurethane spray: 10–15 years with normal use. Professional waterborne alkyd spray: 5–10 years. Brush-applied waterborne enamel: 3–6 years. The kitchen's use intensity matters — a rental kitchen with heavy daily use will see more wear than a lightly-used holiday home kitchen.

Can I spray paint my own kitchen cabinets in NZ? DIY spraying kitchen cabinets is possible but requires: an HVLP spray gun ($200–$600), proper respirator and PPE, a clean spray environment (dust ruins the finish), and practice on scrap panels first. Most homeowners find the result disappointing vs a professional — kitchen cabinets are a high-visibility surface where finish quality really shows. DIY brush painting with a quality enamel is more accessible.

What colour should I paint my kitchen cabinets? Most popular NZ kitchen cabinet colours in 2026: white (Resene White, Dulux Lexicon Quarter), soft white/off-white (Resene Alabaster, Dulux Antique White USA), deep navy or teal (accent lowers), and warm greens (Resene Double Tea, Dulux Muted Sage). Two-tone (white uppers, darker lowers) is a popular update for existing kitchens.
