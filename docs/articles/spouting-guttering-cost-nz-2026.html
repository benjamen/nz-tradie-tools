Spouting & Guttering Cost NZ 2026 — Replacement Prices Per Metre

Updated June 2026 7 min read

Leaking or blocked spouting is one of the most common causes of water damage and foundation issues in NZ homes. Replacing old spouting is a high-ROI maintenance job. In 2026, expect to pay $60–$150 per metre for colorsteel quad gutter installed — with a full 3-bedroom home costing $2,500–$9,000 all up.

Signs Your Spouting Needs Replacing

  • Rust and holes — surface rust can be patched short-term but is usually a sign the whole run needs replacing
  • Sagging or pulling away from fascia — brackets have failed or fascia board is rotting
  • Overflowing in rain — blocked (clean first) or undersized for your roof area
  • Leaking joins — sealant failure at lap joins; can be resealed but usually indicates end of life
  • Water staining on exterior walls or foundations — sign of chronic overflow or leaking downpipes
  • Age over 25–30 years — proactive replacement prevents emergency repairs and water damage

FAQ — Spouting Costs NZ

How much does spouting replacement cost in NZ?

Colorsteel quad gutter costs $60–$120 per metre installed. A full 3-bedroom home typically costs $2,800–$7,500 for the spouting, plus $800–$2,500 for scaffolding if it's two-storey. Aluminium adds 20–30% to the spouting cost.

How long does spouting last in NZ?

Colorsteel: 20–35 years. Aluminium: 30–40 years. uPVC: 20–25 years. All types last longer with regular cleaning to prevent leaf and debris build-up. Coastal homes near salt air should budget for the lower end of these ranges.

Do I need scaffolding for spouting replacement?

Single-storey homes can usually be done from ladders — no scaffolding required. Two-storey homes require scaffolding or a boom lift for worker safety, adding $800–$2,000 to the project. A reputable contractor will always provide a safe work plan.

Can I replace spouting myself in NZ?

Spouting installation is not licensed or restricted work in NZ, so technically a homeowner can DIY it. However, working at height carries significant safety risks and poor installation leads to water damage. Most homeowners are better off using a professional for any work above single-storey.
