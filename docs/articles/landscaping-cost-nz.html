Landscaping Trades and Who Does What

A large landscaping project touches multiple trades — understanding who does what helps you get the right people quoting:

Landscape designer / architect: Creates the design — planting plans, layout, materials specification. Not all landscaping needs a designer, but for projects over $20,000, a design plan prevents costly mistakes and helps get accurate quotes.

Landscaper / landscape contractor: Installs the physical elements — earthworks, drainage, irrigation, paving, planting, lawn. The main contractor on most residential landscaping jobs.

Irrigation specialist: Designs and installs in-ground irrigation systems. Increasingly standard in new NZ sections and larger gardens.

Arborist: Tree work — pruning, removal, stump grinding. See Arborist Dunedin for more on arborist costs.

Fencing contractor: Boundary and garden fencing. See Fence Installation Cost NZ.

Deck builder: Outdoor decking and pergolas. See Deck Building Cost NZ.

Lawn Installation: Turf vs Seed

Turf (Instant Lawn)

Rolled turf is laid on prepared soil — you have an instant lawn the same day. Premium turf varieties (Sir Walter buffalo, Waiau ryegrass blend) are best suited for NZ conditions.

Cost: $25–$45/m² installed including site preparation, topsoil if needed, and turf supply. A 200m² lawn: $5,000–$9,000.

Best for: Front lawns, areas needing immediate use, or sections where a quick result matters for sale or rental.

Care: Water daily for 3–4 weeks while establishing. Don't mow until roots have taken (when you can't peel it up). First mow: 3–4 weeks after laying.

Seeding

Seed is broadcast over prepared soil. Takes 4–8 weeks to establish, longer to achieve a full sward. Cheaper than turf.

Cost: $8–$18/m² installed including preparation, seed, fertiliser, and straw mulch. A 200m² lawn: $1,600–$3,600.

Best for: Large areas, rear sections not immediately visible, patient homeowners, or where budget is the primary concern.

Limitation: Establishment period = no use and risk of weed invasion. Good soil preparation and weed control are critical.

All paving should have a proper compacted sub-base (AP20 or GAP20, 100mm compacted) and be laid to fall away from the house (minimum 1:100 fall). Poor sub-base preparation is the leading cause of paving failure in NZ.

Planting: What It Costs

Planting costs vary enormously by plant selection and quantity:

  • Ground cover / perennial planting (per m²): $30–$80/m²
  • Shrubs (small, 1–2L pot): $15–$50 each installed
  • Specimen shrubs (3–5L pot): $40–$120 each installed
  • Native grasses and flaxes: $20–$60 each
  • Small trees (25–45L pot): $150–$500 each installed
  • Mulch (per m²): $8–$20/m² (bark chip or wood mulch, 75mm depth)

Natives vs exotics: Native plantings (phormium, pittosporum, carex, coprosma) are increasingly popular — adapted to NZ conditions, require less water and maintenance once established, and attract native birds. They typically cost similar to or slightly less than equivalent exotic ornamental plants.

Finding a Good Landscaper

For design + build projects: Get a landscape designer to produce plans before seeking contractor quotes — this ensures all contractors quote the same scope and you're comparing like for like.

For straightforward work: A good landscaping company can design and build without a separate designer for most residential projects.

What to look for: - NZILA member (New Zealand Institute of Landscape Architects) for design-focused work - Photos of previous projects similar to yours - Ability to manage subcontractors (irrigation, fencing, decking) or clear referrals for these trades - References from recent residential clients in your area

Questions to ask: 1. Do you design and build, or design only? 2. Can you manage all trades (irrigation, fencing, decking) or do I need to coordinate separately? 3. What topsoil depth and quality do you include in lawn preparation? 4. What plant species are you specifying (ask for a plant list)? 5. What maintenance is required in the first year after planting? 6. Do you offer a plant replacement guarantee?

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does landscaping cost in NZ? A front garden refresh (new plants, mulch, edging): $2,000–$6,000. A full new lawn plus garden beds on a standard section: $8,000–$20,000. A complete outdoor transformation including paving, deck, lawn, and planting: $25,000–$80,000+. Auckland is 15–20% above these ranges.

Is it cheaper to lay turf or seed a lawn in NZ? Seed is significantly cheaper — $8–$18/m² vs $25–$45/m² for turf. The trade-off is time (4–8 weeks to establish vs instant lawn from turf) and risk (weed invasion during establishment). For back sections and large areas, seed is often the right choice. For front lawns or where an immediate result is needed, turf is worth the premium.

How much does an irrigation system cost in NZ? A residential in-ground irrigation system covering a typical 500–700m² section: $3,000–$8,000 installed, including controller, pop-up heads or drip lines, and backflow prevention. Larger properties or systems with multiple zones cost proportionally more.

Do I need a landscape designer for my project? For projects under $15,000–$20,000, most landscaping companies can design and build without a separate designer. For complex projects (large sections, multiple levels, significant construction elements), a landscape designer's fee ($1,500–$5,000) typically saves more than it costs by preventing expensive mistakes and ensuring you get what you actually want.

What plants grow well in NZ gardens? Climate varies enormously across NZ — from subtropical Northland to frosty Otago. Native plants (phormium, pittosporum, carex grasses, hebes, kowhai) are adapted to NZ conditions across most regions. For specific planting advice, a local landscape designer or nursery with regional knowledge is invaluable.
