Tilers Auckland 2026 — Rates, Lead Times and How to Find a Good Tiler

Published 23 June 2026 · NZ Tradie Tools

Auckland has a high demand for tilers — driven by ongoing new builds, bathroom renovations, and the city's large housing stock. Here's what tilers charge in Auckland in 2026, typical project costs, and how to find a reliable one.

Why Auckland Tiling Costs More

Labour rates: Auckland tiler day rates ($550–$850/day) are above the national average. High cost of living, parking, and travel time all feed into pricing.

Demand: Auckland's construction boom means tilers are busy year-round. Good tilers are often booked 4–8 weeks in advance — particularly for bathroom renovations.

Tile supply: Auckland has excellent access to tile suppliers (Tile Warehouse, Beaumont Tiles, Tiles Direct, Italian Tiles NZ) with wide ranges. However, imported large-format and specialty tiles can have 4–12 week lead times — factor this into your renovation schedule.

Traffic and travel: Auckland tilers lose real time to traffic — this affects day rate efficiency and can influence quoting.

Finding a Good Auckland Tiler

Auckland's tiling market has a wide quality range. Some things to look for:

Experience with your tile type: Large format tiles (600×600+) require a flatter substrate and more skill to lay without lippage. Mosaic tiles require more patience. Shower waterproofing requires specific knowledge of E3 Building Code requirements. Ask specifically about the tile type and application you need.

Waterproofing knowledge: For any wet area (shower, bathroom floor), confirm the tiler understands and follows NZ Building Code Clause E3. Ask which waterproofing system they use (Mapei, Ardex, Davco) and whether they use fabric tape at corners.

References: Ask for 2–3 recent jobs in Auckland you can contact or visit. A good tiler won't hesitate to provide these.

What to watch for: - Tilers who quote very low without seeing the job - Anyone who doesn't mention waterproofing for wet area work - Paying large deposits upfront (30% maximum before start is reasonable) - No fixed start date in the quote

Getting Accurate Quotes

Have ready: - Room dimensions (m²) - Tile you've selected (or photos of the style you want) - What substrate is there now (concrete slab, timber floor, existing tiles) - Whether wet area waterproofing is required - Any complex features: niches, feature walls, floor patterns

Questions to ask your Auckland tiler: 1. What waterproofing system do you use for showers? 2. How do you handle large format tiles — do you use a levelling system? 3. Is adhesive, grout, and silicone included in your quote? 4. What is your lead time to start? 5. Do you supply the tile or should I? 6. Is tile removal included if stripping existing tiles?

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do tilers charge in Auckland? Labour-only: $60–$140/m² depending on tile type and complexity. Shower tiling (which includes waterproofing): $90–$180/m² labour. A full bathroom tile job (25m², supply + labour, mid-range tiles): $4,500–$10,000. Auckland rates are 15–25% above the national average.

How long do I have to wait for a tiler in Auckland? Good Auckland tilers are typically booked 4–8 weeks ahead. Plan your bathroom renovation timeline to allow for this — tile lead times (especially imported tiles) can be 4–12 weeks on top of tiler availability.

Do I need to supply my own tiles in Auckland? Either arrangement works. Some tilers prefer to supply everything (full control of product quality and quantities). Others prefer you supply tiles. If you supply tiles, buy 10% extra for cuts and breakage — this is standard practice. Tilers will typically purchase adhesive, grout, and silicone unless you agree otherwise.

What is the best tile for an Auckland bathroom floor? Porcelain with R10 or R11 slip rating for bathroom floors (NZ Building Code requirement for wet areas). 300×300mm or 200×200mm format works well for bathroom floors — follows the floor fall and has good drainage between smaller tiles. Large format (600×600+) on a bathroom floor requires very precise levelling to avoid lippage and water pooling.

Can I tile over existing tiles in Auckland? Sometimes — if the existing tiles are firmly bonded, flat, and the substrate can handle the extra weight. Adds 5–10mm to floor height (affects door clearance, thresholds). For shower tiles: always remove existing tiles before waterproofing — you cannot apply a waterproofing membrane over existing tiles.
