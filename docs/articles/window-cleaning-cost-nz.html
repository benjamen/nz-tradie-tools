Window Cleaning Cost NZ 2026 — Residential and Commercial Prices

Published 23 June 2026 · NZ Tradie Tools
window cleaningwindow cleanercleaningcostsNZ2026

Clean windows dramatically improve how a home looks and let in more light — and in NZ's coastal and urban environments, windows need regular attention. Here's what window cleaning costs in NZ in 2026 and what's involved.

Window Cleaning Cost NZ 2026

Service Typical cost

Large home (4–5 bed, inside + outside) $250–$500

All prices GST inclusive. Auckland 15–20% above. Two-storey, difficult access, and heavily soiled windows add cost.

What Affects Window Cleaning Cost

Number of windows: Most cleaners count individual panes or windows — more windows means more cost. A standard 3-bedroom NZ home typically has 12–20 windows.

Inside vs outside: Outside-only cleaning (most common regular service) is faster and cheaper. Inside-out (full clean) takes longer and is often done annually or before moving/selling.

Storey height: Ground-floor windows — standard pricing. Second-storey exterior — ladder required, adds time and risk premium. High-rise or multi-storey commercial — rope access or elevated work platform (EWP) required, significantly more expensive.

Condition: Windows with heavy hard water calcium deposits, paint splatter, or building material residue take much longer to clean — many cleaners charge a first-time or restoration rate for neglected windows.

Frame cleaning: Some quotes include cleaning the frame and sill; others are glass only. Confirm what's included.

Window Cleaning Methods

Traditional (Squeegee and Mop)

The classic method — a mop (applicator) soaked in soapy water scrubs the glass, a rubber squeegee wipes it clean. Highly effective for standard windows.

Best for: Ground-floor and accessible first-floor windows where the cleaner can reach comfortably.

Water-Fed Pole

A telescoping pole (up to 15–20m) with a brush head fed by purified water. Purified water (with minerals removed) leaves no residue when it dries — no squeegee needed.

Best for: Second-storey exterior windows reachable from the ground. The cleaner stays on the ground — safer and faster for higher windows. Common method for NZ commercial window cleaning.

Purified water: The key to a streak-free result from a water-fed pole. Tap water leaves mineral deposits when it dries; purified (demineralised or reverse-osmosis) water leaves nothing.

Rope Access

For high-rise commercial buildings or residential properties on steep cliffs where ladders or poles can't reach. Industrial window cleaners use rope access (abseiling) or elevated work platforms (EWPs, cherry pickers).

Licensing: Rope access workers must hold IRATA (Industrial Rope Access Trade Association) certification. EWP operators require training. Residential homeowners very rarely need rope access — it's primarily a commercial requirement.

How Often Should Windows Be Cleaned?

Location / type Recommended frequency
Suburban home (no salt, low dust) 2× per year (spring + autumn)
Coastal home (salt air) 4–6× per year
Urban/high traffic (dust, pollution) 3–4× per year
Commercial office (street level) Monthly or fortnightly
Before sale / move Once (thorough inside + out)
After building work / construction Once (post-construction clean)

Coastal NZ (Auckland harbourside, Wellington Bays, Christchurch coastal): Salt deposits from sea spray build up rapidly on windows — more frequent cleaning maintains clarity and prevents salt etching the glass over time.

What Window Cleaners Won't Do

Broken or cracked glass: A window cleaner shouldn't and won't clean cracked glass — the cleaning process can worsen cracks or cause injury. Replace cracked panes before booking a clean.

Asbestos-containing window putty: Pre-1980s sash windows sometimes have asbestos in the glazing putty around panes. Never sand, scrape, or disturb old window putty without testing first.

Inside windows where clutter prevents access: If rooms are full of furniture or items blocking access to windows, most cleaners won't move things — clear the windowsills before they arrive.

DIY Window Cleaning

For a single-storey home, DIY window cleaning is straightforward:

Equipment needed: - Squeegee (30–40cm, quality rubber blade) - Mop / applicator (same width as squeegee) - Bucket with warm water + window cleaning concentrate (or diluted dishwash liquid) - Chamois or microfibre cloth for edges

Technique: Wet the glass with the mop, apply the squeegee from top to bottom in overlapping strokes, dry the squeegee blade between strokes with a cloth. Wipe drips from edges.

Avoid: Direct sunlight while cleaning (dries too fast, streaks). Newspaper (old advice — modern ink smears). Dry wiping before wetting (scratches glass with grit).

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does window cleaning cost in NZ? A 3-bedroom home (inside + outside): $150–$350. Outside only: $100–$220. Per window (standard, inside + out): $10–$25. Coastal homes needing more frequent cleaning: $100–$220 per outside-only visit, 4–6× per year.

How often should windows be cleaned in NZ? Most NZ suburban homes: twice a year (spring and autumn). Coastal homes in salt-air environments (Auckland harbourside, Wellington Bays, Christchurch coastal suburbs): 4–6 times per year. Salt deposits etch glass over time if left too long.

Do window cleaners clean the inside of windows too? Many will, but inside cleaning typically costs extra and needs to be specifically requested. Most regular services are outside-only. Annual inside-and-out cleans are popular before winter or for pre-sale preparation.

Can window cleaners reach second-storey windows? Yes — professional window cleaners use water-fed poles (telescoping poles up to 15–20m) to clean second and even third-storey windows from the ground safely. This is safer than ladders and produces excellent results with purified water.

What is the best way to clean NZ coastal windows? Frequent cleaning (4–6× per year) with a quality squeegee and clean water is the main defence against salt damage. Between professional cleans, a quick rinse with fresh water after heavy sea-spray events washes off salt before it can etch or stain the glass. Hard water calcium deposits from tap water spray also need periodic acid-based treatment.
