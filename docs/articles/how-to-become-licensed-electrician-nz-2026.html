How to Become a Licensed Electrician NZ

Introduction to Becoming a Licensed Electrician in NZ

In New Zealand, the demand for skilled and licensed electricians is on the rise, driven by the country's growing construction and infrastructure development sectors. As of 2026, the electrical industry is expected to experience significant growth, with the government investing heavily in renewable energy and infrastructure projects. The Electrical Workers Registration Board (EWRB) is responsible for regulating the electrical industry, ensuring that all electrical workers are competent and safe to practice. To work as an electrician in NZ, you must be licensed by the EWRB, which involves completing an apprenticeship, passing a registration exam, and meeting the required standards.

Becoming a licensed electrician in NZ offers a rewarding and challenging career, with excellent job prospects and competitive wages. According to the Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment (MBIE), the median annual salary for electricians in NZ is around $65,000-$90,000. However, with experience and specialization, electricians can earn upwards of $120,000 per year. In this comprehensive guide, we will walk you through the step-by-step process of becoming a licensed electrician in NZ, covering the different types of licenses, the apprenticeship process, costs, and career prospects.

EWRB Licensing Explained

The EWRB offers several types of licenses for electricians, each with its own set of responsibilities and requirements. The main types of licenses are:

  • Electrical Worker: This is the entry-level license, which allows you to work under the supervision of a licensed electrician.
  • Craftsman Electrician: This license requires a minimum of 2 years of experience as an electrical worker and allows you to work independently on electrical installations.
  • Certifying Electrician: This is the highest level of license, which requires a minimum of 5 years of experience as an electrical worker and allows you to certify electrical installations.
  • Electrical Inspector: This license requires a minimum of 5 years of experience as a certifying electrician and allows you to inspect electrical installations.

Each type of license has its own set of requirements and responsibilities, and electricians must meet the necessary standards to progress to the next level. The EWRB is responsible for ensuring that all licensed electricians meet the required standards, and it provides a range of resources and support to help electricians achieve their goals.

Licence Types and Responsibilities

The following table outlines the different types of licenses and their corresponding responsibilities:

Licence Type Responsibilities
Electrical Worker Work under supervision, assist with electrical installations
Craftsman Electrician Work independently on electrical installations, supervise electrical workers
Certifying Electrician Certify electrical installations, work on complex electrical projects
Electrical Inspector Inspect electrical installations, identify and report defects

Step-by-Step Path to Becoming a Licensed Electrician

To become a licensed electrician in NZ, you must follow a step-by-step process:

Step 1: Find an Apprenticeship

The first step is to find an apprenticeship with a registered training provider, such as the Building and Construction Industry Training Organisation (BCITO) or a company-sponsored apprenticeship. BCITO apprenticeships are available through a range of providers, including polytechnics and private training establishments. Company-sponsored apprenticeships, on the other hand, are offered by employers who are willing to take on an apprentice and provide on-the-job training.

Step 2: Complete the 4-Year Apprenticeship

The apprenticeship typically lasts for 4 years, during which time you will complete a range of unit standards and practical hours. The apprenticeship is designed to provide you with the necessary skills and knowledge to become a competent electrician. You will work under the supervision of a licensed electrician and complete a range of tasks, including electrical installations, maintenance, and repairs.

Step 3: Sit and Pass the Registration Exam

After completing your apprenticeship, you will be eligible to sit the registration exam, which is administered by the EWRB. The exam tests your knowledge and skills in areas such as electrical theory, safety, and installation practices. The pass rate for the exam is around 65%, and you will need to achieve a minimum of 70% to pass.

Step 4: Apply for Electrical Worker Registration

Once you have passed the registration exam, you can apply for electrical worker registration, which costs between $250-$400. This registration allows you to work as an electrical worker under the supervision of a licensed electrician.

Step 5: Work Toward Certifying Electrician Licence

To become a certifying electrician, you will need to complete an additional 3 years of experience as an electrical worker and pass a range of exams. This will require you to demonstrate your competence in areas such as electrical design, installation, and testing.

BCITO Apprenticeship Details

BCITO apprenticeships are a popular way to become a licensed electrician in NZ. Here are some key details:

  • NZQA Qualifications: BCITO apprenticeships lead to NZQA qualifications, such as the New Zealand Certificate in Electrical Engineering (Level 4).
  • Block Course Schedule: Apprentices attend block courses, which are intensive training sessions that cover specific topics, such as electrical theory and safety.
  • Typical Apprentice Wages: Apprentice wages vary by year, but here is a rough guide:
    • 1st year: $14-$18 per hour
    • 2nd year: $16-$20 per hour
    • 3rd year: $18-$22 per hour
    • 4th year: $20-$25 per hour

BCITO apprenticeships are a great way to get started in the electrical industry, and they provide a range of benefits, including on-the-job training, mentorship, and support.

Costs of Becoming an Electrician

The costs of becoming an electrician in NZ can vary, but here are some estimated costs:

  • Training Costs: $2,000-$5,000 per year (depending on the provider and location)
  • EWRB Fees: $250-$400 (registration fee)
  • Exam Fees: $200-$500 (depending on the exam and provider)
  • Tools: $1,000-$3,000 (depending on the quality and brand)
  • Ongoing Licensing: $280 per year (registration renewal fee)

Overall, the total cost of becoming an electrician in NZ can range from $8,000 to $20,000 or more, depending on the individual circumstances.

Timeline

The timeline for becoming a licensed electrician in NZ can vary, but here is a rough guide:

  • 4 Years: Complete apprenticeship and become a registered electrician
  • 7 Years: Complete additional experience and become a certifying electrician

It's worth noting that the timeline can vary depending on individual circumstances, such as prior experience and qualifications.

The wages and career outlook for electricians in NZ are excellent. Here are some estimated wages:

  • Apprentice Wages: $14-$22 per hour
  • Qualified Electrician: $35-$55 per hour
  • Self-Employed Certifying Electrician: $80-$150 per hour

The demand for electricians in NZ is high, and the industry is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. According to the Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment (MBIE), the electrical industry is expected to grow by 10% per year over the next 5 years.

Recognised Prior Learning

If you are an overseas-trained electrician, you may be eligible for recognised prior learning (RPL) through the EWRB. This can help you fast-track your registration and reduce the amount of training you need to complete. The EWRB has an equivalency pathway for overseas-trained electricians, which allows them to demonstrate their competence and experience in the electrical industry.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about becoming a licensed electrician in NZ:

  1. Can I do electrical work without a licence in NZ?: No, it is illegal to do electrical work without a licence in NZ. You must be registered with the EWRB to work as an electrician.
  2. What's the difference between a registered electrician and a certifying electrician?: A registered electrician can work under the supervision of a licensed electrician, while a certifying electrician can work independently and certify electrical installations.
  3. How hard is the EWRB exam?: The EWRB exam is challenging, with a pass rate of around 65%. You will need to study hard and be well-prepared to pass the exam.
  4. Can I become a licensed electrician through online training?: No, you cannot become a licensed electrician through online training alone. You must complete an apprenticeship and gain practical experience to become a licensed electrician.
  5. How much does it cost to become a licensed electrician in NZ?: The cost of becoming a licensed electrician in NZ can vary, but estimated costs range from $8,000 to $20,000 or more, depending on the individual circumstances.
