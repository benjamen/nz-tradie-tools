Concrete Driveway NZ 2026 — Costs, Options and What to Expect

Published 23 June 2026 · NZ Tradie Tools
concrete drivewaydrivewayhomeownercostsNZ2026

Introduction

When it comes to home improvement, a well-designed and constructed driveway is a significant investment that can greatly enhance the overall appearance and value of your property. In New Zealand, where the climate can be quite harsh with heavy rainfall, intense UV rays, and significant vehicle weights, a durable and long-lasting driveway is essential. A concrete driveway, in particular, is a popular choice among homeowners due to its low maintenance requirements and aesthetic appeal. However, with so many options available, it can be overwhelming to decide on the best type of concrete driveway for your needs. In this guide, we will provide an overview of the different types of concrete driveways, their costs, and what to expect during the construction process.

Driveway Surface Options

When it comes to choosing a concrete driveway, there are several surface options to consider, each with its own unique characteristics, benefits, and costs. Here are some of the most common types of concrete driveways:

  • Plain Concrete: This is the most common type of concrete driveway and is known for its low maintenance requirements. The cost of plain concrete driveways ranges from $80 to $130 per square metre.
  • Exposed Aggregate: This type of concrete driveway features pebbles or stones that are exposed by washing the fresh concrete, providing a textured and slip-resistant surface. The cost of exposed aggregate driveways ranges from $110 to $160 per square metre.
  • Stamped/Coloured Concrete: This decorative type of concrete driveway features patterns and colours that can be customized to suit your desired design. The cost of stamped/coloured concrete driveways ranges from $130 to $200 per square metre.
  • Asphalt: While not a concrete driveway per se, asphalt is a popular alternative that is known for its durability and low cost. However, it requires resealing every 5 years. The cost of asphalt driveways ranges from $50 to $90 per square metre.
  • Pavers (Concrete Block): This type of driveway features interlocking concrete blocks that can be lifted and re-laid if services need access. The cost of pavers ranges from $120 to $220 per square metre.
  • Chip Seal (Tar Seal): This is the least expensive option, but it requires resurfacing every few years. The cost of chip seal driveways ranges from $30 to $60 per square metre.

Concrete Driveway Sizing and Thickness

The size and thickness of your concrete driveway will depend on several factors, including the type of vehicles that will be using it and the soil conditions of your property. Typically, residential concrete driveways are 80-120mm thick, while those that will be subjected to heavy vehicle traffic may require a thickness of 125-150mm. Reinforcing mesh, such as F72 mesh, is also standard for residential concrete driveways to provide added strength and durability. Sub-base preparation is also critical to preventing cracking, with a compacted layer of AP40 or GAP40 material typically used.

Drainage Requirements

In New Zealand, councils require that driveway drainage does not direct water onto neighbouring properties or public footpaths. A channel drain at the entrance of the driveway, also known as a vehicle crossover, is mandatory in many areas. Permeable options, such as permeable pavers or permeable asphalt, are also increasingly encouraged as they allow water to percolate through the surface, reducing the risk of flooding and erosion.

Full Cost Breakdown for a Standard Driveway

The cost of a standard concrete driveway can vary depending on several factors, including the size of the driveway, the type of concrete used, and the complexity of the job. Here are some estimated costs for a standard concrete driveway:

  • 50m² plain concrete driveway (including sub-base, reinforcing, and channel drain): $5,000-$9,000
  • 50m² exposed aggregate driveway: $7,000-$12,000
  • 100m² plain concrete driveway: $9,000-$16,000
  • Sub-base excavation (if significant): $1,000-$4,000 extra
  • Retaining if sloped: variable
  • Concrete pump if poor access: $600-$1,200 extra

It's also important to note that these costs do not include the cost of council permits, inspections, and other regulatory requirements. To manage these costs and stay on top of your finances, consider using a job management and invoicing tool like Xero or Fergus.

Council Crossover/Vehicle Crossing

The section of the driveway that connects your property to the street is known as the council crossover or vehicle crossing. This area is subject to council regulations and may require a permit and inspection. The cost of installing a council crossover can range from $500 to $3,000, depending on the complexity of the job and the requirements of your local council. It's essential to check with your council before starting your driveway project to ensure compliance with all regulations.

DIY vs Contractor

While it may be tempting to try to save money by doing the concrete work yourself, it's generally not recommended. Concrete work is physically demanding and time-sensitive, with a limited window of 30-60 minutes to place and finish the concrete once the truck arrives. Additionally, formwork, screeding, and floating require specialized skills to avoid cracking and surface defects. It's best to hire a professional contractor who has the experience and expertise to do the job correctly.

Quotes — What to Include

When obtaining quotes from contractors, make sure to include the following items:

  • Excavation and disposal of excavated material
  • Sub-base supply and compaction
  • Formwork and reinforcing mesh
  • Concrete supply (including volume and strength specification)
  • Pour and finish
  • Channel drain if required
  • Sealer (first application)

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about concrete driveways:

  1. How long does a concrete driveway last in NZ?: A well-constructed concrete driveway can last for 20-30 years or more, depending on the quality of the materials and workmanship.
  2. How soon can I drive on a new concrete driveway?: It's generally recommended to wait at least 7-10 days before driving on a new concrete driveway to allow the concrete to cure and harden.
  3. Do I need council permission for a driveway in NZ?: Yes, you may need a council permit and inspection to ensure compliance with local regulations and standards.
  4. What causes concrete driveways to crack?: Cracking can be caused by a variety of factors, including poor sub-base preparation, inadequate reinforcing, and extreme temperature fluctuations.
  5. Can I seal my own driveway?: While it's possible to seal your own driveway, it's generally recommended to hire a professional to ensure the job is done correctly and safely.

Conclusion

In conclusion, a concrete driveway is a significant investment that requires careful planning and consideration. By understanding the different types of concrete driveways, their costs, and what to expect during the construction process, you can make an informed decision that meets your needs and budget.
