NZ Tiler Pricing Guide 2026: Hourly Rates, Job Costs & How to Quote

Tiling is one of the most detail-intensive trades in New Zealand construction — and getting your pricing right is the difference between a profitable job and one that bleeds time. Whether you're quoting a bathroom renovation in Auckland or a commercial kitchen floor in Christchurch, this guide gives you real 2026 NZ rates to benchmark against and practical advice on structuring quotes that win work without underselling yourself.

What Do Tilers Charge in New Zealand in 2026?

Tiler rates in NZ vary by experience, region, and job complexity. Based on current market rates:

Experience Level Hourly Rate
Entry-level / first years out of apprenticeship $50 – $65/hr
Journeyman tiler (5–10 years' experience) $65 – $85/hr
Senior / specialist tiler $85 – $110/hr
Large-format or natural stone specialist $100 – $130/hr

Day rates typically run $450 – $900 depending on the above, with most experienced tilers charging $550 – $750/day for standard residential work.

Auckland and Wellington tend to run 10–15% above national averages. Southland and West Coast sit slightly below. Factor your local market into your baseline rate.

Use the NZ Hourly Rate Calculator if you want to double-check your rate covers your actual costs — many tilers underprice when they don't account for travel, downtime between jobs, and vehicle expenses.

Per-Square-Metre Pricing: What to Charge for Common Jobs

Most residential tiling quotes include labour and materials together. Below are typical fully installed rates (labour + standard materials) in 2026:

Job Type Installed Cost (per sqm)
Standard ceramic floor tiles (400x400 or similar) $75 – $110/sqm
Porcelain floor tiles (standard format) $90 – $140/sqm
Large-format tiles (600x600 or larger) $110 – $180/sqm
Wall tiles (bathroom, kitchen) $80 – $130/sqm
Mosaic tiles $180 – $280/sqm
Natural stone (marble, travertine) $200 – $350/sqm

These rates assume standard prep work. Waterproofing, demolition, floor levelling, and heated floor installation are all extras.

Pricing Common Tiling Jobs in NZ

Here are ballpark totals for common tiling projects in 2026:

Bathroom floor (5–8 sqm, standard tiles): $600 – $1,200
Shower recess, fully tiled (floor + walls to 2.1m): $1,200 – $2,800
Full bathroom renovation tiling (floor, shower, feature wall): $3,000 – $7,500
Kitchen splashback (standard tiles, 3–5 sqm): $600 – $1,500
Laundry floor (4–6 sqm): $500 – $900
Outdoor pavers / alfresco (10–20 sqm): $1,400 – $3,500
Commercial kitchen floor (50 sqm, anti-slip): $6,000 – $14,000

These are starting points. Always price by the specific job — a 5 sqm bathroom with niches, underfloor heating, and complex cuts will take three times as long as a simple laundry.

Material Costs: What to Expect in 2026

Tile and adhesive prices have risen across the board in early 2026, driven by increased freight costs and the weaker NZD against Asian tile exporters. Budget for:

  • Ceramic tiles: $25 – $60/sqm (supply only)
  • Porcelain tiles: $40 – $100/sqm
  • Natural stone: $80 – $200/sqm
  • Tile adhesive: $20 – $30 per bag (covers approx. 5–6 sqm)
  • Grout: $18 – $35/kg
  • Waterproofing membrane (wet areas): $18 – $35/sqm applied
  • Backer board / substrate: $40 – $65 per sheet

Always allow 10–15% tile wastage in your material calculations (more for diagonal cuts or complex patterns). Underordering on tiles is one of the most expensive mistakes a tiler can make — matching a batch dye lot months later can cost you a fortune or leave a client without a solution.

How to Build a Tiling Quote That Protects Your Margin

1. Separate labour from materials

Quote materials at cost-plus a markup (typically 15–25% on tiling supplies is reasonable). This protects you if prices shift between quote and purchase. See Materials Markup Strategy for NZ Tradies for guidance on what's defensible.

2. Price prep work separately

Waterproofing, floor levelling (self-levelling compound, grinding), and demolition are not "included" by default. Make these explicit line items in your quote. Clients often don't understand what's involved, and a clear breakdown prevents disputes later.

3. Include a variation clause

Unforeseen substrate problems — like finding a soft subfloor under tiles you're removing — can blow your timeline and material costs. A one-line clause covering variations at your hourly rate protects you without scaring the client.

4. Factor in cut complexity

Simple square rooms with standard 400x400 tiles are fast. Diagonal layouts, mosaic feature walls, hexagonal tiles, and rooms with multiple fixtures, alcoves, or niches can easily add 40–60% to your time. Scope it before you quote.

5. Apply your actual overhead, not a guess

Many tilers use a ballpark "1.5x labour" rule for quoting, but this doesn't account for your actual vehicle costs, ACC levies, insurance, and admin time. Use the NZ Job Cost Calculator to build quotes from real numbers.

What IRD Expects from Tiling Contractors

If you're self-employed or running a tiling business, IRD treats materials differently from labour for GST purposes — but the end result is the same: you charge GST on the total invoice (labour + materials + markup) once your turnover exceeds $60,000/year. If you're close to that threshold, see Should I Register for GST? to understand your obligations.

For business expenses, tiling tools are depreciable assets under IRD's schedule. The Investment Boost deduction — introduced in May 2025 — allows a 20% one-off deduction on new eligible assets, so major tile saw, grinder, or mixer purchases made since then may qualify for accelerated write-off.

Summary: NZ Tiler Rates at a Glance (2026)

  • Hourly rate: $50 – $130/hr depending on experience and region
  • Standard ceramic install: $75 – $110/sqm fully installed
  • Large format / stone: $110 – $350/sqm fully installed
  • Full bathroom tiling: $3,000 – $7,500 depending on complexity
  • Always allow 10–15% tile wastage, price prep as a separate line item, and apply your real overhead — not a guess
