Blocked Drain Cost NZ 2026 — Call-Out Prices, Causes and When to Call a Drainlayer

Published 23 June 2026 · NZ Tradie Tools

A blocked drain is one of those household problems that needs sorting immediately — and knowing whether to call a plumber, a drainlayer, or try DIY first can save you time and money. Here's what drain clearing costs in NZ in 2026 and how to identify what type of blockage you're dealing with.

Is It a Plumber or a Drainlayer Job?

The distinction matters — they're different licensed trades in NZ:

Plumber: Handles above-ground plumbing — slow draining sinks, blocked toilets, blocked shower drains. They clear blockages within the building using a plunger, hand snake, or hydro-jetter on the internal system.

Drainlayer: Handles below-ground sanitary drainage — the pipes from the building to the sewer. Tree root intrusion, collapsed pipes, offset joints, and whole-system blockages that affect multiple fixtures are usually drainlayer territory.

In practice: Many plumbing companies have drainlayers on their team or can assess and refer. If you're not sure, call a company that offers both — they'll assess which trade is needed.

How to Tell What Kind of Blockage You Have

Single fixture slow/blocked (one sink, one toilet, one shower): Likely a local blockage. Plumber call-out — often clearable with a plunger or hand snake. Can often try DIY first (see below).

Multiple fixtures slow or backed up at the same time: Likely a main drain blockage between the house and the sewer. Drainlayer / hydro-jet required. Don't attempt DIY — you can't reach this with a standard plunger.

Sewage smell from drains or gurgling sounds: Main drain issue — call a drainlayer. Gurgling indicates a partial blockage with air being drawn through the water seal.

Overflow at the inspection chamber: The main drain is blocked. The inspection chamber (a small access point in your yard or driveway) is overflowing — main drain emergency, call a drainlayer.

After heavy rain, outdoor drains overflow: Stormwater drain blockage — drainlayer or council (if the blockage is in the public stormwater system, which is the council's responsibility to clear).

DIY First — When It's Worth Trying

For a single blocked sink or slow drain:

  1. Plunger: Cup plunger for sinks, flange plunger for toilets. Proper technique: seal over the drain, 15–20 firm strokes.
  2. Boiling water: Pour a kettle of boiling water down the drain (works on grease blockages, not solid obstructions).
  3. Baking soda + vinegar: Pour 1/2 cup baking soda, then 1/2 cup white vinegar. Leave 30 minutes, flush with boiling water. Effective for mild grease and soap blockages.
  4. Drain snake (hire or buy): A hand-operated coiled snake can clear blockages 1–3m down the pipe. Available at hardware stores ($30–$80 to buy, or hire).

Don't use: Chemical drain cleaners (Drano, etc.) — caustic chemicals damage older pipe materials (especially cast iron and rubber joints), are hazardous to handle, and don't work on solid obstructions. They also create a hazardous chemical situation if you then need a plumber to attend.

Hydro-Jetting: The Professional Solution

Hydro-jetting is the most effective professional drain clearing method — high-pressure water (up to 4,000 PSI) is blasted through the drain, cutting through grease, roots, and debris.

  • Cuts through tree roots (temporary — roots regrow, but buys 1–3 years)
  • Clears grease buildup in kitchen drain lines
  • Removes scale in older pipes
  • Safe for most pipe materials (cast iron, PVC, clay earthenware)

After hydro-jetting, a good drainlayer will recommend CCTV inspection to see if root intrusion, cracked pipes, or offset joints are the underlying cause — otherwise the same problem will return.

CCTV Drain Inspection

A small camera is run through your drain to identify the exact location and cause of blockage or recurring problems. This is the most valuable diagnostic tool for any recurring or unclear drain problem.

What CCTV inspection shows: - Root intrusion (where and how severe) - Cracked or broken pipes - Offset or settled joints - Correct fall (drains must fall toward the sewer — settled pipes can run flat or even backwards) - Condition of the overall pipe

Cost: $350–$700 for a standard residential CCTV inspection. Most drainlayers include this in a combined jetting + camera package ($450–$900).

Worth doing before committing to expensive drain repairs — CCTV confirms what needs fixing and where, avoiding unnecessary excavation.

When Drain Repair or Replacement Is Needed

Some blockages are symptoms of underlying pipe damage that clearing alone won't fix:

  • Tree root intrusion: Roots regrow and will block again. Options: root-cut and monitor (short-term), drain reline (medium-term, ~$300–$600/m), or excavation and replacement (long-term).
  • Offset joints: Settlement has moved pipe sections out of alignment. CCTV shows this clearly. Usually requires excavation to fix.
  • Collapsed or cracked pipe: Often from ground movement, vehicle loading over old pipes, or corrosion. Excavation and replacement required.
  • Incorrect fall: Flat or reverse-fall drain sections pool water and solids. Requires regrading — excavation needed.

Get CCTV inspection before agreeing to any excavation work — it confirms the scope of the problem.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to unblock a drain in NZ? A plumber call-out to clear a minor internal blockage: $150–$350. Hydro-jetting the main drain: $250–$600. After-hours emergency: add $150–$350 surcharge. CCTV inspection: $350–$700.

Can I unblock my own drain in NZ? For single fixture blockages (one sink or toilet), DIY with a plunger or drain snake is worth trying first. For main drain blockages (multiple fixtures, gurgling, inspection chamber overflow), call a licensed drainlayer — these are beyond DIY tools.

What causes blocked drains in NZ homes? In kitchens: grease and food waste buildup. In bathrooms: hair, soap scum. In toilets: wet wipes (never flush them — they don't break down). In outdoor drains: tree root intrusion (especially from older clay or earthenware pipes). After storms: leaf debris and stormwater overwhelm.

How do I stop my drain from blocking again? Use sink strainers to catch food and hair. Never pour grease down drains — let it cool and bin it. Never flush wet wipes. For recurring tree root blockages, CCTV inspection and professional assessment of drain relining or replacement options.

What is the difference between a plumber and a drainlayer for blocked drains? Plumbers clear blockages within the building (internal pipework above ground). Drainlayers handle the buried drain pipes from the building to the public sewer. A main drain blockage — where multiple fixtures are backing up — is a drainlayer job. Many companies offer both services.
