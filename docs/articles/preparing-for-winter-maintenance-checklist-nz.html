Preparing for Winter: A Maintenance Checklist for NZ Tradies

The colder months are approaching fast in New Zealand, and for many trades, this means a shift in the type of work you’ll be doing. Instead of just new builds, you’ll likely see an increase in call-outs for weatherproofing, roof repairs, and insulation upgrades.

This guide provides a professional checklist you can use to consult with your clients on preparing their properties for the upcoming NZ winter.

1. Roof and Gutter Inspection

Heavy rainfall and wind are standard features of an NZ winter. - Check for blockages: Clear out gutters and downpipes to prevent overflow, which can lead to timber rot or structural issues. - Inspect roofing: Look for loose tiles, damaged flashings, or signs of rust on metal roofs. Early intervention prevents costly leaks.

2. Insulation and Moisture Control

Warmer homes are healthier homes. - Ventilation: Check sub-floor ventilation and bathroom/kitchen extraction to prevent condensation buildup, which leads to mould—a major issue in many NZ homes. - Insulation: Suggest top-up insulation if properties are older. Better insulation not only keeps heat in but also helps manage moisture balance.

3. Outdoor Areas

  • Drainage: Ensure external drains are clear to prevent localized flooding during heavy storm events.
  • Decking: Check for slippery surfaces. Regular cleaning helps remove moss and algae that become hazardous in wet conditions.

4. Digital Record-Keeping with Fastcrew

Whatever maintenance work you take on this winter, staying organized is key to profitability. Use Fastcrew to: - Schedule your maintenance runs. - Keep photos of "before and after" work for your records—this is invaluable for providing peace of mind to clients and proving work quality.

Managing Your Business

As we head into mid-2026, keep your financial house in order. Don't forget to use our GST Calculator to calculate your liabilities accurately, and refer to our job pricing guide to ensure you are factoring in any seasonal price adjustments for materials or travel time.

Winter is an excellent time to reach out to existing clients to offer "winter prep" packages. It keeps your schedule full during the slower season and helps homeowners protect their biggest assets.
