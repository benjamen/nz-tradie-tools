NoCowboys vs Builderscrack vs TradieTools — Which NZ Tradie Directory Is Best? (2026)

Published 14 June 2026 · NZ Tradie Tools
nocowboysbuilderscracktradietoolscomparisonNZ

If you've been hunting for a tradie in New Zealand — or you're a tradie trying to win more work — you've almost certainly run into NoCowboys, Builderscrack and TradieTools. They all describe themselves as ways to connect Kiwi homeowners with trustworthy tradespeople, but under the hood they work in completely different ways. One charges tradies a flat annual fee. One runs an auction-style job marketplace. One gives tradies a free listing and pulls reviews from everywhere into one place.

This guide breaks down exactly how each platform makes money, what they're best at, and which one you should reach for depending on whether you're a homeowner looking to hire or a tradie looking to get found. Spoiler: the honest answer is often "use more than one" — but knowing why helps you spend your time and money in the right place.

How Each Platform Actually Works

NoCowboys is the elder statesman of the three. It's essentially a directory with a strong, verified review system bolted on. Tradies pay a fixed annual fee to maintain a profile, and homeowners search by trade and location, then read reviews to decide who to call. The model is simple: you pay to be listed, and your reviews do the selling. Because the fee is the same whether you get two leads or two hundred, established tradies with a steady stream of work tend to get the best value out of it.

Builderscrack flips the model. Instead of homeowners coming to tradies, homeowners post a job describing what they need, and tradies "spend" to express interest or quote. It's a marketplace — closer to bidding for work than maintaining a shopfront. That makes it great for filling gaps in your schedule, but the cost is variable and competitive: popular jobs attract multiple quotes, and you're paying for the chance to win, not the win itself. Builderscrack also runs handy interactive cost estimate tools that homeowners use before posting.

TradieTools takes the position that being listed shouldn't cost anything. The core directory listing is free forever — create a profile, show up in searches, get contacted directly by homeowners. Revenue comes from optional premium tiers ($29–$59/month) that add lead matching, priority placement and Pro features. The other distinguishing move is review aggregation: rather than building yet another isolated review silo, TradieTools imports publicly available ratings from NoCowboys, Builderscrack and Google so a tradie's whole reputation shows up in one badge.

Which Is Best for Homeowners?

It depends on what stage you're at.

If you know roughly what you want and you'd like competing prices, Builderscrack is hard to beat. You post once, tradies come to you, and you compare quotes side by side without making a single phone call. The trade-off is that you can get a flood of contacts, and the cheapest quote isn't always the best fit.

If reviews are your priority — you want to read detailed, verified feedback from past customers before you trust someone in your home — NoCowboys has the deepest, most established review base in New Zealand. It's the place to go when reputation matters more than price-shopping.

If you already have a specific tradie or two in mind and just want to find them, check their reputation, and contact them directly, TradieTools is the most frictionless. Because it pulls ratings from multiple sources, you get a fuller picture of a business without opening five browser tabs — and you're contacting them directly rather than entering an auction.

Which Is Best for Tradies?

Here the calculus is about cost versus certainty.

NoCowboys is more established and carries genuine brand recognition with homeowners — a strong NoCowboys review profile still pulls weight. But the annual fee is a real commitment, and if your area is quiet or you're just starting out, you're paying the same whether the leads roll in or not.

Builderscrack can deliver work fast, but the pay-per-quote model means costs add up, and you're often competing on price against several other tradies for the same job. It rewards quick, sharp quoting and works best when you have capacity to fill.

TradieTools is the cheapest way to get online and stay online: the listing is free, so there's no downside to claiming your profile even if you also use the others. If you want more, the Pro tier adds lead matching at $29–$59/month — still well below a typical NoCowboys annual fee spread across the year. For a new tradie, "free listing now, upgrade if it pays off" is a low-risk starting point.

Why We Import NoCowboys / Builderscrack Ratings

One of the most common frustrations for homeowners is that a good tradie's reputation is scattered. They might have 40 five-star reviews on NoCowboys, a strong Builderscrack history, and a handful of Google reviews — but you'd have to check all three to see the full picture.

That's why TradieTools imports publicly available ratings from NoCowboys, Builderscrack and Google into a single badge on each listing. For homeowners, it's one place to gauge trust. For tradies, it means the reviews you've already earned elsewhere count toward your TradieTools profile from day one — you don't start from zero just because you joined a new platform. We're not trying to replace those review systems; we're trying to make the reputation you've already built more visible.

When to Use All Three

The platforms aren't really rivals so much as different tools for different moments. A sensible approach for most people looks like this:

  • Homeowners: Post big, well-defined jobs on Builderscrack to get competitive quotes; read NoCowboys reviews to vet the shortlist; use TradieTools to search, compare aggregated ratings and contact a specific tradie directly for smaller or urgent jobs.
  • Tradies: Claim your free TradieTools listing as a baseline (there's no reason not to), then layer on whichever paid platform actually produces leads in your region. In some areas that's NoCowboys; in others Builderscrack fills the gaps. Let results, not loyalty, decide where you spend.

You don't have to pick a single winner. The smart play is matching each platform to the job it does best.
