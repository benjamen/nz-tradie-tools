Garden Design Cost NZ 2026 — Designer Fees, Plans and What's Included

A professionally designed garden can transform a property and add real value — but design fees vary widely in NZ. Here's what garden design costs in NZ in 2026, what different levels of service include, and how to decide what you need.

All prices GST inclusive. Auckland and Wellington designers typically charge 15–25% above national average. Complex sites, consented retaining, and large properties increase design fees significantly.

What Levels of Garden Design Are Available?

Garden Consultation Only

An experienced designer visits, walks the property, and gives verbal advice and a written summary. Best for homeowners who know what they want and just need guidance on plant selection, layout, or problem areas. Cost: $150–$400.

Concept Design

Sketch plans showing the overall layout — areas, zones, approximate shapes, key features. Usually includes a mood board or image references. Helps you see the big picture before committing to detailed plans or construction quotes. Cost: $500–$2,000.

Detailed Design Package

Full CAD-drawn plans including: - Site survey and levels - Detailed layout plan with dimensions - Planting schedule (species, sizes, quantities) - Hard landscaping details (paving, edging, retaining walls) - Irrigation zones - Lighting plan (if included) - Specification notes for contractors

This is what you need to get accurate construction quotes from multiple landscapers. Cost: $1,500–$6,000 for a typical residential garden.

Landscape Architecture (Consented Projects)

For projects requiring building consent (retaining walls over 1.5m, significant earthworks, ponds near structures), a registered landscape architect can prepare consent documents and oversee construction compliance. Cost: $5,000–$20,000+, depending on project size and consent complexity.

NZ Institute of Landscape Architects (NZILA): Registered landscape architects have recognised professional qualifications. For projects involving consent applications, engineering co-ordination, or complex earthworks, use an NZILA member.

For most residential garden redesigns: A qualified garden designer is sufficient and more cost-effective.

What Affects Garden Design Cost?

Site complexity: Steep sections, poor access, unusual shapes, significant existing structures, and heritage overlays all increase design time.

Project scope: A planting plan for an existing structured garden costs far less than a full design including new paving, retaining, irrigation, and outdoor structures.

Level of detail: Some designers provide sketch-level plans; others produce construction-ready CAD documentation. The latter takes much longer and costs more, but produces better outcomes from contractors.

Designer experience: New garden designers may charge $80–$100/hr; established specialists with strong portfolios charge $130–$160/hr.

Auckland premium: Auckland designers with strong residential portfolios charge 15–25% above designers in other centres.

How to Get Value from a Garden Designer

  1. Be clear about your brief before meeting: Know your budget, how much maintenance you want to do, which areas matter most, and what function the garden serves (entertaining, children's play, vegetables, privacy).

  2. Ask for a fixed-price quote for the design: Open-ended hourly billing for design can escalate. Good designers will quote a fixed fee for design deliverables once they've understood the scope.

  3. Separate design from construction: Getting designs done first lets you get competitive quotes from multiple landscapers. Bundling design and build with one company limits comparison.

  4. Ask to see comparable completed projects: Visit or see photos of gardens the designer has completed in similar conditions — size, soil, climate.

  5. Clarify what's included: Will you receive CAD files you can share with contractors? Is a planting schedule included? Are irrigation and lighting plans in scope?

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does a garden designer cost in NZ? Initial consultation: $150–$400. Concept design: $500–$2,000. Full detailed design package (CAD plans, planting schedule): $1,500–$6,000. Landscape architect for consented complex projects: $5,000–$20,000+. Hourly rates range from $80–$160/hr for designers and $120–$250/hr for registered landscape architects.

Do I need a landscape architect or garden designer in NZ? For most residential gardens — new planting, paving, lawn, and features up to 1.5m retaining — a garden designer is sufficient. If you need building consent (retaining walls over 1.5m, significant earthworks, structures near boundaries), use a registered landscape architect (NZILA member) who can prepare consent documentation.

Is garden design worth the cost? Yes, if the garden project is significant. A professional design typically: produces better outcomes than ad-hoc decisions, allows accurate competitive quoting, avoids expensive mistakes (wrong plants, poor drainage, structures in wrong place), and adds resale value. Design costs are typically 5–15% of construction cost — worthwhile on projects over $15,000.

How long does garden design take in NZ? Initial consultation to concept design: 1–3 weeks. Full detailed design: 3–8 weeks depending on complexity. For consented projects (engineer input, Council submission): allow 2–4 months for the full process before construction can begin.
