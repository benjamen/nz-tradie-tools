Cabinet Makers NZ 2026 — Costs, Custom vs Flatpack, Christchurch and Tauranga

Published 23 June 2026 · NZ Tradie Tools
cabinet makerscabinetskitchen cabinetsjoinerycostsNZ2026ChristchurchTauranga

Custom-made cabinetry is the biggest variable in any NZ kitchen or bathroom renovation. Here's what cabinet makers charge in NZ in 2026, how custom compares to flatpack, what to look for in a joiner, and how to find cabinet makers in Christchurch, Tauranga and other NZ centres.

The honest answer for most NZ renovations: Flatpack is a legitimate choice for rental properties, quick refreshes, or tight budgets. But for a home you're staying in, custom cabinetry from a NZ joiner fits the space perfectly, lasts 20–30 years, and adds more to resale value than flatpack.

Kitchen Cabinet Materials

Carcass (box) materials: - Moisture-resistant MDF (MR MDF): The standard in NZ kitchens. Dimensionally stable, takes paint well, moisture resistant. - Plywood: Stronger and more moisture resistant than MDF; preferred in premium builds and coastal environments. - Melamine-faced particleboard: Budget option; adequate in dry areas, not ideal in kitchens with steam exposure.

Door materials and finishes: - 2-pack polyurethane (2-pac): High-gloss or satin finish, hard-wearing, the premium choice for painted kitchen doors. Requires spray booth — done at the factory. - Vinyl-wrapped MDF: Routed profile wrapped in vinyl. Popular mid-market option. Good appearance, lower cost than 2-pac. - Timber veneer: Real timber veneer on MDF or ply. Beautiful grain, warm feel. Requires clear coat maintenance over time. - Solid timber: Used in traditional or heritage style kitchens. Higher movement with humidity than composite options.

Lead Times for NZ Cabinet Makers

Cabinet making is not fast — you cannot book a kitchen in March and expect it in April. Typical lead times:

  • Medium-sized joinery workshops: 6–10 weeks from design sign-off to delivery
  • Larger manufacturers: 8–14 weeks
  • Premium custom joiners: 12–20 weeks
  • Flatpack assembly: 1–4 weeks including delivery

Plan early. Most NZ cabinet makers are booked 2–3 months ahead during peak renovation season (Sept–Jan). For a Christmas kitchen, you need to be placing your order by September at the latest.

Cabinet Makers in Christchurch

Christchurch has a strong joinery and cabinet-making sector — a legacy of post-earthquake rebuilds. Key areas to find cabinet makers:

  • Wigram, Sockburn and Hornby — industrial zones where many joinery workshops are based
  • Rolleston and Kaiapoi — growing areas with newer joinery businesses

What to look for in a Christchurch cabinet maker: - NZKB (New Zealand Kitchen and Bathroom) member or Master Joiners affiliation - 3D design service (most reputable joiners provide this before you commit) - Factory visit — Christchurch joiners generally welcome customers to view work in progress

Cabinet Makers in Tauranga

Tauranga's rapid growth has driven strong demand for joinery. The Bay of Plenty has a healthy base of cabinet makers serving residential and commercial clients.

Key areas: Greerton, Tauriko, Te Rapa corridor have workshop concentrations. Lead times in Tauranga can be longer than main centres — book early.

How to Get the Best Quote

Before approaching cabinet makers: 1. Have a kitchen plan (even a rough sketch with measurements) 2. Know your style direction (handle-less, shaker, traditional, contemporary) 3. Decide on carcass and door material preferences 4. Have a realistic budget in mind — tell them, it saves everyone time

Questions to ask: 1. Can you do a 3D render before I commit? 2. What carcass material do you use — MR MDF or plywood? 3. What's the door finish — 2-pac, vinyl wrap, or veneer? 4. What hardware brands do you use? (Blum and Hettich are quality; avoid unknown brands) 5. What's your current lead time? 6. Is installation included or quoted separately? 7. What warranty do you offer on materials and workmanship?

Red flags: - Won't provide a 3D render or detailed specification sheet - Vague quote with just a total — no breakdown by cabinet count or material - Hardware brand not specified (cheap hardware fails fastest) - No showroom or previous work you can view

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do custom kitchen cabinets cost in NZ? A medium-sized kitchen (12–15 cabinets, 2-pac doors, MR MDF carcass, Blum hardware): $15,000–$35,000 supply only. Add $2,000–$6,000 for installation, plus your benchtop, splashback, and appliances separately. Total kitchen budget including all elements: $30,000–$70,000 for a mid-range renovation.

How long does it take to get custom cabinets made in NZ? Typically 6–12 weeks from design sign-off to delivery, depending on the joinery company's current capacity. Add 2–3 weeks for installation. Plan at least 4 months from first contact with a cabinet maker to a completed kitchen.

What is 2-pac paint for kitchen cabinets? 2-pac (two-pack polyurethane) is a hard, high-gloss or satin finish applied in a factory spray booth. It's harder, more durable, and easier to clean than standard paint. Most premium NZ kitchens use 2-pac doors. The finish cannot be applied on site — it must be done by the cabinet maker in a spray-controlled environment.

Is custom joinery worth it vs IKEA kitchen? For a home you're staying in: yes, almost always. Custom joinery fits your exact space, uses better materials and hardware, and lasts 20–30 years. IKEA and flatpack kitchens use standard sizes (often leaving gaps at ceilings or walls) and cheaper hardware that wears faster. Custom joinery also adds more to resale value. For a rental property or very tight budget, flatpack is a legitimate choice.
