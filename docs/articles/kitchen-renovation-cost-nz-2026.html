Kitchen Renovation Cost NZ 2026 — Budget, Mid-Range & Full Gut Prices

Published 14 June 2026 · NZ Tradie Tools
kitchenrenovationcostsNZ2026

The kitchen is the most expensive room in the house to renovate per square metre, and it's also the one where budgets blow out most often. Ask around and you'll hear stories of $12,000 refreshes and $90,000 gut jobs — both described simply as "a kitchen reno". The gap comes down to three things: how much you change the layout, the quality of your cabinetry, and what you put on the benchtop. This guide breaks down realistic 2026 New Zealand prices so you can set a budget that survives contact with reality.

Kitchen Renovation Cost at a Glance

New Zealand kitchen renovations fall into three broad tiers. Knowing which one you're aiming for is the single most useful thing you can do before getting quotes.

Tier What's included Typical cost (NZD)
Budget refresh New benchtop, painted cabinets, new taps, handles & appliances — no layout change $8,000–$15,000
Mid-range Full gut, new cabinetry, stone benchtop, sink, appliances, flooring & lighting $20,000–$45,000
High-end / custom Bespoke joinery, premium stone, integrated appliances, island, butler's pantry $55,000–$120,000+

If you live in Auckland or Wellington, add roughly 15–20% to all of these figures. Demand is higher, joiners and installers are busier, and overheads are bigger in the main centres.

Kitchen Renovation Cost by Component

Most of the budget sits in three places — cabinetry, the benchtop, and the trades who connect everything up. Here's what each piece costs on its own.

Cabinetry

Cabinetry is the biggest single line in nearly every kitchen quote, and it's where the flat-pack vs custom decision really bites.

  • Flat-pack (Kaboodle, IKEA, etc): $3,000–$8,000 installed. Pre-made in standard sizes, excellent value for a standard layout.
  • Custom cabinetry: $12,000–$40,000+. Built to your exact dimensions by a joiner — the answer for awkward spaces, premium finishes, and unusual layouts.

Benchtops

The benchtop sets the tone of the whole kitchen, and the price range is enormous:

  • Laminate: $800–$2,000. Hard-wearing and affordable; modern laminates look far better than they used to.
  • Engineered stone (Silestone, Caesarstone, etc): $3,000–$8,000. The most popular mid-to-high choice in NZ.
  • Marble: $6,000–$20,000. Stunning, but porous and high-maintenance.

The Trades

A kitchen reno pulls in several trades, each with their own line on the quote:

  • Plumber (disconnect/reconnect sink + dishwasher): $800–$2,500. See our plumber cost guide for detail.
  • Electrician (stovetop, rangehood, extra circuits): $1,000–$3,000. More in our electrician cost guide.
  • Tiler (splashback labour only): $400–$1,200.
  • Appliances (freestanding range, dishwasher, rangehood): $3,000–$15,000.

Do You Need a Building Consent?

This trips up a lot of homeowners. The good news: a like-for-like kitchen reno — same layout, swapping cabinets, benchtop and appliances in their existing positions — does not require a building consent.

You will need a consent if you:

  • Move plumbing or electrical to a new wall
  • Make structural changes, such as removing a load-bearing wall to open up the space
  • Add a new window or door

Consent admin typically costs $800–$2,000 and adds a few weeks to your timeline. If your reno crosses any of these lines, factor it in early — retrospective consents are expensive and stressful.

How Long Does a Kitchen Renovation Take?

Once work starts on site, a mid-range reno runs 3–6 weeks. The bigger timing factor is what happens before work starts: cabinetry lead times. Flat-pack is quick to get hold of, but custom joinery can take 6–10 weeks to be built. Order your cabinetry as early as possible — your kitchen can't be installed until it arrives, no matter how available your builder is.

How to Keep Costs Down

A few decisions early on make a big difference to the final number:

  • Lock in your layout before ordering cabinetry. Changing your mind after the joiner has started is the fastest way to blow the budget.
  • Don't move the sink unless you have to. Relocating plumbing adds $2,000–$5,000 — keeping the sink where it is keeps that money in your pocket.
  • Use a project manager if you're spending over $40,000. Coordinating multiple trades yourself is a job in itself, and mistakes at this level are costly.
  • Mix tiers. Flat-pack carcasses with a stone benchtop and quality handles can look high-end at a mid-range price.

Getting Accurate Quotes

Always get at least three quotes, and make sure each one covers the same scope — it's easy to compare a cabinetry-only quote against a full turnkey quote and think one tradie is wildly cheaper. Confirm whether appliances are included, who handles the consent (if needed), and whether the benchtop price is for the material you actually want. The cheapest quote often turns out to be the one with the most exclusions.
