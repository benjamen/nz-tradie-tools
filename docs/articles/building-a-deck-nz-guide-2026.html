Building a Deck NZ 2026 — Costs, Consent Rules and DIY Guide

Published 28 June 2026 · NZ Tradie Tools
deckdeckingDIYhomeownerbuilding consentNZ2026

Introduction

Building a deck is one of New Zealand's most popular outdoor projects, and for good reason. A well-designed deck can add significant value to a property, provide a seamless transition between indoors and outdoors, and create a perfect space for entertaining and relaxation. However, with so many options and regulations to consider, it can be overwhelming to decide where to start. This guide covers everything you need to know about building a deck in NZ in 2026, from consent rules and construction guides to costs and maintenance requirements. Whether you're a seasoned DIY enthusiast or a homeowner looking to hire a professional, this guide will help you navigate the decision points and create a beautiful and functional outdoor space.

Do you need building consent for a deck?

Under Schedule 1 of the Building Act 2004, decks that are less than 1.5m above ground level generally do not require building consent. However, there are some important exceptions to consider. If your deck is attached to a consented structure, such as a house, in a way that affects its weathertightness, you may need to obtain consent. Additionally, if your deck includes a significant structural element, such as a beam or a post, you may need to obtain consent. It's also important to check your district plan for any specific rules or regulations that may apply to your deck, such as setback rules or pool fencing requirements. If your deck is near a pool, you will need to ensure that it meets the requirements of the Fencing of Swimming Pools Act 1987, which includes a minimum balustrade height of 1.2m. To determine whether you need building consent, it's always best to call your local council's duty planner for free advice. Timber selection

When it comes to choosing the right timber for your deck, there are several options to consider. Here are a few popular choices: * Pine (treated): Treated pine is a popular choice for decking in NZ, and is available in a range of treatment levels, including H3.2 for above-ground decking and H4 for posts in ground. Kiln-dried pine is also available, which can help to reduce cupping and warping. The price of treated pine decking can range from $30 to $60 per linear metre, depending on the treatment level and the supplier. * Hardwood (kwila/merbau, vitex): Hardwood decking is a beautiful and durable option, but it can be more expensive than treated pine. Hardwood decking is dense and resistant to rot and insect damage, and can last for many years with proper maintenance. The price of hardwood decking can range from $60 to $120 per linear metre, depending on the species and the supplier. * Composite decking (Trex, Futurewood, EasyDek): Composite decking is a low-maintenance option that is made from a combination of materials, such as wood and plastic. Composite decking is resistant to rot and insect damage, and can last for many years with minimal maintenance. The price of composite decking can range from $100 to $200 per square metre, depending on the brand and the supplier.

Design considerations

When designing your deck, there are several important considerations to keep in mind. Here are a few key things to think about: * Board spacing: The spacing between your decking boards will depend on the type of decking you choose. For timber decking, a spacing of 5mm is typical, while composite decking may have a different spacing requirement depending on the manufacturer's specifications. * Joist spacing: The spacing between your joists will depend on the size of your decking boards and the load that your deck will be carrying. A typical joist spacing for 90mm decking boards is 450mm centres. * Post sizing: The size of your posts will depend on the height and load of your deck. A minimum post size of 90x90mm is typical for most decks. * Ledger attachment: The attachment of your ledger board to your house is critical for ensuring the weathertightness of your deck. Make sure to use the correct type and number of fixings to secure your ledger board. * Drainage falls: The drainage falls of your deck are important for ensuring that water runs away from your house and does not accumulate on your deck. A minimum fall of 1:100 is typical for most decks.

Step-by-step construction overview

Here is a step-by-step overview of the construction process for a deck: 1. Layout and posts: The first step in building your deck is to lay out the position of your posts and mark their location on the ground. Dig holes for your posts and set them in concrete. 2. Bearer installation: Once your posts are in place, you can install your bearers. These are the horizontal beams that will support your joists. 3. Joist frame: With your bearers in place, you can install your joist frame. This is the framework that will support your decking boards. 4. Decking boards: Once your joist frame is in place, you can install your decking boards. Make sure to space them correctly and secure them to your joists with the correct type and number of fixings. 5. Balustrades: If your deck is more than 1m above the ground, you will need to install balustrades to ensure safety. You can either build your own balustrades or purchase a kit system. 6. Finishing: The final step in building your deck is to finish it with oil or paint. This will help to protect your decking boards and enhance their appearance.

Balustrade requirements

If your deck is more than 1m above the ground, you will need to install balustrades to ensure safety. The minimum height of your balustrades will depend on the height of your deck, but a minimum height of 1m is typical. You will also need to ensure that the openings in your balustrades are no more than 100mm to prevent children from falling through. You can either build your own balustrades or purchase a kit system.

Cost breakdown

The cost of building a deck in NZ can vary widely, depending on the size and complexity of the project. Here are some estimated costs for a 20m² deck: * Pine deck (materials only): $3,000-$6,000 * Pine deck (professionally built): $8,000-$15,000 * Composite deck (materials only): $5,000-$9,000 * Composite deck (professionally built): $12,000-$22,000 It's worth noting that these costs can vary depending on the region, with Auckland typically being around 20% more expensive than other parts of the country.

Deck maintenance

The maintenance requirements for your deck will depend on the type of decking you choose. Here are some general guidelines: * Pine: Pine decking will need to be re-oiled or re-painted every 2-3 years to maintain its appearance and protect it from the elements. * Hardwood: Hardwood decking will need to be oiled annually for the first 2 years, and then as needed after that. * Composite: Composite decking is low-maintenance and will only need to be washed annually to keep it clean.
