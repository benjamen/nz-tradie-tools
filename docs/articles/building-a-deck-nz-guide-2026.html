Building a Deck NZ 2026 — Costs, Consent Rules and DIY Guide

Published 25 June 2026 · NZ Tradie Tools
deckdeckingDIYhomeownerbuilding consentNZ2026

Introduction

Building a deck is one of New Zealand's most popular outdoor projects, and for good reason. A well-designed deck can add significant value to a property, provide a comfortable outdoor living space, and enhance the overall aesthetic of a home. However, with so many options and considerations, it can be overwhelming to decide where to start. This guide covers the essential aspects of building a deck in NZ, including consent rules, timber selection, design considerations, and construction costs. Whether you're a seasoned DIY enthusiast or a homeowner looking to hire a professional, this guide will help you navigate the decision points and create a beautiful and functional outdoor space.

Do you need building consent for a deck?

Under Schedule 1 of the Building Act 2004, decks that are less than 1.5m above ground level generally do not require building consent. However, there are some important exceptions to consider. If your deck is attached to a consented structure, such as a house, in a way that affects its weathertightness, you may need to obtain consent. Additionally, if your deck includes a significant structural element, such as a beam or a post, you may need to consult with a building professional to determine if consent is required. It's also important to check your district plan, as some councils have specific setback rules or requirements for pool fencing if your deck is near a pool. Balustrade height rules also apply, with a minimum height of 1m required where the fall exceeds 1m. If you're unsure about any of these requirements, it's always best to call your local council's duty planner for free advice. For more information on building work that doesn't require consent, visit our guide.

Timber selection

Choosing the right timber for your deck is a critical decision, as it will affect the appearance, durability, and maintenance requirements of your deck. Here are some popular options to consider:

  • Pine (treated): H3.2 treated pine is suitable for above-ground decking, while H4 treated pine is required for posts that will be in contact with the ground. Kiln-dried pine is a good option for decking, as it is less prone to cupping and warping. Price: $30–$60 per linear metre.
  • Hardwood (kwila/merbau, vitex): Hardwood decking offers a beautiful finish and is more dense than pine, making it more durable. However, it is also more expensive. Price: $60–$120 per linear metre.
  • Composite decking (Trex, Futurewood, EasyDek): Composite decking is a low-maintenance option that is resistant to splintering and rot. It also comes with a 25-year warranty. Price: $100–$200 per square metre.

Design considerations

When designing your deck, there are several key considerations to keep in mind. These include:

  • Board spacing: For timber decking, a spacing of 5mm is typical. For composite decking, follow the manufacturer's specifications.
  • Joist spacing: A spacing of 450mm centres is typical for 90mm decking.
  • Post sizing: A minimum post size of 90×90 is recommended.
  • Ledger attachment: The ledger must be attached to the house in a way that ensures weathertightness.
  • Drainage falls: A minimum fall of 1:100 is required to ensure that water drains away from the house.

Step-by-step construction overview

Building a deck requires careful planning and execution. Here is a step-by-step overview of the construction process:

  1. Layout and posts: Mark out the deck area and dig holes for the posts. Concrete the posts in place.
  2. Bearer installation: Install the bearers, which will support the joists.
  3. Joist frame: Install the joist frame, which will support the decking boards.
  4. Decking boards: Install the decking boards, starting from the outside and working your way in.
  5. Balustrades: If required, install the balustrades.
  6. Finishing: Oil or paint the deck to protect it from the elements.

Balustrade requirements

If your deck is more than 1m above the adjacent ground level, you will need to install balustrades. The minimum height of the balustrade is 1m, and openings must not exceed 100mm to ensure child safety. You can either build your own balustrades or purchase a kit system.

Cost breakdown

The cost of building a deck can vary widely, depending on the materials and labour required. Here are some estimated costs for a 20m² deck:

  • Pine deck (materials only): $3,000–$6,000
  • Pine deck (professionally built): $8,000–$15,000
  • Composite deck (materials only): $5,000–$9,000
  • Composite deck (professionally built): $12,000–$22,000
  • Regional variation: Auckland has a 20% premium on these prices.

Deck maintenance

To keep your deck looking its best, regular maintenance is required. Here are some tips:

  • Pine: Re-oil or paint every 2–3 years.
  • Hardwood: Oil annually for the first 2 years, then as needed.
  • Composite: Wash annually, and no oiling is required.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about building a deck in NZ:

  1. How close to the boundary can I build a deck in NZ?: This will depend on your local council's rules and regulations. It's best to check with them before starting your project.
  2. Does a deck add value to a NZ home?: Yes, a well-designed and well-built deck can add significant value to a property.
  3. Can I build a deck myself?: If you have the necessary skills and experience, yes. However, if you're unsure about any aspect of the project, it's best to consult with a professional.
  4. What's the best decking material for NZ?: This will depend on your budget, lifestyle, and personal preferences. Consider factors such as durability, maintenance, and aesthetics when making your decision.
  5. Do I need a balustrade on my deck?: If your deck is more than 1m above the adjacent ground level, yes.

Conclusion

Building a deck can be a rewarding and enjoyable project, but it requires careful planning and execution. By following the guidelines outlined in this article, you can create a beautiful and functional outdoor space that will add value to your property.
