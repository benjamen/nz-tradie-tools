## Competitor Overview

The NZ tradie tools space splits into two lanes: broad financial platforms (Calculate.co.nz with 507 calculators for all NZ consumers) and niche tradie SaaS products with content marketing (Automate The Trades for painters). No competitor is a tradie specialist with free tools + articles + templates covering all 47 trades. That's our positioning.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Trade rates × city data is our moat** — 47 trades × 20 cities = 940 data points. Calculate doesn't have this. Automate has it for painting only. This is our strongest differentiator.

2. **Templates are unique** — 29 free downloadable templates (quote, invoice, SWMS) have no equivalent at either competitor. High-intent, linkable content.

3. **Calculate.co.nz outguns us on breadth and DA** — We cannot win "NZ calculator" head terms. We must win on "tradie [term]" queries where our specialisation creates topical authority.

4. **Automate The Trades is a narrow but well-executed niche player** — Monitor if they expand trades. Their honest comparison content format is worth studying.

5. **Software comparison content is an opportunity** — Neither us nor Calculate.co.nz has a neutral tradie software comparison hub. ATT only compares in the context of promoting their own product.

## Gaps and Opportunities

- **Tradie software comparison hub** — Neutral Tradify vs Fergus vs Simpro vs NextMinute comparisons with no product to push. High-intent, linkable.
- **Industry-specific sub-hubs** — "For builders", "for plumbers", "for electricians" landing pages that aggregate all our tools + articles for that trade.
- **"How much does [trade] cost NZ?" consumer content** — We have the data; consumer-facing cost guides get links from media and homeowner sites.
- **Calculator SEO refinement** — Ensure all 60 calculator pages target "tradie [calculator name] NZ" not generic terms.
- **Named expert attribution** — Both competitors lack strong author signals. Adding a "written by a NZ trade professional" byline would differentiate.
